You should wash your dog bed every 1-2 weeks for most dogs, though dogs with allergies, skin conditions, or those who spend significant time outdoors may benefit from weekly washing. The frequency depends on your dog's health, activity level, and the bed's construction.

The Short Answer

Most dog owners should aim to wash their dog's bed every 7-14 days to maintain hygiene and eliminate odors, bacteria, and dust mites. Dogs that are older, have skin allergies, or are frequently outdoors should have their beds washed weekly. Even with regular washing, spot-cleaning between washes can help extend the time between full washes and keep the bed fresher longer.

The Full Explanation

The ideal washing frequency for a dog bed depends on several factors that directly impact how quickly the bed accumulates dirt, bacteria, and allergens. Understanding these factors helps you establish a routine that keeps your dog comfortable and healthy while maximizing the lifespan of the bed itself.

Standard Washing Schedule (Every 1-2 Weeks)

For most healthy dogs living primarily indoors, washing the dog bed every 1-2 weeks strikes the right balance between cleanliness and practicality. This frequency removes accumulated dust, dander, saliva, and urine traces that naturally build up from daily use. Weekly washing is recommended during warmer months when bacteria and odors develop faster due to increased moisture and heat.

Weekly Washing (Every 7 Days)

Dogs with specific health needs require more frequent bed washing. If your dog has skin allergies, dermatitis, or other skin conditions, weekly washing prevents bacteria and yeast from accumulating and exacerbating these issues. Additionally, older dogs or those with incontinence, puppies still being housetrained, or dogs that spend significant time outdoors should have their beds washed weekly to maintain hygiene standards.

Spot-Cleaning Between Full Washes

To extend the time between full washes and maintain freshness, spot-clean visible stains, wet areas, or smelly spots as they occur. Use a enzymatic cleaner designed for pet messes to break down organic compounds that cause odors. This approach can reduce the need for full washes while keeping the bed presentable and hygienic.

Factors That Increase Washing Frequency

  • Dogs with allergies or skin conditions require weekly washing
  • Senior dogs or those with incontinence need weekly washes
  • Dogs that swim, play in mud, or spend time outdoors need more frequent washing
  • Multiple dogs sharing one bed or households with immunocompromised individuals benefit from weekly washing
  • Humid climates promote faster bacterial growth, requiring more frequent washes

How to Wash a Dog Bed

Most dog beds can be machine washed on gentle cycles with cold or warm water and pet-safe detergent. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions first, as some beds have memory foam or special materials requiring hand-washing. Remove the cover if possible, wash separately from the filling, and avoid fabric softeners that can irritate sensitive skin. Air-dry completely before returning to your dog to prevent mold and mildew growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you wash a dog bed?

Most dog beds should be washed every 1-2 weeks, depending on how dirty they get and whether your dog has any skin issues. If your dog spends a lot of time outdoors or has allergies, washing weekly is recommended. Beds with removable covers can be washed more frequently, while non-removable covers should be spot-cleaned between full washes.

Can you put a dog bed in the washing machine?

It depends on the dog bed's construction and care label. Most beds with removable, machine-washable covers can go in the washer on a gentle or delicate cycle with cold water. For non-removable covers or orthopedic beds, check the manufacturer's instructions first, as some require hand washing or dry cleaning only.

What temperature should you wash a dog bed?

Use cold or warm water (not hot) to wash dog beds, as hot water can damage the fabric and reduce the bed's lifespan. Cold water is best for preventing color fading and is effective at removing most dirt and odors when paired with a pet-safe detergent.

How do you dry a dog bed after washing?

Air drying is the safest option for most dog beds—hang dry or lay flat in the sun, which naturally kills odor-causing bacteria. If using a dryer, use a low heat setting to avoid damaging the fabric and filling. Never use high heat, as it can warp orthopedic memory foam or elastic materials.
