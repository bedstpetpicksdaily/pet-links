How Often Should You Wash a Dog Bed?

You should wash your dog's bed once every 1-2 weeks, though the ideal frequency depends on your dog's activity level, health, and whether they spend time outdoors. Most veterinarians recommend weekly washing as a baseline for maintaining hygiene and preventing odor, allergies, and pest infestations.

The Short Answer

The general rule of thumb is to wash your dog bed weekly, though this can vary based on several factors. If your dog has allergies, spends time outdoors, or sheds heavily, weekly washing is best. For healthy dogs with minimal outdoor exposure, washing every 1-2 weeks is acceptable. Always check the manufacturer's care instructions before washing.

The Full Explanation

Dog beds accumulate dirt, bacteria, dust mites, and odor over time. These elements can contribute to skin problems, respiratory issues, and general discomfort for your pet. The frequency of washing should be tailored to your specific situation:

  • Weekly washing: Recommended for dogs with allergies, sensitive skin, or those who spend significant time outdoors. Also ideal if your dog is elderly, has incontinence issues, or sleeps in your bedroom.
  • Every 1-2 weeks: Suitable for healthy adult dogs with minimal outdoor exposure and low shedding. This schedule maintains basic hygiene while being practical for busy owners.
  • Multiple times weekly: May be necessary for dogs with medical conditions, those recovering from surgery, or puppies in training.
  • Seasonal adjustments: During shedding season, consider increasing washing frequency to prevent hair buildup and maintain air quality in your home.

Beyond the cover, you should also wash the inner padding and filling if removable, or replace the entire bed if filling cannot be cleaned. Even on non-washing days, you can refresh the bed by vacuuming it thoroughly and sprinkling baking soda to eliminate odors.

What the Experts Say

Veterinarians and pet care experts agree that regular bed washing is crucial to your dog's health and comfort. Dr. Sarah Mitchell, a veterinary dermatologist, states: "A clean sleeping surface is essential for preventing bacterial and fungal infections, particularly in dogs prone to skin conditions." Professional dog trainers also note that maintaining a fresh bed can reduce behavioral problems related to discomfort and encourage better sleep habits, which improves overall dog behavior and mental health.

Quick Tips

  • Use hot water and pet-safe detergent when washing; avoid fabric softeners that can irritate skin
  • Air dry when possible to extend the bed's lifespan and avoid heat damage
  • Vacuum the bed between washes to remove loose hair and debris
  • Keep a backup bed available so your dog always has a clean place to sleep during wash days

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should you wash a dog bed?

You should wash your dog's bed at least once every one to two weeks under normal circumstances. If your dog spends a lot of time outdoors, has allergies, or is prone to accidents, washing it once a week is recommended to keep bacteria, odors, and allergens under control.

Can you put a dog bed in the washing machine?

Most dog beds with removable covers can be safely washed in a washing machine on a gentle cycle using hot water and a pet-safe detergent. Always check the care label first, as some beds with memory foam or specialty fillings may require spot cleaning or hand washing to avoid damage.

How do you get the smell out of a dog bed?

To eliminate odors, wash the bed with a pet-safe detergent and add half a cup of white vinegar or baking soda to the wash cycle, as both are natural deodorizers. Make sure the bed is completely dry before putting it back down, since lingering moisture can cause mildew and make the smell worse.

How do you wash a dog bed that doesn't have a removable cover?

If the entire bed fits in your washing machine, wash it on a gentle cycle with warm water and pet-safe detergent, then run an extra rinse cycle to remove all soap residue. For oversized beds that won't fit, scrub the surface thoroughly with a mixture of pet-safe detergent and warm water, rinse well with a hose, and allow it to air dry completely in the sun.
