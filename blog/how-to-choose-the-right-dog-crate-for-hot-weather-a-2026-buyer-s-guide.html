Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is too hot for a dog crate?

Generally, temperatures above 85°F (29°C) inside a crate become dangerous for dogs. Well-ventilated AECOJOY crates with proper airflow can maintain cooler internal temperatures, but always monitor conditions and provide additional cooling aids when needed.

Can I use a plastic crate in hot weather?

Standard plastic crates are not ideal for hot weather as they trap heat and limit airflow. AECOJOY's ventilated designs with mesh panels are far superior alternatives that prevent dangerous heat accumulation while maintaining security.

How do I keep a dog crate cool without air conditioning?

Position the crate in a shaded area, use AECOJOY cooling pads or gel inserts, provide adequate water nearby, ensure excellent ventilation with mesh panels, use reflective covers, and maintain consistent airflow with fans if possible.

What size crate should I choose for hot weather?

Your dog should be able to stand, turn around, and stretch comfortably. AECOJOY offers multiple sizes designed to balance comfort with security—larger crates promote better airflow, but oversized ones reduce the cozy den feeling dogs prefer.

Are AECOJOY crates suitable for outdoor use?

Yes, AECOJOY specializes in outdoor pet supplies and their crates are designed for both indoor and outdoor use. Their durable construction and weather-resistant materials make them excellent choices for sunny patios and covered outdoor spaces.
