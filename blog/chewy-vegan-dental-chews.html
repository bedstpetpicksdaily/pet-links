Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Leaving unsupervised chewing sessions too long: Even durable chews can break apart unexpectedly, so monitor your dog during the first few uses to understand their chewing style and remove the product when it becomes a choking hazard.
  • Choosing the wrong size for your dog: A chew that's too small defeats the purpose and poses a swallowing risk, while one that's too large might frustrate your dog into abandoning it. Match the size to your dog's breed and jaw strength.
  • Expecting immediate results: Dental chews work gradually through mechanical action and beneficial ingredients—don't assume one chew will transform your dog's breath or plaque buildup overnight. Consistency over weeks and months is what creates noticeable improvements.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Use as part of a routine, not a replacement: Pair these chews with regular tooth brushing and annual vet checkups. The combination of mechanical chewing, enzymatic ingredients, and professional care delivers the best oral health outcomes.
  • Introduce gradually to avoid digestive upset: If your dog isn't accustomed to plant-based chews, start by offering 15-20 minutes of chewing time for the first few days, then increase duration as their digestive system adjusts.
  • Store in a cool, dry place and check for freshness: Keep chews sealed and protected from moisture to maintain their texture and potency. Just like any natural product, they have a shelf life—check expiration dates and discard any that show signs of mold or unusual odor.
