Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is Embark's breed identification compared to other DNA tests?

Embark's 350+ breed database is among the most comprehensive available. In side-by-side comparisons with Wisdom Panel and other competitors, Embark identifies rare or regional breeds with better precision. That said, mixed breeds with unclear ancestry (especially rescues) will always show some uncertainty—that's genomics, not a flaw. The percentages are accurate within the limits of reference databases.

What's the difference between a carrier status and a risk for a genetic condition?

Critical distinction: a carrier has one copy of a recessive gene but won't develop the disease. A dog with risk factors for conditions like dilated cardiomyopathy might develop symptoms later. Embark flags both but requires your vet's expertise to assess which results matter for your individual dog. A carrier of collie eye anomaly in a Labrador retriever, for instance, has minimal clinical relevance.

Can I use Embark results to breed my dog responsibly?

Embark is an excellent starting point for breeding decisions—it identifies carriers of problematic recessive genes and screens for conditions that could be passed to offspring. However, responsible breeding also requires health testing your dog's potential mate, consulting breed club guidelines, and often involving a veterinary geneticist. Embark is one tool in a larger toolkit, not the complete solution for breeding ethics.
