Why Summer Nutrition Matters for Dogs

During summer, your dog's metabolic needs change. Heat stress can suppress appetite, reduce energy levels, and impact digestion. Feeding nutrient-dense, high-quality proteins helps your dog maintain muscle mass and energy without putting extra stress on their digestive system. Raw dog food is particularly effective during warmer months because it's easier to digest and contains natural moisture that supports hydration—something critical when temperatures rise.

Zeal Pet Food specializes in premium raw and freeze-dried formulations with no grains, fillers, or artificial ingredients. Their natural pet food range is designed to be gentle on digestion while providing complete nutrition, making it an excellent choice for summer feeding strategies.

Raw Food Benefits for Summer Cooling

Raw feeding offers several advantages during hot weather. Raw dog food contains higher moisture content compared to kibble, naturally supporting hydration without requiring excessive water intake. Additionally, raw nutrition requires less digestive effort, reducing metabolic heat generation—the body heat produced during digestion. This means your dog's system works more efficiently without generating extra internal heat.

Zeal Pet Food's raw range includes complete meals formulated with high-quality proteins, organ meats, and essential nutrients. Their freeze-dried raw option offers the same benefits with added convenience—no thawing required. These grain-free formulations support lean muscle development and sustained energy, keeping your dog active and cool throughout summer.

Hydration-Supporting Foods and Feeding Tips

Incorporate moisture-rich foods into your dog's diet. Raw meat naturally contains 60-70% water, significantly more than dry kibble. If your dog isn't ready for full raw conversion, consider mixing Zeal Pet Food freeze-dried products with fresh water to create a nutrient-rich, hydrating meal. You can also add low-sodium bone broth or plain water to meals.

Timing matters too—feed larger meals during cooler morning and evening hours, and offer smaller portions during peak heat. Always ensure fresh, cool water is available throughout the day. Zeal's grain-free pet nutrition is formulated to support optimal digestion efficiency, meaning your dog absorbs more nutrients with less digestive strain during summer months.

Transitioning to Raw Food Safely

If you're considering switching to Zeal Pet Food's raw dog food line, do so gradually over 7-10 days to allow your dog's digestive system to adjust. Start by replacing 25% of their current food with raw, then increase by 25% every few days until reaching 100%. This transition supports healthy digestion and prevents upset stomachs during the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe during hot weather?

Yes, raw dog food is safe when handled properly. Keep it refrigerated or frozen until feeding time, use it within 2-3 days of thawing, and maintain proper food safety practices. Zeal Pet Food provides detailed handling guidelines with every order.

How much water should my dog drink in summer?

Dogs typically need 0.5-1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily. In summer, this may increase significantly. Raw food like Zeal Pet Food helps supplement hydration through moisture content, but always provide fresh water access throughout the day.

Can I mix raw food with kibble?

Yes, you can mix Zeal Pet Food's raw or freeze-dried products with kibble, though some experts recommend feeding them separately to optimize digestion. Start with small portions mixed together and monitor your dog's digestive response.

What's the difference between raw and freeze-dried pet food?

Raw food retains full moisture content and requires refrigeration, while freeze-dried food removes moisture for shelf stability and convenience. Both offer the nutritional benefits of raw feeding. Zeal Pet Food offers both options for different lifestyle needs.

Are grain-free diets better for summer feeding?

Grain-free diets like Zeal Pet Food formulations are easier to digest and don't generate excess heat during digestion, making them beneficial for summer. They also typically contain higher protein quality, supporting sustained energy in warm weather.
