Why This Matters

Heartworm is transmitted through mosquito bites and can develop silently in your dog's body for months before symptoms appear. Once diagnosed, treatment is costly, invasive, and stressful for your pet. Prevention is far simpler and more affordable than treatment, making it one of the most important health investments you'll make as a pet owner.

Before purchasing heartworm prevention products, verify the prescription requirement and ensure the medication is appropriate for your pet's specific weight range and age, as dosages vary significantly between dogs and cats and incorrect dosing can reduce effectiveness.

Editor's Tip

During Heartworm Awareness Month, veterinarians emphasize testing and prevention because the disease is entirely preventable with proper medication and lifestyle choices.

What Pet Owners Should Know

  • Heartworm is present in all 50 U.S. states, with higher concentrations in warmer regions
  • Dogs of any age can contract heartworm, but puppies and senior dogs face higher risks
  • Prevention medications work best when given year-round or during mosquito season as recommended by your vet
  • Annual testing is essential, even for dogs on preventative medication
  • Mosquito control around your home reduces transmission risk significantly
