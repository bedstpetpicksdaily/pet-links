Frequently Asked Questions

What size playpen do I need for my puppy?

Choose a playpen that allows your puppy to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably without feeling cramped. A good rule of thumb is to select a size based on your puppy's expected adult weight—larger breeds need 48+ square feet, while smaller breeds do well with 24-36 square feet. You can always add or remove panels as your puppy grows.

How tall should a puppy playpen be to prevent escapes?

For most puppies, a playpen height of 24-36 inches is sufficient to prevent jumping out and escaping. However, if you have a breed known for jumping (like Jack Russell Terriers) or an athletic puppy, opt for 36+ inches to be safe. Always supervise your puppy during playtime regardless of pen height.

Can I use a playpen to housetrain my puppy?

Yes, a playpen is an excellent housetraining tool when used correctly with a designated potty area inside or placed near a door for frequent outdoor breaks. Puppies naturally avoid soiling their sleeping area, so keep one section for a bed and toys while designating another corner as the bathroom spot. Combine playpen use with a consistent potty schedule for best results.

What's the best playpen material for puppies that chew and pee?

Metal or heavy-duty plastic playpens are more durable and easier to clean than fabric options when dealing with puppy accidents and chewing. Avoid fabric-based pens unless they have waterproof liners, as they absorb urine and develop odors quickly. Ensure whatever material you choose is non-toxic and free of sharp edges.
