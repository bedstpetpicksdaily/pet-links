Understanding Heat Stress in Dogs

Dogs don't regulate body temperature the way humans do. They rely primarily on panting and limited sweating through their paw pads to cool down. During summer months, this natural cooling mechanism often isn't enough, especially during peak afternoon hours. Dogs are at risk of heat exhaustion and heat stroke when temperatures soar, which can lead to serious health complications.

Signs of heat stress in dogs include excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, vomiting, and in severe cases, collapse. Senior dogs, puppies, and breeds with short snouts (like Bulldogs and Pugs) are particularly vulnerable. This is why investing in proper cooling solutions—such as those offered by AECOJOY—isn't just about comfort; it's about your pet's safety and wellbeing.
