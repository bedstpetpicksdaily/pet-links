Frequently Asked Questions

Can this feeder work for both cats and small dogs?

Yes, but with caveats. The 1/4 cup portion setting works well for cats (most need 1/4–1/2 cup per meal), but larger dogs needing 1–2 cups per feeding will cycle through meals faster. If you have both a cat and a dog, you'd need separate feeders unless they eat the same amount, which is rare.

How often do you need to clean this feeder?

Weekly cleaning is recommended, especially the bowl. The stainless steel bowl is dishwasher-safe, but the hopper should be hand-cleaned to prevent kibble dust and moisture from damaging the motor. If you're using wet food or raw diets, plan on daily cleanup—this is strictly for dry kibble.

What happens if the power goes out?

The digital timer resets. If your area experiences frequent outages, this becomes a real problem because you'll lose your feeding schedule and need to reprogram it every time. Battery backup models exist from other brands (usually $10-$20 more), so ask yourself if that's worth the extra cost in your area.

Is this better than cheaper $20-$30 automatic feeders?

Depends on your needs. Cheaper models often have smaller hoppers (1-2 meals vs. 5), less precise portions, and plastic bowls that retain odors. However, if you only need one automatic feeding per day and your pet isn't picky, a cheaper model covers 80% of the functionality at 40% of the cost. The PetSafe wins for reliability and durability—you won't replace it in two years.
