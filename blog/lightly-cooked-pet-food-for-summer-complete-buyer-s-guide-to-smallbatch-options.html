Frequently Asked Questions

Is lightly cooked pet food better than raw for summer?

Lightly cooked food offers significant advantages for summer feeding. It eliminates harmful bacteria that multiply quickly in heat while preserving more nutrients than fully cooked kibble. Smallbatch Pets' gentle cooking process maintains digestibility and nutritional value without the food safety concerns of handling raw meals in warm climates.

How should I store Smallbatch lightly cooked meals in summer?

Keep Smallbatch refrigerated meals at consistent temperatures between 35-40°F. Their compact packaging fits standard refrigerators easily. For outdoor activities, transport in insulated coolers with ice packs. Once opened, consume within 3-4 days for optimal freshness and safety.

Can I transition my pet to Smallbatch lightly cooked food quickly?

A gradual 7-10 day transition is recommended to prevent digestive upset. Start by mixing 25% Smallbatch with your pet's current food, gradually increasing the ratio. Smallbatch's highly digestible formulation typically results in smooth transitions, though some pets may require extended adjustment periods.

What protein varieties does Smallbatch offer in lightly cooked formulas?

Smallbatch Pets rotates premium protein options including grass-fed beef, free-range chicken, heritage turkey, and wild-caught fish. This variety prevents dietary boredom while ensuring nutritional balance and allowing you to identify your pet's preferred protein sources.

Are Smallbatch lightly cooked meals suitable for pets with allergies?

Smallbatch's limited ingredient lightly cooked recipes work well for allergic pets. Their transparency regarding sourcing and preparation allows you to identify potential allergens easily. However, consult your veterinarian before switching diets, especially for pets with diagnosed food sensitivities.
