Frequently Asked Questions

Will my cat actually figure out how to use it, or will it just sit there?

Most cats intuitively understand the sliding mechanism within the first few tries, especially if you demonstrate by moving a cover slightly to reveal a treat. Food-motivated cats catch on immediately. The learning process is actually part of the enrichment—the problem-solving keeps their brain engaged. If your cat has never interacted with puzzle feeders, expect 2-3 days of trial and error before they get consistent use.

How does this compare to other puzzle feeders like Nina Ottosson or cheaper Amazon alternatives?

Cheerble typically costs less than Nina Ottosson ($20-35 range vs. $30-50) while offering similar durability and design. Cheaper generic puzzle boards ($10-15) often have loose parts, poor sliding mechanisms, or materials that crack within weeks. The Cheerble hits the sweet spot between affordable and reliable—you're not overpaying for a brand name, but you're not gambling on a flimsy product either. If durability matters to you, it's worth the extra $5-10 over the cheapest options.

Can I use this with wet food, or just dry kibble and treats?

Stick with dry kibble, treats, and puzzle toys. Wet food or canned meals will create moisture buildup in the compartments, leading to mold growth and making the board impossible to clean thoroughly. If you want to use wet food for enrichment, pair the puzzle board with a separate slow feeder. The board is designed for dry applications, and respecting that extends its lifespan significantly.
