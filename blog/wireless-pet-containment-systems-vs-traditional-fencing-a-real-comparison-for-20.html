Frequently Asked Questions

How does wireless containment compare to underground fencing in terms of reliability?

Underground fencing is more reliable in obstructed yards because the wire boundary is unaffected by weather, vegetation, or metal structures. Wireless systems depend on radio signal propagation, which degrades significantly near dense trees, metal buildings, or hills. In open, flat yards, wireless reliability is comparable; in complex terrain, buried fences are substantially more dependable.

Is the receiver collar safe for dogs with medical conditions or anxiety?

Both wireless and buried systems use mild corrections that are generally safe, but dogs with severe anxiety or certain medical conditions may respond negatively to the correction stimulus. If your dog has a documented anxiety disorder, consult your veterinarian before using any correction-based containment system. Some vets recommend desensitization training first.

Does wireless containment actually work in apartments or rental properties?

Yes—this is wireless systems' strongest use case. Wireless containment requires zero property modification, making it the only practical option for renters and apartment dwellers with patios or small yards. Traditional fencing is impossible in rentals; wireless systems are specifically designed for this scenario.

What's the realistic boundary range for wireless systems, and does it stay 90 feet in all directions?

No. Advertised ranges like 90 feet assume open, flat, obstacle-free terrain. In real yards, effective range typically shrinks to 25–40 feet depending on vegetation, terrain, and structures. Before purchasing, physically test signal propagation in your yard or ask the seller for a return window to verify actual coverage matches your property needs.

How much does wireless containment cost compared to traditional fencing?

Wireless systems (like PetSafe Pawz Away) typically cost $200–$500 upfront. Traditional buried fencing costs $1,500–$5,000+ including installation. Budget wireless alternatives cost $100–$300 but carry higher failure rates. For renters, wireless is far cheaper; for homeowners, buried fencing spreads its higher upfront cost across 15+ years of maintenance-free operation.

Will this work for multiple dogs?

Yes, but you'll need separate receiver collars for each dog, which adds cost. Both wireless and buried systems support multiple collars, though wireless systems work best when all dogs have similar prey drive and boundary-training maturity. Dogs with very different temperaments may require individual training before introducing them to the shared boundary.
