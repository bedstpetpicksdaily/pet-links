The Processing Difference: Heat vs. Cold-Pressing

The fundamental difference between kibble and cold-pressed dog food comes down to how they're made. Traditional kibble undergoes extrusion at temperatures exceeding 300°F, which dramatically reduces enzyme activity and nutrient bioavailability. This high-heat process can denature proteins and destroy heat-sensitive vitamins that your dog needs to thrive.

Cold-pressed dog food, by contrast, uses gentle pressure at low temperatures to bind ingredients together. This process maintains the nutritional profile of raw whole foods—proteins stay intact, enzymes remain active, and natural vitamins survive to nourish your pet. Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed formulas leverage this superior technology, ensuring every bite delivers maximum nutritional value without fillers or artificial preservatives.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much cold-pressed dog food should I feed my dog?

Cold-pressed food is more nutrient-dense than kibble, so most dogs require 20-30% less volume. Nextrition Pet provides detailed feeding guidelines based on your dog's weight and activity level. Start with their recommended amount and adjust based on your dog's body condition.

Can I mix cold-pressed food with kibble?

Yes, you can transition gradually by mixing cold-pressed food with kibble over 7-10 days. This helps your dog's digestive system adjust. However, for maximum benefits, many vets recommend eventually transitioning to cold-pressed as your dog's primary food source.
