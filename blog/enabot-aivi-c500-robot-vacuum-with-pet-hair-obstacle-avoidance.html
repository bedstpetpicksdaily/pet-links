Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Obstacle avoidance actually works: The dual RGB cameras and LIDAR navigation genuinely stopped it from tangling in cords, bumping furniture, or getting stuck under the couch during my testing. It mapped my living room intelligently and created actual pathways rather than random bumbling.
  • ✓ Pet hair performance on hard floors is impressive: On my kitchen tile, the vacuum sucked up dog hair clumps in a single pass. The brush didn't clog during the month of testing, even after my larger dog's spring shedding episodes.
  • ✓ App control is genuinely useful: Setting schedules for weekday mornings before we leave meant coming home to a cleaner floor without thinking about it. Real-time room mapping through the app let me see exactly which zones it cleaned.
  • ✓ Quiet operation won't stress noise-sensitive pets: At around 60dB, my cat didn't hide in the closet like she does with my upright vacuum. Dogs barely reacted after the first run.
Cons
  • ✗ Carpet performance is mediocre: On thick pile rugs, the suction noticeably weakens. I watched it struggle with embedded pet hair on my bedroom carpet in ways it didn't on hard floors—you'll still need a stick or upright for carpeted areas.
  • ✗ Small dustbin fills quickly with heavy shedding: During peak shedding days, I emptied the bin every two runs. For homes with multiple large dogs or cats, this becomes an annoying maintenance task rather than a truly hands-free solution.

Our Verdict

The Enabot Aivi C500 is a solid choice for pet owners who understand its specific strengths and limitations. It's best for hard-floored homes, apartments, or as a supplement to your existing vacuum—not a replacement. At its current price point, it delivers real value if obstacle avoidance and maintenance-free hard floor cleaning matter to you. If your home is 80% carpet or you have multiple heavy-shedding pets, skip it and invest in a dedicated pet hair vacuum instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need to empty the dustbin with pets?

With one dog, I emptied it every 2-3 days during normal shedding. With two dogs and peak summer shedding (like July), it's every 1-2 runs. Plan to empty it frequently if you have multiple pets. For reference, upright vacuum cleaners are still better if you want true set-it-and-forget-it functionality.

Will it get tangled in pet hair wrapped around the brush?

Not during my month of testing. The brush design resisted wrapping better than older robot vacuums I've owned. I checked it twice weekly and found minimal hair accumulation compared to models without similar brush engineering. However, if your pet sheds in long strands, monthly cleaning is still smart maintenance.

Does the pet hair detection feature actually work?

It identifies pet hair and increases suction automatically on detected carpeted areas. I noticed it adjusting power during runs, though the effect is subtle on hard floors where the real pet hair problem exists anyway. The feature works but isn't revolutionary—think of it as a helpful addition rather than a game-changer.
