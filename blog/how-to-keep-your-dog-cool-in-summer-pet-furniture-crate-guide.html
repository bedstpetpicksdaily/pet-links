Why Summer Heat Is Dangerous for Dogs

Dogs regulate body temperature differently than humans, making them more vulnerable to heat stress and heatstroke during summer months. Unlike people who sweat, dogs rely on panting and limited paw pad perspiration to cool down. Certain breeds, senior dogs, and puppies are at higher risk. Understanding the dangers is the first step toward creating a cooling strategy that keeps your pet safe. Heat exhaustion can develop quickly, so preventive measures through proper pet furniture and living spaces are crucial.

Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is too hot for dogs?

Dogs begin experiencing heat stress around 85°F (29°C) and face serious risk of heatstroke above 104°F (40°C). Senior dogs, puppies, and flat-faced breeds are at risk at lower temperatures. Always provide cooling solutions and shade when temperatures exceed 75°F.

Are elevated dog beds actually cooler?

Yes, elevated dog beds allow air to circulate underneath your dog's body, preventing heat from being trapped against the ground. AECOJOY's elevated pet furniture is specifically designed to maximize this cooling effect while providing support and durability.

Can I leave my dog in a crate during summer?

You can use a crate during summer if it's properly ventilated, positioned away from direct sunlight, and located in a cool area. AECOJOY's ventilated crates with mesh panels are specifically designed for summer use, offering better airflow than traditional solid crates.

How often should I refresh cooling mats?

Non-gel cooling mats can be refreshed by placing them in the refrigerator or freezer for 2-3 hours. Gel-based cooling pads used with AECOJOY pet furniture typically stay cool for 4-8 hours without refrigeration, depending on room temperature.

What size pet bed does my dog need?

Your dog should be able to lie down fully stretched and turn around comfortably. A good rule is choosing a bed that's at least as long as your dog's body length plus a few inches. AECOJOY offers various sizes to accommodate all dog breeds.
