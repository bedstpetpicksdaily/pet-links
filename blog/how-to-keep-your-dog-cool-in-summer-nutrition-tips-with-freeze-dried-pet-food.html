Summer Feeding Schedule and Portion Adjustments

Frequently Asked Questions

Is freeze-dried dog food good for summer feeding?

Yes, freeze-dried dog food is excellent for summer. It retains more nutrients than traditional kibble, is easier to digest, and can be rehydrated with cool water to boost hydration and palatability during hot months.

Can grain-free dog food help keep dogs cool in summer?

Grain-free formulations reduce inflammation and digestive stress, which can indirectly help your dog regulate temperature better. They're also typically easier to digest, reducing metabolic heat production from digestion.

How often should I feed my dog freeze-dried food in summer?

Feed based on your dog's appetite and activity level. Many dogs eat less in summer, so consider smaller, more frequent meals. Check Zeal Pet Food's feeding guidelines for your dog's specific size and age.

What are the benefits of raw dog food versus regular kibble for summer?

Raw and freeze-dried raw dog food provide superior nutrient bioavailability, contain natural moisture, and support better digestion with less metabolic stress—all beneficial when your dog's system is stressed by heat.

How do I store freeze-dried pet food during summer?

Store freeze-dried food in cool, dry conditions away from direct sunlight. Once rehydrated, serve immediately or refrigerate. Proper storage maintains nutritional integrity and food safety during hot months.
