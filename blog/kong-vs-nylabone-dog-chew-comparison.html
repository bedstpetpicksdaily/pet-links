Quick Verdict

Kong wins for versatility and durability, especially if you want a toy that does multiple jobs and lasts through aggressive chewers. Nylabone wins for pure chewing satisfaction and longer-lasting engagement, particularly if your dog loves extended solo chewing sessions without your involvement.

Kong Overview

Kong is the Swiss Army knife of dog toys. The iconic rubber toy is stuffed with treats, bounces unpredictably, and doubles as both a chew toy and interactive play device. Dogs engage with it actively—pushing, shaking, and extracting hidden treats—which means it stimulates their brain while they chew.

Pros:

  • Multi-purpose: Works as a chew toy, fetch toy, treat dispenser, and training tool
  • Highly interactive: Encourages active engagement rather than passive chewing
  • Durable rubber: Holds up well to aggressive chewers; rated by size (black Kongs for power chewers)
  • Machine washable: Easy to clean between uses
  • Variety of sizes: Fits dogs from tiny Chihuahuas to large breeds
  • Forgiving: If your dog destroys it, replacement cost is reasonable

Cons:

  • Requires refilling: Once treats are removed, engagement drops sharply
  • Can be destructive if misused: Some dogs obsessively shake them and create a mess
  • Doesn't satisfy pure chewers: Dogs who want to chew continuously will finish it quickly
  • Treat dependency: Kong loses appeal without peanut butter or treats inside

Who It's For:

Kong is perfect for owners who want an interactive toy that keeps dogs mentally stimulated and engaged for 15-30 minutes at a time. Great for puppies, anxious dogs, or owners who want to actively participate in playtime. Best for dogs who enjoy problem-solving and treat extraction.

Nylabone Dog Chew Overview

Nylabone is the dedicated chewer's toy. These nylon or rubber chews are designed purely for extended solo chewing sessions. Dogs can work on a single Nylabone for weeks or months, satisfying their natural chewing instinct without any interactive element or treats involved.

Pros:

  • Long-lasting engagement: A single chew can occupy a dog for months of daily use
  • Pure chewing satisfaction: Textured surface satisfies the primal urge to chew
  • No maintenance required: Just give it and walk away—no refilling or cleaning between uses
  • Affordable per use: Extremely cost-effective when you calculate months of use per toy
  • Gum health benefits: Textured chewing can help reduce plaque buildup (when used with softer varieties)
  • Wide flavor variety: Bacon, chicken, peanut butter, and more appeal to different dogs

Cons:

  • Potential swallowing hazard: Hard varieties can splinter; nylon pieces can cause blockages in some dogs
  • Not interactive: Zero mental stimulation—just passive chewing
  • Less versatile: Only a chew toy; can't be used for fetch, play, or training
  • Less appealing to some dogs: Dogs that prefer interactive play will ignore it
  • Supervision sometimes needed: Dogs may chew off larger chunks, especially with hard varieties

Who It's For:

Nylabone is ideal for owners who want a low-maintenance, long-lasting chew for dogs who are content with solo chewing. Perfect for dogs with moderate to strong chewing drives, or for owners looking for an all-day occupation that requires zero involvement. Best for adult dogs (not puppies) with a preference for independent entertainment.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Kong or Nylabone better for aggressive chewers?

For aggressive chewers, Kong's black rubber Extreme line is generally the better choice since it's made from ultra-durable natural rubber designed specifically for power chewers and is less likely to break into dangerous pieces. Nylabone does offer durable nylon chews for aggressive chewers, but some dogs can gnaw off small nylon shards, which raises safety concerns. Always supervise your dog and replace either product when it shows significant wear.

Can Nylabone or Kong chews be used to help with puppy teething?

Both brands offer puppy-specific products, but Kong's Classic puppy toy (made from softer pink or blue rubber) is often preferred for teething because it's gentler on sensitive gums and can be frozen with treats or paste to provide soothing relief. Nylabone's puppy chews are flavored nylon designed to redirect chewing behavior, though they are better suited for light chewing rather than soothing sore gums. Always choose a size-appropriate product based on your puppy's breed and weight.

Are Kongs or Nylabones safer for dogs?

Kong rubber chews are widely considered safer because natural rubber is digestible in small amounts and the toys are less likely to splinter, whereas Nylabone nylon chews can produce small shards if chewed aggressively, which could cause intestinal issues if swallowed. Both products are generally safe when used as directed and with proper supervision, but vets tend to recommend rubber-based chews over hard nylon for dogs prone to breaking things apart. Whichever you choose, always match the product's durability rating to your dog's chewing strength.

Can you put food or treats inside a Nylabone like you can with a Kong?

No, Nylabones are solid chews and are not designed to be stuffed with food, whereas Kong toys are hollow and specifically engineered to be filled with treats, peanut butter, kibble, or wet food to provide mental stimulation. This stuffability is one of Kong's biggest advantages, as it can keep dogs engaged for extended periods and is useful for crate training or reducing anxiety. If interactive feeding and enrichment are priorities, Kong is the clear winner in this category.
