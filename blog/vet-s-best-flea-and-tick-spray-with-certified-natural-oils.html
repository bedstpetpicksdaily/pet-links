Frequently Asked Questions

Does Vet's Best Flea and Tick Spray actually kill fleas, or just repel them?

It kills them on contact. The cedarwood and lemongrass oils work as insecticides, not just repellents, so fleas and ticks die when the spray hits them directly. You'll see dead parasites on your pet within a few hours. However, it doesn't create residual protection, meaning new fleas can jump on after the spray dries, which is why reapplication every 7 days matters.

Is this safe to use on cats, or only dogs?

It's formulated for both cats and dogs, but application matters with cats. They groom themselves obsessively, so you can't just spray and walk away. Apply it to areas they can't easily lick (back of neck, between shoulder blades) and keep them from grooming the treated area for 10-15 minutes. Many cat owners find it easier to apply with a cloth rather than spraying directly to avoid stress.

How does this compare to prescription flea treatments like Simparica or Advantage?

Prescription treatments offer stronger, longer-lasting protection (up to 30 days) and work systemically through the bloodstream. Vet's Best is topical and requires frequent reapplication, making it less convenient for prevention. However, it's gentler chemically, cheaper, and doesn't require vet appointments. Use prescription treatments as your primary prevention and this spray for breakthrough infestations or between doses if you prefer a natural-first approach.

Will this spray damage my pet's coat or skin?

No, natural oils actually tend to be gentler on skin than chemical-based sprays. However, some pets with sensitive skin or existing dermatitis might experience irritation. Test it on a small patch first. The spray does have a strong herbal smell that some pets dislike, but it doesn't linger on furniture or clothes like chemical sprays do.

Does summer humidity affect how well this spray works?

Yes, slightly. July humidity means the spray takes longer to dry on your pet's fur, which actually extends contact time with fleas—so in some ways, summer can work in your favor. The downside is the natural oils may evaporate faster in heat, so you might need to reapply every 5-6 days instead of 7 during peak summer months.
