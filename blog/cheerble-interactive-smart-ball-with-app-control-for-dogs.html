Frequently Asked Questions

Is the ball durable enough for aggressive chewers?

Yes and no. The Cheerble survived three weeks with Max, a heavy player but not an extreme chewer. The rubber coating held up to constant tumbling and impact, but if you have a dog who actively destroys rubber toys (rather than just playing hard), this isn't the right fit. The app circuit board inside makes replacement more expensive than a standard ball, so durability matters here.

How does the app actually work, and do you need WiFi?

The app uses Bluetooth, not WiFi, so you need to be within roughly 30-50 feet of the ball for reliable connection—that's your backyard or a nearby park, not remote control from across town. The app interface is intuitive: quick-tap buttons for play modes, a light-control slider, and battery indicator. It took me literally 90 seconds to understand it fully.

What's the real battery life, and how often do you charge it?

Expect 3-4 hours of active play, not the advertised 5-6 hours. In my testing, a full charge took roughly 2 hours via USB cable. If you're planning a full day of play, you'll need to charge overnight or have the ball plugged in mid-afternoon. For weekday enrichment sessions (30-45 minutes), one charge covers 4-5 days of casual play.

How does this compare to non-smart interactive balls?

Standard interactive balls like Kong Wobbler cost $12-18 and require no charging or app, but they're passive—your dog triggers them through play rather than you triggering them. The Cheerble's app control means you can reward specific behaviors or extend play sessions remotely, which genuinely changes how you can engage with your dog during the day. For enrichment potential, not just entertainment, the smart features justify the higher price—if you'll actually use them.
