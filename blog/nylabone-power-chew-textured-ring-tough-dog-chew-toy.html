Frequently Asked Questions

Will my aggressive chewer destroy this in a week?

Depends on aggression level, but probably not. Most owners report 2-4 weeks of regular use before significant degradation. Dogs that completely demolish rope toys and rubber toys in days typically get measurable longevity here. That said, an extreme power chewer (the kind that goes through antlers) might accelerate this timeline.

Is the textured surface just marketing or does it actually matter?

It actually matters. The texture creates grip variation that keeps dogs engaged longer—smooth toys get boring faster. The surface also slows down the chewing pace slightly because it requires more force variation. Boring toys get abandoned; toys with textural interest get maintained.

Should I worry about the fragments my dog is ingesting?

Nylon fragments are a known element of chew toys made from this material. If your dog has a history of GI sensitivity or blockages, skip this and go with rubber or antler alternatives instead. For most healthy dogs, occasional nylon fragments pass through without issue—but monitor the first week and watch for vomiting or constipation.

Is this better than the original Nylabone chew sticks?

Yes, distinctly. The Power Chew line uses denser nylon and is purpose-built for serious chewers, while original Nylabone sticks are mid-level toys. If you've tried regular Nylabone products and your dog destroyed them quickly, the Power Chew ring is the upgrade you need.

What size should I buy for my dog?

Nylabone offers multiple sizes. Medium works for dogs 25-50 lbs, Large for 50+ lbs. Don't downsize thinking it'll last longer—undersized toys are uncomfortable and get abandoned. Match the actual weight and jaw size of your dog.
