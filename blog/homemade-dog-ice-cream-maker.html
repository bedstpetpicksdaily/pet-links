Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a regular ice cream maker for dog ice cream?

Yes, you can use a regular ice cream maker for dog ice cream, but it's best to dedicate one machine to pet treats to avoid cross-contamination. Most standard ice cream makers work perfectly fine with dog-safe ingredients like plain yogurt, peanut butter, and fruit purees.

What ingredients are safe to use in homemade dog ice cream?

Safe ingredients include plain yogurt, banana, apple, peanut butter (xylitol-free), pumpkin puree, and bone broth. Avoid chocolate, xylitol, grapes, raisins, avocado, and macadamia nuts, as these are toxic to dogs.

Do I need a special ice cream maker for dog treats?

No, you don't need a special machine—a regular ice cream maker works great for dog ice cream. However, some pet-specific machines exist that are smaller and designed for batch sizes appropriate for dogs, though they're optional.

How long does homemade dog ice cream last in the freezer?

Homemade dog ice cream typically lasts 2-4 weeks in the freezer when stored in an airtight container. Always check for freezer burn or off-smells before serving, and consume within the recommended timeframe for best quality.
