Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between raw dog food and freeze-dried pet food?

Raw dog food contains fresh, uncooked ingredients that preserve natural enzymes and nutrients but require careful storage and handling. Freeze-dried pet food removes moisture while maintaining nutritional integrity, offering convenience and longer shelf life while retaining many benefits of raw feeding. Both are premium options; the choice depends on lifestyle and storage capabilities.

Why is grain-free pet nutrition becoming more popular?

Grain-free formulations are increasingly recommended for pets with food sensitivities, allergies, or digestive issues. Many pet owners report improvements in coat quality, energy levels, and digestive health when switching to grain-free options. However, it's always best to consult with a veterinarian about your specific pet's nutritional needs.

Is Zeal Pet Food suitable for all pets?

Zeal Pet Food offers specialized formulations for both dogs and cats, with various options to address different age groups, activity levels, and dietary preferences. However, it's important to introduce any new premium pet food gradually and consult with a veterinarian if your pet has specific health conditions or dietary restrictions.

How should I store premium natural pet food?

Storage depends on the product type. Raw dog food should be kept frozen until use, then thawed in the refrigerator. Freeze-dried pet food can be stored at room temperature in a cool, dry place. Always follow the specific storage instructions provided by the manufacturer to maintain nutritional quality.

Can premium natural pet food improve my pet's health?

Many pet owners report positive health changes after switching to premium natural pet food, including improved digestion, better coat quality, increased energy, and fewer allergic reactions. However, results vary by individual pet, and it's advisable to work with your veterinarian to determine if premium nutrition is right for your specific pet's needs.
