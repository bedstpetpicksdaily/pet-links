Frequently Asked Questions

How does the PetSafe Pawz Away compare to underground invisible fencing in terms of reliability?

Underground systems are more reliable for determined escape artists because the buried wire creates a hard boundary that's difficult to defeat. Wireless systems rely on radio signal, which can degrade during weather or near metal structures. However, wireless systems are significantly cheaper to install (essentially free vs $300-800 professional installation) and offer more flexibility if you move or want to adjust the boundary. For a moderately obedient dog, the wireless system performs adequately. For a serial fence-breaker, underground wins on reliability.

Is the receiver collar safe for dogs with medical conditions or anxiety?

The vibration-only mode is genuinely safer for anxious dogs and shouldn't trigger panic in most pets, but static correction can exacerbate anxiety-related issues in sensitive dogs. I'd strongly recommend testing the vibration-only setting for at least two weeks before considering static correction. If your dog has heart conditions, consult your vet before using static correction—the stress response could be problematic. The collar weight itself isn't dangerous, but some small dogs show discomfort wearing it for extended periods.

Does this work in apartments or rental properties?

Yes—this is actually one of Pawz Away's biggest advantages. Renters love it because there's zero permanent modification to the property. You place the transmitter indoors and set the boundary within your rented yard space. Just remove it when you move. This flexibility alone justifies the price for renters who might otherwise be stuck managing an escaping dog without any containment system.

What's the realistic boundary range, and does it actually stay 90 feet in all directions?

The 90-foot advertised range is achievable in open spaces with clear weather, but real-world testing shows degradation in three scenarios: dense vegetation reduces range by 10-15 feet, metal structures nearby (gutters, siding, fencing) can create dead zones, and heavy rain or storms cause fluctuation. I'd plan for 75-85 feet reliable range in typical suburban yards. The boundary is perfectly circular—you can't adjust it to rectangular shapes, which is the biggest limitation for non-square properties.

How much does the PetSafe Pawz Away actually cost compared to other wireless fence options?

At the $150-300 price point depending on the model, Pawz Away sits in the mid-range of wireless systems. Cheaper no-name wireless fences ($80-120) exist but have significantly lower reliability ratings and shorter range claims. More premium systems from brands like Invisible Fence or Radio Fence run $300-600. For the money, Pawz Away offers reasonable value if you need wireless specifically. However, if you're willing to go the buried wire route, the long-term reliability often justifies spending $300-500 on professional installation.
