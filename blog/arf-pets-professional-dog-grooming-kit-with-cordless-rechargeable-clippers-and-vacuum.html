Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ The cordless design genuinely changes the grooming experience. No cord tangling around your dog, no hunting for outlets, and movement feels natural. I could work around my Golden Retriever's ears and paws without that constant tug of a cable—it shaved at least 15 minutes off my usual session.
  • ✓ The integrated vacuum attachment actually works. It's not perfect suction, but it catches roughly 70-80% of clipped fur before it hits the floor. Summer grooming usually means fur tumbleweeds across my house for days; this kit reduced that significantly. The container empties easily and doesn't clog.
  • ✓ Multiple blade and guard options ship with the kit, letting you adjust cutting length without buying separate attachments. I went from a 10mm guard for body work down to a 2mm for detail work around the face—all within the same device. That flexibility alone saves money compared to buying multiple clippers.
  • ✓ Battery runtime was solid for typical home grooming sessions. A full charge lasted through both my medium-sized dogs (roughly 90 minutes of active use) without the motor losing power or getting noticeably weaker. The charging base is compact enough to store in a grooming cabinet.
Cons
  • ✗ The vacuum attachment, while functional, feels like a bolted-on feature rather than a seamlessly integrated design. It adds noticeable weight to the clipper head, and switching between vacuum-on and vacuum-off modes takes two hands and takes some getting used to. If you have a very fidgety or anxious dog, the added bulk and slight vibration might be more irritating than helpful.
  • ✗ Blade maintenance requires attention. The blades dull faster than I expected—after about 6-8 grooming sessions on my two dogs, I noticed pulling instead of clean cutting, especially around tougher coat areas. Replacement blades exist but add ongoing costs that some competitors don't require as frequently.

Our Verdict

The Arf Pets Professional Dog Grooming Kit delivers real value if you're serious about home grooming and don't mind a small learning curve. The cordless freedom and integrated vacuum genuinely reduce setup time and mess, which matters when grooming becomes a regular routine rather than a one-off task. At its current price point, it sits firmly in the "invest in your dog's care" category rather than a throwaway purchase—and with 500+ reviews backing a 4.3-star average, you're not gambling on an untested product. That said, the blade dulling issue and the vacuum's added weight mean this kit works best for patient dogs and owners willing to invest in blade maintenance. Skip it if you groom rarely or have a dog that absolutely hates any extra equipment near their face.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the battery actually last on a single charge?

Real-world runtime sits around 90-120 minutes of active clipping on a full charge, depending on coat thickness and how often you're running the vacuum function. Medium-to-long coats and constant vacuum use will pull that down to closer to 90 minutes. For most home grooming sessions on a single dog, one charge is enough. If you're grooming multiple large dogs back-to-back, a second battery or top-up would be smart.

Will this handle thick or curly coats, or is it better for short-haired dogs?

This kit works well for most coat types, but thick, matted, or heavily curly coats (think Doodles or Poodles without prior detangling) will strain the motor and dull blades faster. The clippers handle short, straight, and moderately curly coats excellently. If your dog has serious matting, a professional groomer or a detangling session before using this kit is necessary—trying to force matted hair through will damage the blades and frustrate you both.

Is the vacuum suction strong enough to justify calling it a 'grooming kit with vacuum'?

Be realistic about expectations. The vacuum isn't a shop-vac replacement—it's a "good enough" attachment that captures fur before it settles everywhere. Expect 70-80% capture rate, meaning some fur will still escape. It's best viewed as a convenience feature that reduces cleanup time, not eliminates it. If you're hoping for zero mess, a traditional grooming table with a shop-vac on the side is still the gold standard, but that's a whole different investment level.
