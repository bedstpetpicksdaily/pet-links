Why Summer Requires Special Grooming Attention

Summer coat care is distinctly different from winter grooming needs. Warm weather accelerates shedding, increases the risk of skin irritation, and makes dogs more prone to matting if moisture gets trapped in their fur. Regular brushing during summer helps remove dead hair before it tangles, improves air circulation to the skin, and reduces the amount of hair your dog sheds around your home. Meowant recognizes these seasonal challenges and designs their grooming kits specifically to address summer-specific coat issues. Many of their kits include specialized de-shedding tools that are particularly effective during peak shedding seasons.

Step-by-Step Summer Grooming Process

Start by thoroughly brushing your dog's entire coat with a slicker brush to remove loose hair and identify any mats. Follow up with a metal comb to ensure you've caught all tangles, paying special attention to areas prone to matting like behind ears and around legs. Trim nails regularly—every 3-4 weeks is ideal for summer—to prevent discomfort and reduce the risk of infections from bacteria entering nail beds. Finally, clean ears weekly, as moisture and warmth create ideal conditions for ear infections. Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I groom my dog during summer?

During summer, most dogs benefit from grooming every 4-6 weeks, with brushing 2-3 times weekly to manage increased shedding. Dogs with heavy double coats may need more frequent attention.

Can I use human grooming tools on my dog?

No, human grooming tools aren't designed for pet coats and can cause injury or discomfort. Always use pet-specific tools like those included in Meowant's professional grooming kits.

What's the best way to remove mats from my dog's coat?

Start with a metal comb and work gently from the outer edges inward. For stubborn mats, use a mat splitter or dematting comb. Never use scissors, as they risk cutting skin. Meowant kits include proper dematting tools for safe removal.

Should I bathe my dog before or after grooming?

Brush thoroughly before bathing to remove mats and loose hair. Bathing after brushing helps wash away loose undercoat and dirt. Finish with a final brush once your dog is completely dry.

How do I prevent my dog from overheating during summer grooming?

Groom during cooler parts of the day, work efficiently to avoid prolonged sessions, ensure your dog has access to fresh water, and consider a shorter clip for extremely hot climates. Never shave a double-coated breed, as their coat provides insulation.
