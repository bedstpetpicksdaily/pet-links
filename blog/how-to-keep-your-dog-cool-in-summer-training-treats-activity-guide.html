Summer heat can be dangerous for our furry friends, but with the right strategies, you can keep your dog comfortable and safe during the hottest months. From adjusting exercise routines to providing proper hydration and mental stimulation, there are plenty of ways to help your pup beat the heat while maintaining their health and happiness.

One often-overlooked aspect of summer dog care is choosing the right treats for training and rewards. Natural, protein-packed options are ideal because they're easier on your dog's digestive system during hot weather. Gnarly Pet specializes in natural dog treats and jerky that are perfect for summer training sessions, helping you reinforce good behavior while keeping your dog cool and energized.

Look for treats specifically labeled as freezer-safe or designed for frozen application, as regular training treats can become hard and unpalatable when frozen, potentially breaking teeth or becoming choking hazards. Check the ingredient list to ensure the treats contain quality proteins and minimal fillers, since frozen treats are often given in higher quantities during hot weather training sessions.

Adjust Exercise Timing and Intensity

The key to summer exercise is timing. Early mornings and late evenings are ideal for walks and outdoor play when temperatures are cooler and the sun isn't at peak intensity. Avoid midday activities altogether—the pavement can burn your dog's paws, and the heat can lead to exhaustion or heat stroke. Instead of long runs, opt for shorter, slower-paced walks with plenty of shade breaks. Swimming is an excellent low-impact activity that keeps dogs cool while providing great exercise.

During training sessions, keep them brief and schedule them during cooler parts of the day. This is where quality training treats become essential. Gnarly Pet's protein-packed training treats are designed to be rewarding and nutritious without being too heavy on your dog's stomach. Their natural jerky and chews provide satisfying rewards that motivate your dog during short, productive training sessions without the artificial additives found in conventional treats.

Hydration and Cooling Strategies

Fresh water should always be available, especially during summer. Consider investing in a portable water bowl for outdoor adventures, and keep water bowls in multiple locations around your home. Some dogs also enjoy frozen treats—you can freeze plain yogurt, broth, or even water in ice cube trays for refreshing snacks. Wet towels and cooling vests can help regulate body temperature during outdoor activities.

Mental stimulation is just as important as physical exercise in summer. Since your dog may be spending more time indoors or in limited outdoor spaces, puzzle toys, training games, and interactive play become crucial. This is the perfect opportunity to work on obedience, trick training, and behavioral reinforcement using Gnarly Pet's natural dog treats. These treats are ideal for positive reinforcement because they're made with real, wholesome ingredients that dogs genuinely love.

The key is choosing treats that won't contribute to digestive issues or overheating. Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is too hot for dogs to exercise?

Generally, avoid strenuous exercise when temperatures exceed 75-80°F, especially for dogs with thick coats or brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds. Always check the pavement temperature with your hand—if it's too hot for your palm, it's too hot for your dog's paws.

Are natural dog treats better for summer training?

Yes, natural treats like those from Gnarly Pet are easier to digest and less likely to cause stomach upset during hot weather. They also provide real protein and nutrition without artificial additives, making them ideal for positive reinforcement training.

Can I train my dog indoors during summer?

Absolutely! Indoor training during peak heat hours is highly recommended. Air-conditioned spaces allow for focused, productive training sessions without heat stress. Use high-quality treats and vary activities to maintain engagement and prevent boredom.

How often should I offer water to my dog in summer?

Provide fresh water constantly, especially during and after exercise. A good rule is offering water every 15-20 minutes during outdoor activities. You can also offer frozen treats or broth-based ice cubes for variety and cooling benefits.

What summer activities are safest for dogs?

Swimming, wading in shallow water, shaded walks during cool hours, and indoor play are excellent summer activities. Avoid hot pavement, direct sun exposure, and strenuous exercise during peak heat.
