Frequently Asked Questions

How many hot dogs do competitive eaters eat in Nathan's Hot Dog Contest?

At Nathan's Famous Hot Dog Eating Contest, competitors have 10 minutes to eat as many hot dogs and buns as possible. The current record is 76 hot dogs and buns, set by Joey Chestnut in 2021, though this record has been challenged multiple times since then.

What is the best technique for eating hot dogs quickly?

Professional competitive eaters use several techniques including dunking buns in water to make them easier to swallow, separating the hot dog from the bun, and using specific breathing patterns to maximize consumption. Most top competitors also train their stomachs for weeks beforehand to increase capacity.

Are there health risks to competitive hot dog eating?

Yes, competitive eating can cause serious health issues including stomach rupture, aspiration, and severe dehydration. Most competitive eaters work with medical professionals and follow strict training regimens to minimize risks, though the sport remains inherently dangerous.

When is Nathan's Hot Dog Eating Contest held each year?

Nathan's Famous Hot Dog Eating Contest is held annually on July 4th at Coney Island in Brooklyn, New York. The event has been running since 1972 and is one of the most popular eating competitions in the world.
