Frequently Asked Questions

How do I prepare freeze-dried pet food for my dog?

Most freeze-dried products can be fed dry directly from the bag or rehydrated with water/broth to return them closer to raw form. Preparation methods vary by brand—check product instructions for specific thawing times and water ratios. Rehydration typically takes 5-10 minutes and can improve digestibility for some dogs.

Is freeze-dried pet food safe for all dogs?

Freeze-dried formats are generally safe for adult dogs and are often recommended for those with sensitive stomachs due to minimal processing and absence of synthetic binders. However, puppies, senior dogs, or those with specific health conditions should be introduced gradually and monitored. Consult your veterinarian before switching if your dog has existing digestive or health concerns.

Why is freeze-dried pet food more expensive than regular kibble?

Freeze-drying is a costly preservation process that removes 98% of moisture while retaining nutritional integrity—requiring specialized equipment and quality ingredient sourcing. Premium brands like Zeal add grain-free formulations and marketing costs. The result is higher per-unit pricing, though concentrated nutrition means smaller portions can reduce long-term feeding costs.

How long does freeze-dried pet food stay fresh?

Shelf life depends on storage conditions. Properly stored freeze-dried food can last 12-24 months unopened in cool, dry conditions. Once opened, consume within 2-4 weeks to prevent moisture reabsorption and nutrient degradation. Unlike raw frozen food, freeze-dried doesn't require continuous refrigeration, making it ideal for travel and summer outdoor activities.

Can I mix freeze-dried food with regular kibble?

Yes, gradual mixing is an effective transition strategy and allows you to reduce costs by using freeze-dried as a supplement rather than complete replacement. Start with 25% freeze-dried, 75% regular kibble and adjust ratios over 2-3 weeks. This approach maintains nutritional benefits while easing digestive adaptation and managing budget constraints.

What's the difference between freeze-dried and traditional raw diets?

Freeze-dried food mimics raw diet nutrition by removing moisture and avoiding heat processing, but eliminates refrigeration requirements and handling risks. Traditional raw diets require continuous freezing, careful thawing, and food safety protocols. Freeze-dried provides raw-like benefits in shelf-stable format, making it more practical for most households.
