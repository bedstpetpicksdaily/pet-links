Why Raw Food & Frozen Meals Help Keep Dogs Cool

Raw and frozen meals are naturally high in moisture content, which helps dogs stay hydrated during hot summer months. Unlike dry kibble that can actually dehydrate pets, raw food contains 70-80% water, mimicking the natural diet of wolves and wild canines. This moisture content aids in thermoregulation—your dog's body uses water to cool itself through panting and sweating through paw pads. When you feed your dog Smallbatch Pets' premium frozen raw meals, you're providing both nutrition and hydration in every bite.

Additionally, raw diets are easier for dogs to digest than processed kibble, which means less metabolic heat is generated during digestion. This is particularly important in summer when your dog's body is already working to maintain optimal temperature. The high-quality proteins and fats in Smallbatch Pets' frozen raw meals digest efficiently, reducing the thermic effect of food and keeping your dog cooler from the inside out.

Summer Feeding Tips for Maximum Comfort

To maximize cooling benefits, consider serving Smallbatch Pets frozen raw meals directly from the freezer or slightly thawed for extra coldness. Some dogs love eating frozen meals as they provide natural cooling while eating. You can also freeze bone broth in ice cube trays and offer them as refreshing treats throughout the day. Feed during cooler parts of the day—early morning or evening—to avoid digestive stress when temperatures peak. Always ensure fresh, cool water is available, and monitor your dog for signs of heat stress including excessive panting, letharness, or loss of appetite.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw food safe for dogs in hot weather?

Yes, raw food is safe and beneficial in hot weather when handled properly. The high moisture content actually helps with hydration. Store frozen meals at proper temperatures and thaw in the refrigerator. Smallbatch Pets' raw meals are formulated for complete nutrition and safety.

Can I feed frozen meals directly to my dog?

Yes, many dogs enjoy eating frozen meals directly from the freezer, which provides cooling benefits while eating. You can also slightly thaw them for easier consumption. Smallbatch Pets' frozen raw meals are designed for this convenience.

How much frozen raw food should I feed my dog in summer?

Feeding amounts depend on your dog's weight, age, and activity level. Generally, feed 2-3% of your dog's body weight daily. During summer, some dogs may eat less due to heat, which is normal. Consult Smallbatch Pets' feeding guidelines or your veterinarian for personalized recommendations.

What's the difference between Smallbatch frozen raw and freeze-dried options?

Frozen raw meals are ready-to-serve and highest in moisture content. Freeze-dried meals are lightweight, shelf-stable, and rehydrate with water or broth. Both offer complete nutrition; choose based on your storage space and convenience preferences.

Can bone broth help keep my dog cool?

Bone broth provides hydration and joint support. While it won't directly cool your dog, mixing cold broth with meals increases overall hydration. Smallbatch Pets' bone broth can be frozen into cubes for refreshing treats throughout summer.
