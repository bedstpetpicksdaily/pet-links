    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is pet insurance actually worth it in 2026?

    Pet insurance is worth it in 2026 if your pet is young, healthy, and you want protection against unexpected vet bills that can easily exceed $3,000–$10,000 for emergencies or surgeries. With veterinary costs continuing to rise, a comprehensive plan costing $30–$60 per month can save you thousands if your pet develops a serious illness or has an accident. It's especially valuable for breeds prone to hereditary conditions, as long as you enroll before those conditions are diagnosed.

    What is the best pet insurance company in 2026?

    In 2026, top-rated pet insurance companies include Figo, Embrace, Trupanion, and Healthy Paws, each standing out for different reasons like fast reimbursements, customizable deductibles, or unlimited annual coverage. Trupanion is particularly praised for paying vets directly, eliminating out-of-pocket waiting, while Embrace is known for covering exam fees and offering a diminishing deductible reward. The best choice depends on your pet's age, breed, and your budget, so comparing at least three quotes is strongly recommended.

    How much does pet insurance cost per month in 2026?

    In 2026, pet insurance typically costs between $20–$100 per month for dogs and $10–$40 per month for cats, depending on your pet's age, breed, location, and the level of coverage you choose. Accident-only plans are the most affordable, while comprehensive accident-and-illness plans with wellness add-ons sit at the higher end of the range. Larger breeds and older pets will generally face higher premiums, so insuring your pet while they're young locks in lower rates.

    Does pet insurance cover pre-existing conditions in 2026?

    Most pet insurance companies in 2026 do not cover pre-existing conditions, meaning any illness or injury your pet had before the policy start date will be excluded from coverage. However, some insurers like Embrace and Spot may cover curable pre-existing conditions after a symptom-free waiting period of 12 months. This makes it critical to enroll your pet as early as possible before any health issues arise and are documented in their veterinary records.
