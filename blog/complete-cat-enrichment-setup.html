Frequently Asked Questions

What do I need for a complete cat enrichment setup at home?

A complete cat enrichment setup should include vertical space like cat trees or wall shelves, scratching posts, puzzle feeders, interactive toys, and window perches for bird watching. You should also rotate toys regularly and incorporate hiding spots like tunnels or covered beds to keep your cat mentally stimulated. Aim to engage all of your cat's natural instincts including hunting, climbing, scratching, and exploring.

How many hours a day should I spend enriching my cat?

You should dedicate at least two to three 10-15 minute interactive play sessions per day to actively engaging your cat with wand toys or laser pointers. Beyond structured play, passive enrichment like puzzle feeders, window views, and solo toys can keep your cat stimulated throughout the day without requiring your constant involvement. Consistency matters more than duration, so a regular daily routine will have the greatest positive impact on your cat's behavior and wellbeing.

What are the best enrichment toys for indoor cats?

The best enrichment toys for indoor cats include feather wand toys for interactive hunting play, treat-dispensing puzzle feeders to slow down eating and engage problem-solving, and crinkle balls or motorized mice for solo play. Puzzle boards with varying difficulty levels are highly recommended because they can grow with your cat's abilities and prevent boredom. Rotating a collection of 8-10 toys by swapping them out every few days keeps them feeling novel and exciting to your cat.

How do I know if my cat is bored and needs more enrichment?

Common signs that your cat needs more enrichment include over-grooming, destructive scratching of furniture, excessive meowing, weight gain from inactivity, or aggression toward people and other pets. A bored cat may also sleep far more than the typical 12-16 hours or follow you constantly around the house out of understimulation. If you notice these behaviors, start by adding one or two new enrichment elements like a puzzle feeder or a window bird feeder outside to spark their interest.
