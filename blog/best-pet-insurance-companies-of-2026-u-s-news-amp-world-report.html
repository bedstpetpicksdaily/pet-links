Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best pet insurance company in 2026?

According to U.S. News & World Report, top-rated pet insurance companies in 2026 include Healthy Paws, Embrace, and Figo, which consistently rank highly for coverage options, claim processing speed, and customer satisfaction. The best choice depends on your pet's breed, age, and your budget, so comparing multiple providers is recommended. Using a comparison tool can help you find the right balance between premium costs and reimbursement percentages.

How much does pet insurance cost per month in 2026?

The average cost of pet insurance in 2026 ranges from $25 to $70 per month for dogs and $15 to $40 per month for cats, depending on the provider, your pet's age, breed, and your location. Accident-only plans are typically the most affordable, while comprehensive accident and illness plans cost more but offer broader coverage. Deductibles and reimbursement levels you choose also significantly impact your monthly premium.

Is pet insurance actually worth it?

Pet insurance is generally worth it if your pet requires unexpected emergency care or is diagnosed with a chronic illness, as vet bills can easily reach thousands of dollars. For example, treating a dog's torn ACL or a cat's urinary blockage can cost $3,000 to $6,000 or more, which far exceeds most annual premiums. If you have a young, healthy pet, starting a policy early locks in lower rates and ensures pre-existing conditions won't exclude future coverage.

What does pet insurance not cover?

Most pet insurance plans do not cover pre-existing conditions, meaning any illness or injury your pet had before the policy start date will be excluded from reimbursement. Routine and preventive care like vaccinations, dental cleanings, and flea prevention are also typically not covered unless you add a wellness rider to your plan. Breed-specific hereditary conditions may be excluded by some insurers, so it's important to read the fine print before enrolling.
