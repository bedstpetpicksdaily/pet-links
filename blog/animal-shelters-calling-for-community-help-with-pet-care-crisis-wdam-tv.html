Why This Matters

Shelters are overwhelmed with intake numbers they haven't seen in years, while operating budgets remain strained. This directly affects the quality of care available for animals waiting for homes, and it impacts adoption timelines and availability. When shelters are stretched thin, they often rely on community donations and prepared pet owners who can properly care for newly adopted animals.

What Pet Owners Should Know

  • Shelters are asking for both monetary donations and supplies—food, bedding, cleaning products, and toys make a real difference
  • If you're adopting now, having essential supplies ready at home helps shelter staff focus resources on animals with greater needs
  • Pet ownership requires ongoing investment in quality care items; being prepared prevents returns and ensures animal welfare
