Quick Verdict

Purina Pro Plan edges out Hill's Science Diet for most dog owners due to better ingredient quality, more specialized formulas, and superior digestibility—though Hill's remains a solid veterinary-backed choice if your vet specifically recommends it. If budget allows, Pro Plan delivers better long-term health outcomes; if cost is tight or your vet pushes Hill's, either will keep your dog healthy.

Hill's Science Diet Overview

Hill's Science Diet has been the veterinary gold standard for decades, heavily promoted by vets and used in clinical settings. It's formulated with controlled ingredients and backed by research, making it reliable for dogs with digestive sensitivities or specific health conditions.

Pros:

  • Veterinary credibility: Most vets recommend it, which matters if your dog has medical issues
  • Controlled ingredient list: Consistent quality and minimal fillers
  • Specialized formulas: Excellent options for urinary health, weight management, and digestive care
  • Decades of research: Backed by peer-reviewed studies
  • Wide availability: Found in most pet stores and online

Cons:

  • Corn and soy content: Uses corn meal as a primary ingredient (not ideal for premium positioning)
  • Higher price for basic formulas: Costs more than mainstream brands but less transparent on ingredient sourcing
  • Lower protein options: Some formulas have modest protein levels (18-22%)
  • Artificial ingredients: Contains synthetic vitamins and some artificial preservatives
  • Less transparency: Doesn't disclose ingredient sourcing or manufacturing details publicly

Who It's For:

Hill's is ideal for dogs with diagnosed health conditions (kidney disease, urinary crystals, digestive disorders), senior dogs needing therapeutic nutrition, or owners who prioritize their vet's recommendation over premium ingredients. Best if your dog needs prescription or therapeutic formulation.

Purina Pro Plan Overview

Who It's For:

Pro Plan suits health-conscious owners seeking premium ingredients without prescription costs, dogs with sensitive stomachs (due to probiotics), active dogs needing higher protein, and owners who want modern nutrition science over traditional veterinary positioning. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hill's Science Diet or Purina Pro Plan better for dogs?

Purina Pro Plan is generally considered the stronger choice for most dogs due to its higher protein content, more extensive feeding trials, and strong backing from veterinary nutritionists and performance dog breeders. Hill's Science Diet is a solid option with good quality control, but Pro Plan's formulas tend to offer more muscle-supporting protein at a competitive price point. Ultimately, the best choice depends on your dog's specific health needs, age, and activity level.

Which is more expensive, Hill's Science Diet or Purina Pro Plan?

Both brands sit in a similar premium price range, but Hill's Science Diet typically costs slightly more per pound than Purina Pro Plan. For example, a 30 lb bag of Pro Plan Adult Chicken & Rice often runs $5–10 cheaper than a comparable bag of Hill's Science Diet at major retailers. This makes Pro Plan a slightly better value, especially considering its higher protein percentages.

Do vets recommend Hill's Science Diet or Purina Pro Plan?

Both brands are frequently recommended by veterinarians, but Purina Pro Plan has gained significant momentum in vet circles, particularly after Hill's faced a high-profile sodium recall in 2019 that damaged some trust. Many veterinary nutritionists and board-certified vets cite Pro Plan's rigorous research, WSAVA compliance, and long-term feeding trial data as reasons for their preference. Hill's still holds strong vet recommendations, especially for its prescription Prescription Diet therapeutic lines.

What is the difference between Hill's Science Diet and Purina Pro Plan ingredients?

Purina Pro Plan typically lists a named meat like chicken or salmon as its first ingredient and delivers higher crude protein percentages, often ranging from 26–30% for adult formulas. Hill's Science Diet also uses quality ingredients but tends to have slightly lower protein levels and includes more grain-based fillers like corn and wheat in some formulas. Pro Plan also avoids the controversial 'grain-free' trend while still offering carbohydrate variety, and both brands avoid artificial preservatives in their standard lines.
