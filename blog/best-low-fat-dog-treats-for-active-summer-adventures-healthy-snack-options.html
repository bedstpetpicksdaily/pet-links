Why Low-Fat Treats Are Ideal for Summer-Active Dogs

Active dogs have different nutritional needs than their less energetic counterparts. Low-fat treats offer the perfect balance of taste and nutrition without contributing excess calories. During summer months when dogs are more active, lightweight treats prevent digestive discomfort while providing sustained energy. Low-fat options are particularly beneficial for dogs prone to weight gain or those with sensitive digestive systems, making them universally appropriate for most canine companions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much low-fat treat can I give my active dog daily?

Treats should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's total daily caloric intake. For active dogs, this typically means 1-3 treats per day depending on size and activity level. Always adjust portions based on your individual dog's needs and consult your veterinarian if unsure.

Are PetTreatery treats suitable for dogs with sensitive stomachs?

Yes, PetTreatery's natural pet snacks are formulated with digestive health in mind. Their low-fat options and minimal ingredient lists make them suitable for many dogs with sensitivities. However, always introduce new treats gradually and consult your vet if your dog has specific dietary restrictions.

What's the difference between low-fat and regular dog treats?

Low-fat treats contain significantly less fat content, making them lighter and easier to digest. They're ideal for active dogs, weight-conscious pups, and those prone to pancreatitis. Regular treats may contain higher fat percentages for flavor and binding purposes.

Can I take PetTreatery treats on camping and hiking trips?

Absolutely. PetTreatery treats are lightweight and portable, making them perfect for outdoor adventures. Pack them in an insulated pouch to maintain freshness, and always bring adequate water for your dog during active outings.
