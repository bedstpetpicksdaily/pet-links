Road trips with your furry friend can be stressful if your pet isn't safely secured during the drive. A proper travel carrier transforms car rides from anxiety-inducing to comfortable for both you and your dog or cat, reducing distractions and keeping your pet protected in case of sudden stops or accidents.

What to Look For

  • Crash-tested structural integrity: Your carrier needs rigid walls and reinforced corners to protect your pet during sudden braking or minor collisions. Look for carriers that have undergone safety testing or meet DOT guidelines—this isn't the place to save money on flimsy materials.
  • Proper vehicle installation: The carrier should be easy to secure with a seatbelt, have anchor points, or feature seat-belt loops designed specifically for cars. A carrier that shifts around during turns defeats the purpose of having one in the first place.
  • Ventilation and visibility: Your pet needs adequate airflow without drafts, and enough window access to reduce anxiety and motion sickness. A dark, stuffy carrier makes car rides miserable and can cause stress-related behavioral issues.

What to Avoid

  • Soft-sided carriers and pet booster seats: While great for short vet visits, fabric carriers offer zero protection in accidents and provide inadequate support during vehicle movement. They also tend to tip or shift during turns, making your pet feel unsafe.
  • Oversized carriers: Bigger isn't better for car travel. A carrier that's too large allows your pet to be thrown around during acceleration, braking, and turns. Your pet should be able to stand and turn around, but the fit should be snug.
  • Carriers without proper vehicle attachment: A carrier sitting loose in the back seat becomes a projectile in emergency situations. Never use a carrier that doesn't have a clear, easy way to secure it with your vehicle's seatbelt system.
