Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is breed identification for both tests?

Both Embark and Wisdom Panel screen 350+ breeds and report accuracy rates above 99%. Real owners consistently report results matching visual appearance and shelter estimates—one customer's dog came back 42% golden retriever, 31% lab, 18% mixed, aligning with her actual appearance. The main difference: Wisdom Panel's 1,800+ genetic markers may yield slightly more granular results, but for most mixed breeds, both tests deliver reliable percentages.

What health conditions does each test actually screen for?

Embark includes health screening as part of its standard kit but doesn't publicly specify the exact number of conditions tested. Wisdom Panel 3.0 explicitly screens for 200+ genetic health conditions including hip dysplasia, heart issues, eye problems, and drug sensitivities like MDR1. If comprehensive health screening is your goal, Wisdom Panel provides more transparency and depth; Embark is better for basic health risk overview.

Is the huge price difference ($10 vs. $100-$200) justified?

That depends on your priorities. Embark's $10 price makes testing accessible to budget-conscious owners and eliminates decision paralysis—the low cost means you'll actually order it. Wisdom Panel's premium pricing reflects deeper health screening (200+ conditions vs. Embark's smaller panel) and veterinarian-backed reporting that translates genetic findings into actionable care changes. If you're comparing to veterinary genetic testing ($200-$500), both DNA kits save money; the question is whether you need Embark's affordability or Wisdom Panel's depth.

How long until I get results?

Both tests typically take 2-4 weeks for results, with Wisdom Panel often arriving within 2-3 weeks. Neither test provides rush results, so don't order either if you need answers in days. Plan ahead, especially during peak adoption season (July) when processing times can extend.

Can I actually use these results at my vet appointment?

Yes, but with caveats. Both tests provide data your vet will accept. Wisdom Panel's veterinarian-backed reports are formatted specifically for clinical use and explicitly explain health risks and drug sensitivities—one owner's vet directly changed medications based on MDR1 results. Embark's results are clinically valid but less explicitly formatted for veterinary interpretation. If you plan to bring results to a vet appointment for medical decision-making, Wisdom Panel's formatting makes that conversation easier.
