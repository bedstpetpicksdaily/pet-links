Understanding Your Dog's Heat Sensitivity

Dogs experience heat differently than humans, and certain breeds are more susceptible to heat-related stress. Brachycephalic breeds like Bulldogs, Pugs, and Boston Terriers struggle more in hot weather because of their shorter snouts and compromised breathing passages. Senior dogs, puppies, and those with thick coats also require extra attention during summer months. Heat exhaustion in dogs can lead to serious health complications, making preventative measures crucial for responsible pet ownership.

Setting Up the Perfect Outdoor Crate Environment

If your dog spends time outdoors, an appropriately set up crate becomes essential for both safety and comfort. AECOJOY's outdoor dog crates combine sturdy construction with ventilation optimization, ensuring proper airflow while protecting your pet from direct sunlight. When setting up an outdoor crate, position it in the shadiest area of your yard, ideally under a tree or patio cover. Pair it with one of AECOJOY's cooling pet beds inside the crate to create a comfortable den-like space. Add a water bowl with a spill-proof design, and consider a reflective cover to further reduce heat absorption. This setup allows your dog to enjoy outdoor time safely without overheating.

Essential Summer Pet Care Strategies

Beyond beds and crates, implement these additional cooling strategies: provide constant access to fresh water, take walks during cooler morning and evening hours, never leave your dog in a parked car, and trim longer coats if appropriate for your breed. AECOJOY's gel-infused cooling beds provide consistent temperature regulation throughout the day without needing to be refrigerated. The cooling effect is passive and lasts as long as your dog is resting on the bed, typically cooling for 6-8 hours of active use.

Yes! Many AECOJOY cooling beds are designed for versatile use. However, for outdoor use, we recommend placing the bed inside a covered crate or under a patio to protect it from direct UV exposure and weather damage.
