Frequently Asked Questions

How many Canadians own a dog or cat in 2026?

Approximately 58% of Canadian households own at least one pet, with dogs and cats being the most popular choices. That translates to roughly 8.2 million pet dogs and 8.5 million pet cats living in Canadian homes. Cat ownership slightly edges out dog ownership in total numbers, though dogs remain the most common single-pet choice.

How much does the average Canadian spend on their pet per year?

Canadian pet owners spend an average of $1,500 to $2,500 per year on their dogs or cats, covering food, veterinary care, grooming, and accessories. Veterinary costs are typically the largest expense, with routine annual checkups alone ranging from $200 to $400 depending on the province. Emergency vet visits can push annual spending significantly higher, which is why pet insurance adoption in Canada has been steadily growing.

What percentage of Canadian pet owners have pet insurance?

Roughly 25 to 30% of Canadian dog and cat owners currently have some form of pet insurance, a number that has grown considerably over the past five years. The rising cost of veterinary care is the primary driver pushing more Canadians toward coverage. Monthly premiums in Canada typically range from $30 to $100 depending on the pet's breed, age, and the level of coverage selected.

What is the most popular dog breed in Canada?

The Labrador Retriever has consistently ranked as Canada's most popular dog breed, followed closely by the French Bulldog and Golden Retriever. French Bulldogs have surged in popularity over recent years due to their adaptability to apartment living, which suits Canada's growing urban population. Regional preferences do vary, with certain working and cold-weather breeds like the Siberian Husky being notably more popular in northern and rural communities.
