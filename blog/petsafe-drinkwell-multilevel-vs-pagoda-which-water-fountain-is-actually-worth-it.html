PetSafe Drinkwell MultiLevel

The MultiLevel variant prioritizes capacity over compact design, holding approximately 100 ounces of water. This larger reservoir means refill frequency drops to every 48–72 hours in typical household conditions with two active pets, compared to daily refills for standard bowls. The three-tier cascading design accommodates different pet heights—cats often prefer the middle tier while dogs go for the top—making it genuinely useful in multi-pet homes rather than a gimmick.

Noise performance is the practical standout: the pump produces a gentle trickling sound at 2 AM rather than a grinding hum, making it viable for bedrooms or apartments where sound sensitivity matters. The trade-off is physical footprint—100 ounces demands more counter or floor space than compact alternatives.

Best for: Multi-pet households, work-from-home setups where refilling during the day is inconvenient, and homes where noise at night is a real concern

PetSafe Drinkwell Pagoda

The Pagoda positions itself as the more compact alternative, with a typical capacity around 70 ounces—roughly 30% less than the MultiLevel. This makes it suitable for smaller spaces or single-pet homes, though it demands daily refills in warm weather, which is the functional cost of the smaller footprint. The three-tier cascading design is identical in concept to the MultiLevel, but the reduced water volume means visible aeration and movement happen across a tighter space.

The stainless steel construction appears more premium than plastic equivalents, though real-world durability differences versus other PetSafe materials remain unclear from actual testing. The Pagoda's smaller size works as a genuine advantage only if you have space constraints or actively prefer frequent refilling as part of your pet care routine.

Best for: Single-pet homes, apartments with limited counter space, and owners who don't mind daily refills and prefer a more compact aesthetic

Our Verdict

MultiLevel wins for multi-pet households and work-from-home setups where the 100-ounce capacity (48–72 hour refill cycle) genuinely saves time. If you have two or more pets sharing water access, or you need a fountain that won't demand attention every single day, the larger reservoir justifies the extra footprint and cost. The noise performance also matters here—it's suitable for bedrooms without becoming a 2 AM annoyance.

Pagoda wins only if space is your hard constraint or you actively prefer compact design over convenience. At 70 ounces, it still requires daily refills in warm weather, which means it doesn't actually deliver less maintenance than the MultiLevel—it trades footprint for refill frequency. Choose this only if your kitchen counter literally cannot accommodate the MultiLevel, or if aesthetics genuinely matter more than practical utility.

Neither model is objectively "worth it" without context: both demand filter replacement commitment and both sit at a price point that assumes you're solving a real hydration problem (kidney disease prevention in cats, or pets refusing still water). If your pet drinks from a standard bowl without issue, save the money. If hydration is genuinely your concern, the MultiLevel's superior capacity-to-refill ratio makes it the more practical choice for most households.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does maintenance actually cost beyond the purchase price?

Filter replacement varies by brand—PetSafe's official filters run $15–25 per filter, and if you're replacing monthly (their recommendation), that's $180–300 annually just for filters. Many owners stretch this to 6–8 weeks between replacements by rinsing filters under tap water, but this reduces effectiveness. Budget maintenance as an ongoing cost, not a one-time purchase.

Will my cat or dog actually use a multi-level fountain?

Most pets adapt within minutes to hours—visible water movement triggers drinking behavior in pets that ignore static bowls. However, some pets remain indifferent. The critical variable is positioning: if the fountain height doesn't match your pet's natural drinking posture, they'll avoid it regardless of design. Test height compatibility before committing to either model.

Does the stainless steel on the Pagoda actually perform better than plastic?

Stainless steel resists staining and odors better than plastic over time, but both materials are durable if maintained. The real difference is aesthetic and long-term hygiene—stainless won't discolor or harbor bacteria as readily, but this justifies maybe $20–30 in price difference, not the full gap between models. Capacity difference matters more functionally.

How loud is the pump noise, really?

Both models produce a gentle trickling sound described as noticeable but not disruptive at night—roughly equivalent to a small tabletop water feature. If you're sensitive to any background noise, test audio samples online first. Older fountain models or competitors tend toward grinding hums; these PetSafe versions are genuinely quieter than the market average.

Should I choose based on the 4.3-star average rating?

Both models average 4.3 stars with 500+ reviews, but this masks diverging complaints: MultiLevel owners complain about space; Pagoda owners complain about frequent refilling. The rating doesn't tell you which trade-off fits your household. Read reviews that match your specific situation (multi-pet vs. single-pet, space constraints vs. convenience priority).
