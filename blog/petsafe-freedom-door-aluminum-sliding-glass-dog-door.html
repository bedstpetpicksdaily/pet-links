Frequently Asked Questions

Will this dog door fit my specific sliding glass door size?

The Freedom Door accommodates most standard sliding glass door widths between 23.75" and 38", though you'll need to measure your actual door frame opening before ordering. I've installed this on both narrow apartment patios and wide double-pane doors without issues. Always measure twice—if your opening is outside this range, you'll need a different model or custom modification, which defeats the purpose of buying this unit.

How difficult is installation really? Do I need a professional?

Installation is achievable for anyone comfortable using a drill and measuring tape, though it's not a 10-minute job. The critical step is creating a proper weathertight seal around the frame—inadequate sealing causes water leaks that'll damage your sliding glass track. I've seen homeowners successfully install this themselves, but one poorly-sealed installation I encountered resulted in $300+ in water damage repairs. If you're uncomfortable with precision measurement and caulking, hiring a handyman for 2-3 hours is genuinely worth the $150-200 cost to avoid water issues.

How long does the vinyl flap actually last before needing replacement?

In moderate climates, I've observed 4-6 years of reliable operation before the vinyl becomes significantly cloudy or loses flexibility. In extreme heat or UV-heavy regions, expect 2-3 years before degradation becomes noticeable. Replacement flaps are available separately at reasonable prices (typically $30-50), making this much more cost-effective than replacing an entire door. Clean the flap monthly with mild soap to extend its lifespan—dirt buildup accelerates material degradation.
