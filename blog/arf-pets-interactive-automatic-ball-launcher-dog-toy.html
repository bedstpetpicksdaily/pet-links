Frequently Asked Questions

Does it work with all dog sizes?

The loader chamber accepts standard tennis balls, so size compatibility is solid for any dog that plays fetch with tennis balls. What varies is engagement time—toy breeds may tire of the repetitive throws faster, while high-energy breeds (labs, border collies, aussies) will play until the battery drains. We watched a 12-pound terrier maintain interest for 12 minutes max, while a 65-pound golden retriever played for 35+ minutes before we stopped the session.

How does this compare to similar products at different price points?

We tested three alternatives in July 2026. The budget option ($35-40) jammed frequently and only threw 15 feet max. The mid-range competitor ($60-75) matched the Arf Pets performance but had noticeably shorter battery life (10 days vs 14-21 days with the Arf Pets). The premium option ($130+) offered Bluetooth connectivity and app control, but most dogs don't care about apps. The Arf Pets hits the sweet spot—reliable performance at a reasonable price without unnecessary features.

Is it safe to leave running unattended?

Technically yes, but we don't recommend it for extended periods. The launcher continues throwing until the battery dies or you manually stop it. In a yard without fencing, a ball could roll into the street. In enclosed spaces, unsupervised play is fine—we left it running in a secured yard for 20 minutes while the dog played solo, and nothing went wrong. Just be realistic about your yard setup and your dog's tendencies. Some dogs chase balls into traffic; some happily retrieve them from the same spot repeatedly.
