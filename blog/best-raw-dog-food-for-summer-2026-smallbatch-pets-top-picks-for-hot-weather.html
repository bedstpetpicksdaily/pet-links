Why Raw Dog Food is Perfect for Summer

Raw dog food offers several advantages during hot months. Unlike kibble, raw meals have high moisture content that supports hydration—critical when dogs are losing fluids through panting and increased activity. Raw proteins are also easier to digest, reducing the metabolic heat generated during digestion. Additionally, raw meals don't require cooking, keeping your kitchen cooler and making meal prep more convenient during summer heat.

Smallbatch Pets' raw and freeze-dried options deliver these benefits without compromise. Their frozen raw meals maintain nutritional integrity while their freeze-dried products offer convenience with concentrated nutrition. Both formats allow your dog to eat lighter, more digestible meals that support summer wellness.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe for summer feeding?

Yes, raw dog food is safe and beneficial for summer. Proper storage (frozen until feeding time) prevents bacterial growth, while the high moisture content and easy digestibility make raw meals ideal for hot weather. Always follow food safety guidelines and handle raw meals with the same care as human food.

How much raw dog food should I feed during summer?

Feed 2-3% of your dog's body weight daily, adjusted based on activity level and individual metabolism. Summer activity levels may vary, so monitor your dog's weight and energy. Smallbatch Pets provides feeding guidelines tailored to their specific products, making portion planning easier.

Can I mix Smallbatch Pets' freeze-dried food with regular meals?

Absolutely. Smallbatch Pets' freeze-dried options work excellently as meal toppers or mixed with other foods. They rehydrate quickly with water or broth, allowing you to customize nutrition and boost palatability while maintaining digestive health.

Does Smallbatch Pets offer fish-based raw meals for summer?

Yes, Smallbatch Pets includes fish-based proteins in their raw meal lineup. Fish provides omega-3 fatty acids supporting skin and coat health—beneficial during summer shedding season. These meals are particularly appealing to dogs preferring lighter proteins.

How do I transition my dog to Smallbatch Pets' raw food?

Transition gradually over 7-10 days, mixing increasing amounts of raw food with current meals. Start with 25% Smallbatch Pets raw food, increasing by 25% every few days. Most dogs adapt quickly; monitor digestion and adjust pace if needed.
