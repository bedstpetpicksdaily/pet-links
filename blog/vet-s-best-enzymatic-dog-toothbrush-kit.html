Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping consistency: Enzymatic toothpaste only works with daily use—brushing sporadically won't prevent plaque accumulation or tartar formation.
  • Pressing too hard: Many owners brush like they're cleaning their own teeth, which can irritate your dog's gums. Use gentle, circular motions instead.
  • Neglecting the gum line: The real problem starts where teeth meet gums, so focus your effort there rather than on the visible tooth surface.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Make it a ritual: Brush at the same time daily—after the evening walk works well—so it becomes habit for both you and your dog rather than something to remember.
  • Start slow with acclimation: Spend the first week just letting your dog taste the paste, then progress to 10 seconds of gentle brushing before building toward a full 2-3 minute session.
  • Pair with professional cleanings: This kit complements but doesn't replace veterinary dental cleanings. Use it to extend the time between professional treatments and catch problems early.
