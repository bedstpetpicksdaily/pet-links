Frequently Asked Questions

Does the self-cleaning button work on all coat types?

Yes, but with nuance. On lighter shedding (like a short-haired dog's maintenance brushing), the button clears fur easily. On heavy undercoat like my Husky during peak shed season, you might need to press twice to fully clear dense clumps. It still beats manually raking fur out by hand every single stroke.

Is this better than a FURminator or similar deshedding tool?

Different tools, different jobs. A FURminator targets deep undercoat aggressively but can irritate sensitive skin if used carelessly. The Arf Pets slicker is gentler, works on mats and general shedding equally well, and the ergonomic handle makes extended sessions less brutal on your hands. If your dog has sensitive skin, start with the Arf Pets. If you need aggressive undercoat removal and your dog tolerates firm brushing, a FURminator might be the better choice.

How often should I use this brush on my dog?

During heavy shedding seasons (spring and summer), 2-3 times weekly for 20-30 minutes per session works well. In fall and winter when shedding slows, once weekly is fine. The self-cleaning mechanism makes frequent use realistic since you're not spending half your time clearing the brush. For mat-prone dogs, daily quick brush-throughs with this tool prevent mats before they form.

Will this work for cats?

Technically yes, but most cats won't tolerate it. The slicker wires, while fine, are designed for dog coat density and spacing. Cats have more sensitive skin and prefer gentler tools. If your cat is unusually tolerant of grooming and sheds heavily, it might work for short 5-minute sessions, but there are better-designed cat brushes available.
