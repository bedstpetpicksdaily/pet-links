How long does the puzzle actually hold a dog's attention?

Smart/motivated dogs crack it in 2-3 sessions. The real value isn't infinite engagement—it's that 3-5 minute window of mental work that genuinely tires dogs. For high-energy breeds, that's worth running twice daily.

Can you use wet food or just kibble and treats?

Technically yes to wet food, but practically no—not without regret. Wet food (wet dog food, pumpkin, peanut butter) gets sticky in the compartments and creates cleanup headaches. Kibble and dry treats are ideal. If you want to use softer rewards, freeze them first, which slows decomposition and makes cleanup easier. I tested with frozen peanut butter and it worked well.

Is this safe for aggressive chewers?

Moderately aggressive, yes. Heavy-duty power chewers will eventually destroy it. The plastic is durable but not indestructible, and this toy isn't designed to replace rubber chew toys for that demographic. If your dog can demolish a Kong in weeks, this isn't the right toy. But for dogs who play with puzzles rather than demolish them, it's solid.
