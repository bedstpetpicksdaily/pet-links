What Makes Cold-Pressed Dog Food Different?

Cold-pressed dog food is manufactured at low temperatures, which preserves the nutritional integrity of raw ingredients far better than conventional extrusion methods. Traditional kibble uses heat treatment that can denature proteins and destroy beneficial enzymes, making it harder for your dog's digestive system to process. Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed formulas maintain these vital nutrients, resulting in a food that's easier to digest and more bioavailable for your dog's body. This gentle processing method is particularly beneficial for dogs with sensitive stomachs, as it reduces the risk of digestive upset and inflammation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cold-pressed dog food safe for all dogs?

Yes, cold-pressed dog food is safe and beneficial for most dogs. However, it's especially advantageous for dogs with sensitive stomachs, food allergies, or digestive issues. Always introduce new foods gradually, mixing Nextrition Pet's formula with your dog's current food over 7-10 days to allow their digestive system to adjust.

How does Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed food compare to raw dog food?

Cold-pressed food offers many benefits of raw feeding—preserved enzymes and nutrients—while being safer and more convenient. Unlike raw diets, cold-pressed food is pathogen-tested and doesn't carry the same bacterial contamination risks, making it a practical middle ground for health-conscious pet owners.

Can cold-pressed dog food help with specific allergies?

Nextrition Pet offers various natural formulas with limited ingredients, which can help identify and manage food sensitivities. By eliminating common allergens and artificial additives, many dogs show improvement in itching, digestive upset, and skin issues. Consult your vet to determine which Nextrition formula best suits your dog's specific allergies.

How should I store Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed dog food?

Cold-pressed dog food should be stored in a cool, dry place and refrigerated after opening. Unlike traditional kibble, it has a shorter shelf life due to the absence of artificial preservatives. Always check the package for specific storage instructions and expiration dates.
