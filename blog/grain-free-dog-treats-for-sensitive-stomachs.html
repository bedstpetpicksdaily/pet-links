What Are the Best Grain Free Dog Treats for Sensitive Stomachs?

Grain free dog treats are exactly what they sound like — snacks formulated without common grains like wheat, corn, or soy. These ingredients are frequent culprits behind digestive upset in dogs with food sensitivities or intolerances. By removing them from the equation, manufacturers are able to create treats that are gentler on the gut while still delivering the flavor and nutrition your dog craves.

What sets the top grain free dog treats for sensitive stomachs for dogs apart from the rest is the quality of their substitute ingredients. The best options lean on wholesome proteins like salmon, turkey, or lamb, combined with easily digestible carbohydrates such as sweet potato or chickpea. Many also include added prebiotics or fiber to actively support gut health, which is a huge bonus if your dog has been struggling for a while.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are grain free dog treats actually healthier?

Grain-free treats eliminate common allergens like wheat and corn, which benefit dogs with food sensitivities. However, healthy grains are fine for most dogs — grain-free is most important for dogs with diagnosed grain allergies.

What ingredients should I avoid in dog treats?

Avoid treats containing xylitol (toxic to dogs), artificial colors, BHA/BHT preservatives, propylene glycol, and unnamed 'meat by-products'. Always read the ingredient list.

How many treats can I give my dog per day?

Treats should make up no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. For most medium-sized dogs, that's 5-10 small treats per day depending on treat calorie content.

Are grain free treats good for training?

Yes. Small, soft grain-free treats work well for training because dogs find them highly rewarding and they're easy to consume quickly between commands.
