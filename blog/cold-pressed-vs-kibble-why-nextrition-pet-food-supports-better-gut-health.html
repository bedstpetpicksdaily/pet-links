Understanding Cold-Pressed Dog Food

Cold-pressed dog food is manufactured at low temperatures (typically below 90°C), which preserves the integrity of vital nutrients, enzymes, and natural probiotics. Unlike kibble, which undergoes high-heat extrusion processes that can degrade amino acids and destroy beneficial bacteria, cold-pressed formulas maintain the nutritional profile closer to raw ingredients.

Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed recipes are specifically designed with canine digestive science in mind. Each batch contains natural, whole-food ingredients without artificial preservatives, fillers, or by-products. The result is a nutrient-dense meal that your dog's digestive system recognizes and processes efficiently.

Why Kibble Falls Short for Gut Health

Traditional kibble relies on high-temperature extrusion to create shelf-stable pellets. While convenient and affordable, this process generates heat damage that compromises essential nutrients and eliminates naturally occurring enzymes. Kibble also often contains grain fillers and synthetic additives that can trigger digestive sensitivity in many dogs.

Additionally, kibble requires high moisture absorption in the gut, which can lead to bloating and digestive discomfort. Dogs fed primarily kibble frequently experience inconsistent stools, gas, and reduced nutrient absorption—signs that their digestive systems are working harder than necessary.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cold-pressed dog food safe for all dogs?

Yes, cold-pressed dog food is safe for most dogs and often *beneficial* for those with sensitive stomachs. However, always consult your veterinarian before switching foods, especially if your dog has specific health conditions or allergies.

How long does it take to see improvements in my dog's digestion?

Many pet parents notice positive changes within 2-3 weeks of switching to cold-pressed food like Nextrition Pet. Full digestive adjustment may take 4-6 weeks as your dog's microbiome stabilizes.

Can I mix cold-pressed food with kibble?

Yes, you can gradually introduce cold-pressed food by mixing it with your dog's current kibble, increasing the ratio over 7-10 days. This helps prevent digestive upset during the transition.

Does Nextrition Pet offer supplements to support gut health further?

Yes, Nextrition Pet offers premium pet supplements designed to complement their cold-pressed meals, including probiotics and digestive enzymes that further support optimal gut health.

Why is cold-pressed food more expensive than kibble?

Cold-pressed manufacturing preserves nutrients but is more labor-intensive and uses higher-quality whole-food ingredients, resulting in higher production costs—but superior nutritional value for your dog.
