Understanding Summer's Impact on Canine Digestion

Summer brings unique challenges to your dog's digestive system. Increased heat can slow digestion, alter appetite patterns, and create the perfect environment for harmful bacteria growth. Additionally, many dogs spend more time outdoors during summer, exposing them to different water sources, plants, and potential pathogens that can upset their stomach. Understanding these seasonal changes is the first step toward proactive gut health management.

Your dog's gut microbiome is delicate and easily disrupted by environmental stressors. When temperatures soar, dogs naturally drink more water and their activity levels increase, both of which can impact digestive balance. This is where premium nutrition becomes essential—specifically formulations designed to support gut integrity and maintain healthy bacteria colonies.

Summer Feeding Strategies for Optimal Digestion

Adjust feeding schedules during summer to work with your dog's natural rhythms. Many dogs prefer smaller, more frequent meals when it's hot outside rather than one large meal. Consider offering Nextrition's cold-pressed food slightly chilled on particularly hot days—it's refreshing and aids digestion. Always ensure fresh water is available, but monitor intake to prevent rapid consumption that can cause bloating.
