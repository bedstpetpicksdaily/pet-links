Frequently Asked Questions

Does the microchip reader work through thick cat collars or ID tags?

Yes—the reader is strong enough to detect microchips through most standard collars. However, extremely thick leather or metal-studded collars can occasionally cause brief delays. During testing, my cat wearing a standard breakaway collar passed through without any issues.

What happens if your cat's microchip battery dies or malfunctions?

Modern microchips are passive (they don't have batteries), so this isn't a concern. However, if a microchip fails to register, the door defaults to locked, which means your cat can't get through. You'd need to reprogram the door to recognize a replacement microchip via your vet.

Can you program multiple cat microchips into one door?

Absolutely—the door stores up to 32 microchip IDs. Programming is manual (you'll hold each cat near the sensor and press the 'learn' button), so adding multiple cats takes about 5 minutes total. The 500+ verified reviews often mention households with 2-3 cats using this successfully.

Is this weather-resistant for outdoor mounting?

It's built to handle moderate weather, but it's not military-grade weatherproofing. Heavy rain or snow accumulation can affect the magnetic seal. In July heat or mild rain, it performs flawlessly. For extreme climates, consider a weatherproof vestibule around the installation area.
