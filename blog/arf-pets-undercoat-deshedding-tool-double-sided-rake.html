Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I use this deshedding tool?

During peak shedding season (spring and early summer), 2-3 times per week works well for double-coated breeds. Outside shedding season, once every 2-3 weeks maintains coat health. More frequent use won't hurt, but daily use is overkill and unnecessary for most dogs. Listen to your dog's coat—if you're pulling significant undercoat, you need the tool. If barely anything comes out, wait another week.

Will this tool work on my short-haired dog?

It depends. Short-haired breeds like Labs and Boxers have less dramatic undercoat, but many still benefit from seasonal deshedding. Use the fine side only on short coats—the coarse side might be too aggressive. Test on a small area first. If nothing comes out after a few passes, your dog probably doesn't need this tool and regular brushing is sufficient.

Is this better than a slicker brush or undercoat rake?

Different tools, different jobs. Slicker brushes detangle and smooth the topcoat. This deshedding rake targets the undercoat layer specifically. A slicker won't remove embedded undercoat the way this tool does. For serious shedding management, most groomers recommend using a slicker first to detangle, then switching to an undercoat rake like this one. The combination beats either tool alone.

Does this tool hurt my dog?

Not when used correctly. The tool shouldn't dig into skin or pull painfully—it should glide through the coat with slight resistance as it catches undercoat. If your dog is yelping or pulling away, you're either pressing too hard, using it on matted areas, or working against the grain. Always brush in the direction fur naturally grows. Most dogs actually enjoy the sensation once they adjust to the tool.
