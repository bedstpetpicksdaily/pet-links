Frequently Asked Questions

Will my cat actually use an elevated feeder if they've always eaten from the floor?

Most cats adjust within three to five days. The key is placing the feeder in the same spot where they already eat and leaving it there consistently. If your cat is extremely resistant, try raising the bowl height gradually using books or a different stand first to test their willingness before investing in a fixed elevated station. Older cats and cats with joint issues typically adapt fastest because the comfort improvement is immediate.

How often do you need to replace the stainless steel bowls?

Properly maintained stainless steel bowls last indefinitely. The Whisker City bowls don't scratch easily, don't hold odors, and don't chip like ceramic. What matters is rinsing them daily and not leaving wet food sitting in them for hours. We've seen owner reports of the same bowls lasting five-plus years without degradation. The stand itself is the lasting component—bowls are just bonuses.

Is this better than cheaper plastic elevated feeders on Amazon?

Yes, with significant caveats. Plastic feeders are lighter, cheaper ($15-25), and take up less space. But they retain odors after 2-3 months, can crack if your cat is rough with meals, and many owners report they slide around on hard floors despite non-slip claims. The Whisker City stands cost roughly 2x as much but last 5-10x longer. If cost is the only factor, go plastic. If you'll be annoyed by replacing it in two years or dealing with bowl-sliding, spend the extra $20-30 now.

Is July a good time to buy this for my cat?

July is actually ideal. Cats eat less during hot months and are more finicky about food, so optimizing their eating position and bowl cleanliness becomes more important. Additionally, summer is when elevated feeders sell out on Amazon due to seasonal pet care spending, so buying now means inventory is still solid. If you wait until August or September, price fluctuations and stock issues become more likely.
