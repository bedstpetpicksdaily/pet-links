Understanding Summer Heat Stress in Dogs

Summer heat affects dogs differently than other seasons. Their bodies work overtime to regulate temperature, which increases their caloric needs and puts stress on their digestive systems. Dogs can't sweat like humans do, so they rely on panting and internal metabolic processes to cool down. During this time, their nutritional requirements shift—they need easily digestible proteins, proper hydration support, and antioxidants to combat heat-related inflammation. Understanding these changes is the first step toward keeping your dog healthy and comfortable when temperatures rise.

Feeding Schedule and Portion Tips for Summer

Adjust your feeding schedule during summer months to capitalize on cooler parts of the day. Feed your dog early in the morning or late in the evening when temperatures are lower, and their digestive system is less stressed. Portion sizes may need adjustment as dogs typically become less active in extreme heat—consult the guidelines on Nextrition Pet's packaging for heat-adjusted portions. Consider serving their cold-pressed food slightly chilled or mixing it with water to create a more hydrating meal. Avoid feeding large meals during peak heat hours, and instead offer smaller, more frequent meals that are easier to digest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should my dog drink during summer?

Dogs should have access to fresh water at all times during summer. Most dogs need 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily, but this increases in heat. Watch for signs of dehydration like excessive panting, dry gums, and lethargy, and always provide water during walks.

Is cold-pressed dog food better than kibble for summer?

Cold-pressed food is processed at lower temperatures, preserving nutrients, enzymes, and probiotics that support digestion—especially important during heat stress. Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed formulas are easier to digest and provide superior nutrient absorption compared to traditional high-heat kibble.

Can I give my dog frozen treats during summer?

Yes, frozen dog treats can help cool your dog down, but ensure they're made from healthy ingredients. Consider Nextrition Pet's nutritious dog treats frozen as a refreshing summer option that provides nutrition without excess calories.

Should I change my dog's diet during summer?

While you don't need to switch foods entirely, consider transitioning to Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed, gut-healthy dog food for better digestibility during summer. Adjust portion sizes downward as dogs are typically less active in heat, and add hydrating elements like water or broth to meals.
