Frequently Asked Questions

How long will my pet actually stay occupied with these puzzle feeders?

Initial testing shows 15-30 minutes of sustained engagement for both Cheerble versions and Heydoggie. However, this assumes your pet hasn't already mastered the difficulty level. Fast learners (especially cats) can solve beginner configurations within 2-3 days, which is why both Cheerble products include multiple difficulty settings. Without rotating difficulty, engagement drops significantly after the first week.

Are these puzzle feeders durable enough for aggressive or power chewers?

All three products use durable materials that withstand months of regular paw contact and moderate chewing. However, 'durable' doesn't mean 'indestructible.' Heavy chewers and power-jaw dogs can still cause cracking or damage. If your dog consistently destroys toys, verify product reviews specifically mentioning your dog's chewing intensity before purchasing. Heydoggie and Cheerble both have user reviews commenting on durability—check breed-specific feedback.

Can I use wet food in these, or just kibble and dry treats?

Heydoggie explicitly accommodates both wet food and kibble in its compartments. Cheerble products work better with dry kibble and treats; wet food leaves residue in compartments that's harder to clean and can harbor bacteria. If you prioritize wet food feeding, Heydoggie is the better choice. Otherwise, stick with kibble or freeze-dried treats in Cheerble products to simplify cleaning.

How do I choose between Cheerble and Heydoggie for my dog?

Choose Cheerble if your primary goal is mental stimulation and you want a toy that keeps your dog engaged during work hours. Choose Heydoggie if your dog eats too fast and you want to combine slow-feeding (which vets recommend) with enrichment. Cheerble delivers longer engagement windows (20-30 min vs. feeding-based interaction); Heydoggie delivers digestive and satiety benefits. Both have adjustable difficulty, but Cheerble's is more granular.

Will my cat actually figure out how to use a puzzle feeder, or will it just sit there?

Cats typically figure out basic Cheerble Sniff Puzzle Board configurations within 2-3 days, and testing shows even senior cats engage with it—they don't just ignore it. The sniff-and-hunt mechanic is more intuitive for cats than peg-based designs. However, extremely disengaged or older cats may need initial guidance (placing a treat partially visible to show the mechanic). Once they understand the concept, they actively problem-solve rather than giving up.

Do I need to rotate difficulty levels, or can I just set it once?

You should rotate difficulty actively if you want sustained engagement beyond 2-3 weeks. Both Cheerble products include multiple difficulty configurations for this reason. If you set the puzzle once and never adjust it, your pet will master it and lose interest. The rotation requirement means these aren't truly 'set and forget' enrichment—but the payoff is sustained engagement over months rather than weeks.

The Cheerble dog version prioritizes compartment complexity and extended engagement through non-obvious puzzle mechanics (20-30 min engagement). The cat version uses sniff-and-hunt mechanics with sliding pieces that tap into prey drive (15-30 min engagement). Both have multiple difficulty levels, but the dog version focuses on problem-solving complexity while the cat version focuses on natural hunting behavior. Choose based on your pet's species and behavioral needs.
