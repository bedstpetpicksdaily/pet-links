What to Look For

  • Real-time tracking with minimal lag: Huskies can cover miles in minutes. You need a tracker that updates location every 5-30 seconds, not every few minutes. This real-time capability is crucial for intercepting an escape before your husky reaches a busy road or gets too far to find.
  • Extended battery life and durability: Huskies are rough on gear. Look for a tracker with at least 5-7 days of battery life per charge, a waterproof design, and a sturdy collar attachment. Your husky will dig, roll in mud, and swim—the tracker needs to survive that lifestyle.
  • Reliable signal in rural and varied terrain: Many huskies escape to explore wooded areas or neighborhoods far from cellular towers. Choose a tracker that uses GPS + cellular or multi-network technology (not WiFi-only), so it works in remote areas where your husky might roam.
