Frequently Asked Questions

Will this toy actually survive my aggressive chewer, or is that just marketing?

It's genuinely durable, but 'extreme' has limits. Dogs that shred toys in minutes will eventually damage any rubber toy—Kong Extreme just takes weeks instead of hours. The black rubber is noticeably tougher than standard Kong toys, and hundreds of verified reviews confirm it outlasts competing brands significantly. That said, it's not indestructible. Expect 2-4 weeks of heavy chewing before minor wear appears, with the toy remaining safe and functional for much longer.

What's the difference between Kong Extreme Black and Kong Classic Red?

The black rubber compound is denser and puncture-resistant compared to the softer red rubber. Kong Classic is better for moderate chewers and lighter play; Kong Extreme is specifically engineered for dogs that view toy destruction as a mission. If you're considering this toy, your dog probably isn't happy with Kong Classic—that's the real distinction. The black version costs roughly the same but lasts significantly longer for aggressive chewers.

Can I stuff it with regular peanut butter, or do I need Kong-brand products?

Regular peanut butter works fine, though Kong Stuff'N paste spreads more easily and stays inside better during freezing. However, check your peanut butter for xylitol, which is toxic to dogs. Natural peanut butter without added sweeteners is safe. Many owners freeze it with chicken broth, wet dog food, or yogurt for variety. The hollow design is versatile—you're not locked into Kong brand products, though their paste is convenient if you use it regularly.
