Saint Bernards are magnificent dogs with thick, double coats that shed consistently throughout the year—and heavily during spring and fall. Finding the right brush makes the difference between endless fur tumbleweeds and a manageable grooming routine.

What to Look For

  • Double-coat capability: Your brush must penetrate both the outer guard hairs and the dense undercoat where most shedding originates. Single-layer brushes will only skim the surface and won't address the real problem.
  • Durable construction: Saint Bernards are large dogs with thick, coarse fur that demands heavy-duty tools. Look for stainless steel or reinforced materials that won't bend, break, or lose effectiveness after a few sessions.
  • Comfort for frequent use: Since year-round shedding means you'll be brushing 2-3 times weekly, choose a tool with an ergonomic handle and design that reduces hand fatigue and makes the job feel less like a chore.

Why This Works for Saint Bernards with Year-Round Shedding

Saint Bernards have one of the thickest double coats in the dog world, with an undercoat that's constantly cycling through growth and shedding phases. A standard slicker brush or comb simply cannot reach this undercoat effectively—you'd need to brush for hours to make a real difference. A deshedding rake removes 80-90% of loose undercoat in a single session, which is why professional groomers rely on these tools for heavy-shedding breeds. What to Avoid

  • Slicker brushes alone: While slicker brushes are great for finishing work, they won't tackle Saint Bernard undercoat shedding effectively on their own. They tend to only brush the outer coat, leaving the problematic loose undercoat behind.
  • Cheap metal rakes: Discount deshedding tools often have dull, inflexible teeth that bend under pressure or pull rather than cut through thick fur. They'll frustrate you and potentially irritate your dog's skin. Invest in a quality rake.
  • Furminator-type tools with serrated blades on sensitive skin: While these tools work, they can sometimes cut or strip hair if you're not extremely careful with a Saint Bernard's gentle temperament. Choose a rake with blunt, rounded teeth instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I brush my dog?

Short-haired dogs benefit from brushing 1-2 times per week. Long-haired and double-coated breeds should be brushed daily during shedding seasons and 3-4 times per week otherwise.

Do self-cleaning slicker brushes work?

Yes. The retractable bristle mechanism makes it easy to remove collected fur with a single button press — no more picking hair out of the bristles by hand.

Can I use a dog brush on cats?

Many slicker brushes are marketed for both dogs and cats. However, cats have more sensitive skin, so choose a brush with softer, more flexible bristles for feline use.

What is the best brush for a shedding dog?

Self-cleaning slicker brushes excel at removing loose fur from the topcoat. For undercoat shedding, combine with an undercoat rake or deshedding tool for best results.
