Winter Pet Care: What the Sandton SPCA Wants You to Know

As temperatures drop across South Africa, pet owners face new challenges in keeping their furry companions healthy and comfortable. The Sandton SPCA's recent winter care guidance reminds us that seasonal changes require thoughtful adjustments to our pet care routines.

Why This Matters

Winter isn't just uncomfortable for pets—it can be genuinely dangerous. Cold weather increases risks of hypothermia, dry skin, joint stiffness, and reduced mobility in older animals. Indoor heating can cause dehydration, while icy conditions pose slipping hazards. Pet owners who proactively prepare for winter can prevent health emergencies and ensure their animals stay comfortable throughout the season.

What Pet Owners Should Know

  • Limit outdoor exposure: Pets lose body heat quickly in winter. Short potty breaks are safer than extended outdoor play.
  • Protect paws: Ice, snow, and salt can damage sensitive paw pads. Wipe paws after outdoor time and consider protective booties.
  • Maintain hydration: Heated homes dry out the air. Ensure fresh water is always available.
  • Adjust exercise routines: Cold, dark mornings mean modified activity schedules. Indoor play becomes essential.
  • Watch for signs of discomfort: Reluctance to go outside, excessive shaking, or limping indicate your pet is struggling with the cold.
  • Keep shelter warm: Whether indoor or outdoor, pets need cozy, insulated spaces away from drafts.

Bottom Line

Winter care isn't complicated—it's about being intentional with small adjustments that make a big difference in your pet's comfort and safety. By following these guidelines and investing in practical winter gear, you'll help your furry friend thrive even when temperatures drop.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I keep my dog warm in winter in Johannesburg?

Keep your dog indoors during cold Highveld nights and provide a warm, dry sleeping area away from drafts, elevated off the cold floor with a blanket or pet bed. Sandton SPCA recommends that short-haired and small breed dogs wear a dog coat when going outside in winter. Always ensure your pet has access to fresh, unfrozen water as dehydration is still a risk in cold weather.

Should I change my pet's diet in winter?

Pets that spend time outdoors in winter may need slightly more food to help maintain body heat and energy levels, so consult your vet about adjusting portion sizes. However, indoor pets that are less active in winter may actually need fewer calories to avoid weight gain. Always ensure food and water bowls are checked regularly, as water can become cold very quickly during Johannesburg winters.

How do I protect my outdoor cat from the cold in winter?

The Sandton SPCA strongly advises bringing cats indoors at night during winter, as temperatures on the Highveld can drop to near freezing. If your cat must stay outside, provide an insulated shelter that is off the ground, waterproof, and filled with dry straw or blankets to retain body heat. Check warm engine bays and car hoods before starting your vehicle, as cats often shelter in these dangerous spots during cold weather.

What are the signs that my pet is too cold?

Signs that your pet is dangerously cold include shivering, lethargy, weakness, shallow breathing, and seeking warm places to curl up constantly. Hypothermia is a real risk for pets left outside overnight in Gauteng winters, especially for puppies, kittens, elderly animals, and short-haired breeds. If your pet shows these symptoms, warm them gradually with blankets and contact your vet or the Sandton SPCA immediately for guidance.
