Winter Pet Care: What the Sandton SPCA Wants You to Know

As temperatures drop across South Africa, pet owners face new challenges in keeping their furry companions healthy and comfortable. The Sandton SPCA's recent winter care guidance reminds us that seasonal changes require thoughtful adjustments to our pet care routines.

Why This Matters

Winter isn't just uncomfortable for pets—it can be genuinely dangerous. Cold weather increases risks of hypothermia, dry skin, joint stiffness, and reduced mobility in older animals. Indoor heating can cause dehydration, while icy conditions pose slipping hazards. Pet owners who proactively prepare for winter can prevent health emergencies and ensure their animals stay comfortable throughout the season.

What Pet Owners Should Know

  • Limit outdoor exposure: Pets lose body heat quickly in winter. Short potty breaks are safer than extended outdoor play.
  • Protect paws: Ice, snow, and salt can damage sensitive paw pads. Wipe paws after outdoor time and consider protective booties.
  • Maintain hydration: Heated homes dry out the air. Ensure fresh water is always available.
  • Adjust exercise routines: Cold, dark mornings mean modified activity schedules. Indoor play becomes essential.
  • Watch for signs of discomfort: Reluctance to go outside, excessive shaking, or limping indicate your pet is struggling with the cold.
  • Keep shelter warm: Whether indoor or outdoor, pets need cozy, insulated spaces away from drafts.
