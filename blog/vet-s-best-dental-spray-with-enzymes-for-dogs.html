Frequently Asked Questions

How does Vet's Best Dental Spray compare to dental chews or treats?

Completely different approach. Dental chews (like Greenies or Virbac C.E.T.) rely on mechanical abrasion and texture to reduce plaque buildup over time, while this spray uses enzymes to target bacteria directly. Chews work better for dogs who tolerate them and don't have swallowing restrictions. The spray wins for senior dogs, dogs with sensitive teeth, or puppies too young for rigid chews. Many veterinarians recommend using both together for maximum coverage—spray for daily enzyme support, chews for mechanical action 2-3 times weekly.

Will this spray replace professional dental cleanings?

No. Full stop. Tartar buildup and periodontal disease require veterinary scaling under anesthesia. This spray maintains teeth between cleanings and slows tartar formation, but it cannot remove existing hard deposits. Think of it like mouthwash for humans—helpful for oral health, useless against established cavities or gum disease. Use it as preventative maintenance, not treatment.
