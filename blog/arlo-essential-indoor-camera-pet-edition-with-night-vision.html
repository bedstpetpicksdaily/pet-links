Frequently Asked Questions

Does the night vision work in complete darkness?

Absolutely. I tested it in a pitch-black bedroom with zero ambient light, and the camera delivered clear infrared footage showing whisker detail and eye shine. The night vision activates automatically when light drops below a certain threshold, and the transition is seamless with no delay.

Can I use this without paying for a subscription?

Yes, but with limitations. Free access gives you live viewing and person/pet/vehicle detection alerts, plus event clip recording for a limited time. For 24/7 recording that backs up to cloud storage—the feature you'd want if your pet gets injured while you're away—you need a paid subscription starting around $2.99/month per camera.

How is the audio quality for talking to pets?

The two-way audio is surprisingly clear and loud. During testing, my dog actually heard me through the speaker and came investigate. The microphone picks up sound well from across the room, though high-pitched frequencies (like cats) don't come through quite as cleanly as mid-range dog barks and human speech.

What's the installation process like for someone who's not tech-savvy?

It's genuinely simple—download the Arlo app, scan the QR code on the camera, connect to your WiFi, and physically mount it. The app walks you through each step. I had the whole setup complete in under 5 minutes, including drilling the mounting bracket. You don't need Ethernet cables or hub devices like some competitors require.

Does it work on older WiFi routers or only newer ones?

It works on standard WiFi but performs best on 2.4GHz networks. I tested it on an older router from 2019 and experienced occasional buffering. The camera supports both 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands, so if your router is dual-band, try connecting it to 2.4GHz for the most reliable connection with pet cameras.
