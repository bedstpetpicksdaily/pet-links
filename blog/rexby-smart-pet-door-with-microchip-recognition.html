Frequently Asked Questions

Will the Rexby door work with my pet's existing microchip?

Yes, if your pet was already microchipped (which most adopted animals are), the Rexby recognizes standard 125kHz and 134.2kHz microchips without additional hardware. You don't need to buy a collar tag or new microchip. Registration is simple—scan your pet's microchip with the door's built-in scanner during setup, and it stores the ID. This is one of the biggest advantages over cheaper doors that require visual collar tags.

Does the smart app actually work reliably, or will I lose connection constantly?

It depends on your WiFi strength. Reviewers in homes with strong 5GHz WiFi networks report zero issues. Those with weak signals or 2.4GHz-only networks experience frequent dropouts (roughly 1-3 disconnects per week based on negative reviews). If your router is near the pet door entrance, you'll be fine. If the door is on the opposite end of your home, expect frustration. The door functions manually when disconnected, but the app convenience disappears—which is partly why you're paying the premium.

Is Rexby worth $250+ compared to a $100 SureFlap door?

Honest answer: not for everyone. SureFlap's classic microchip door does the core job (selective pet access) just as well and costs one-third the price. Rexby's extras (app alerts when pets enter/exit, remote locking, adjustable access schedules) appeal to remote workers, pet parents who travel, or those managing multiple pets with different feeding times. If you need only basic exclusion (keep raccoons out, let your cat in), save the $150 and buy SureFlap. If you want real-time notifications and remote access, Rexby's premium is defensible—barely.
