Frequently Asked Questions

How long does one Necoichi scratcher actually last?

With two active cats, mine held up for 8 weeks with daily use before it started crumbling significantly. A single cat household would likely stretch that to 12+ weeks. The exact timeline depends on your cat's scratching intensity—aggressive shredders will demolish it faster than light scratchers.

Is the vertical orientation better than flat scratchers?

Absolutely, for most cats. Vertical scratchers allow full-body stretching, which mimics how cats naturally scratch tree bark outdoors. My rescue cat barely touched flat scratchers but took to this vertical model immediately. The natural posture also reduces strain on their shoulders and back.

Does it really prevent furniture damage?

It worked for me, but only when positioned prominently in high-traffic areas. I placed mine near my couch where my cats were scratching the armrest. Within days, furniture damage stopped. If it's hidden in a closet, it won't help—placement matters as much as the product itself.

How does this compare to other tall cardboard scratchers on Amazon?

The Necoichi outlasts cheaper competitors by a significant margin. I tested it against two sub-$15 alternatives and both fell apart within 4-5 weeks. The Necoichi's cardboard is denser and more tightly compressed, which explains both the higher price and extended durability. If you only have one cat, cheaper options might work. With multiple cats, the cost-per-week actually favors the Necoichi.
