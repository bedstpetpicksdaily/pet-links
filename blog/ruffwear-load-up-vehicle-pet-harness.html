Frequently Asked Questions

Does this harness work in every car?

Not perfectly in every configuration. The Load Up requires both a seat belt anchor point and ideally a floor attachment, which older vehicles sometimes lack or position awkwardly. Check your vehicle's manual for seat belt anchor locations before buying. That said, most standard sedans, SUVs, and trucks manufactured since 2010 will work fine.

Can my dog move around in the harness while driving?

Limited movement is intentional—that's the whole point of restraint during vehicle motion. Your dog gets enough slack to shift position slightly and won't be painfully restricted, but they won't be able to roam the entire back seat or jump between front and back. If your dog demands complete freedom while driving, this harness will frustrate both of you.

How does the Load Up compare to harnesses half the price?

Cheaper harnesses often use single-point attachment or have lower-quality webbing that degrades after 18-24 months of regular use. The Load Up's multi-point system and heavy-duty materials will likely outlast two cheaper alternatives. The question isn't whether you're getting more features—you're getting durability and engineering that actually distributes crash forces. That costs more, and there's a real difference in longevity and safety performance.

Is this harness comfortable for long road trips?

Yes, with the caveat that 'long' means 2-4 hours reliably. Beyond that, any harness will cause some discomfort because your dog is stationary and restrained. Plan to stop every 2-3 hours, let your dog walk and stretch, then reassess fit before continuing. The Load Up's padding and distribution help with comfort, but it's not a solution for all-day driving without breaks.
