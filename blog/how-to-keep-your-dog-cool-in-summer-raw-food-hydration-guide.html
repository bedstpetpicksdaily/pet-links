The Benefits of Raw Food for Summer Dog Health

Raw food diets are naturally high in moisture content, making them ideal for keeping dogs hydrated during hot months. Unlike processed kibble that can contain as little as 10% moisture, Smallbatch Pets' frozen raw meals retain up to 70% water content, providing essential hydration with every bite. This moisture-rich approach supports kidney function, promotes healthy digestion, and helps regulate body temperature naturally. Additionally, raw diets are rich in bioavailable nutrients and enzymes that support your dog's immune system during seasonal stress.

Freeze-dried raw food from Smallbatch Pets offers another excellent summer option. These products maintain the nutritional integrity of fresh raw meals while being lightweight and convenient for travel. Whether you're heading to the beach or camping, freeze-dried portions can be easily rehydrated with cool water or bone broth, giving your dog the benefits of raw nutrition on the go.

Hydration Strategies Beyond Water Bowls

While fresh water should always be available, going beyond basic hydration can significantly improve your dog's summer comfort. Smallbatch Pets bone broth is an excellent addition to your summer hydration strategy. Rich in collagen, amino acids, and electrolytes, bone broth encourages dogs to drink more while providing joint support and digestive health benefits. You can serve it chilled as a refreshing treat or pour it over raw meals for added flavor and nutrition.

Consider incorporating Smallbatch Pets lightly cooked meals as well, which offer a middle ground between raw and traditional feeding. These meals maintain high moisture content while providing extra digestibility for sensitive dogs. Mix in cooling ingredients like pumpkin or green beans, and serve slightly chilled for maximum summer appeal.

Practical Summer Feeding Tips with Raw Food

When feeding raw food during summer, timing matters. Feed your dog during cooler parts of the day—early morning or evening—to avoid adding internal heat during peak temperatures. Store Smallbatch Pets frozen raw meals in a quality freezer and thaw portions in the refrigerator rather than at room temperature to maintain food safety and freshness. Consider serving meals in smaller, more frequent portions to aid digestion and prevent overheating from large meals. Always ensure your dog has access to fresh water before, during, and after meals, especially when feeding protein-rich raw diets that require adequate hydration for optimal digestion.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw food safe to feed during hot summer months?

Yes, raw food is safe when handled properly. Store frozen meals at 0°F or below, thaw in the refrigerator, and serve within 2-3 days. The high moisture content actually supports your dog's hydration needs during summer. Smallbatch Pets maintains strict food safety standards for all their frozen raw products.

How much water should my dog drink in summer?

Dogs typically need 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily, but this increases in hot weather. With moisture-rich raw food diets, your dog gains additional hydration through meals. Always provide fresh, cool water and consider adding Smallbatch Pets bone broth to encourage drinking.

Can I mix raw food with other supplements for summer cooling?

Absolutely. Smallbatch Pets raw meals pair well with cooling ingredients like watermelon, green beans, and their bone broth. Avoid feeding during peak heat hours, and consider serving meals slightly chilled. Consult your vet before adding new supplements to ensure nutritional balance.

What's the difference between Smallbatch Pets frozen and freeze-dried raw food?

Frozen raw meals contain full moisture and can be served immediately, while freeze-dried options are lightweight and portable, requiring rehydration with water or bone broth. Both maintain nutritional integrity—choose based on your lifestyle and storage capabilities.
