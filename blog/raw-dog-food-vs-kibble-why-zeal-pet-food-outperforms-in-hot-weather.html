Understanding Kibble's Limitations in Hot Weather

Traditional kibble, while convenient, presents significant challenges during hot weather. The manufacturing process removes up to 90% of moisture content, requiring dogs to consume large amounts of water to compensate. This increased water intake can stress the digestive system and leave pets feeling sluggish and dehydrated despite drinking frequently. Additionally, kibble's high carbohydrate content requires substantial digestive energy, generating internal heat that compounds the discomfort of warm climates.

During summer months, kibble-fed dogs often experience reduced appetite, lethargy, and digestive upset. The dense, dry food sits heavier in the stomach, diverting blood flow to digestion rather than cooling mechanisms. Pet owners frequently notice their kibble-fed dogs panting excessively and avoiding activity—signs of thermal stress that shouldn't be ignored.

The Raw Dog Food Advantage: Natural Hydration & Digestibility

Raw dog food, particularly Zeal Pet Food's premium formulations, operates on an entirely different nutritional principle. With naturally high moisture content (60-70%), raw diets provide hydration at the cellular level while dramatically reducing digestive strain. This moisture-rich nutrition means your dog's body doesn't need to work as hard to extract nutrients, generating less metabolic heat in the process.

Zeal's raw dog food contains whole prey models and muscle meat that mirror what canines evolved eating. Without grains or unnecessary fillers, digestion becomes remarkably efficient—some dogs show 30% improvement in nutrient absorption within weeks of switching. The natural enzymes present in raw food remain intact (unlike kibble's high-heat processing), supporting superior gut health and immune function during stressful summer months.
