Frequently Asked Questions

Will this actually stop my dog from scratching, or does it just feel good in the moment?

Both. The oatmeal and aloe provide immediate relief during and after the bath—many owners report their dog stops scratching for 24-48 hours. However, this isn't a cure. It temporarily soothes inflamed skin, but if the allergy trigger (food, pollen, fleas) is still present, itching returns. Think of it as a reset button, not a solution.

How often can I use this shampoo safely?

Weekly is safe for most dogs without causing dryness or irritation—the gentle formula won't strip the natural oils like prescription medicated shampoos can. However, bathing more than twice weekly can actually worsen dry skin. Stick to once weekly during allergy season, and every 2-3 weeks when symptoms improve.

Does this work for all types of allergies?

No. This shampoo is best for itching from environmental allergies or mild skin irritation. Food allergies, yeast infections, and bacterial issues need different approaches—a vet can tell you which. Using this on a yeast infection alone will delay proper treatment. If itching is severe or your dog has ear infections alongside skin issues, see a vet first.

Why does the bottle seem to empty so fast?

The formula is concentrated, so you only need a small amount—but the 16oz size is genuinely small for large dogs or thick coats. A 16oz bottle typically lasts 4-6 baths depending on dog size. If you bathe your dog weekly in July and August, expect to buy 2-3 bottles per summer, which adds up to $20-35 total.

Is this better than cheaper grocery store dog shampoos?

Yes, for allergies specifically. Basic dog shampoos contain fragrance and detergents that can trigger more itching. Vet's Best uses oatmeal as the primary soothing ingredient rather than filler. The price difference ($8-12 vs. $3-5) is worth it during allergy season, though it's not necessary for healthy dogs without sensitivities.
