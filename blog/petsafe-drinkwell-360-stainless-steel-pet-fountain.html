Frequently Asked Questions

How much does the Drinkwell 360 actually cost compared to other stainless steel fountains?

Pricing varies on Amazon (check current rates via our link), but you're typically looking at $70-90 for the Drinkwell 360. A comparable stainless steel alternative might be $60-75, and basic plastic fountains run $25-40. The Drinkwell brand premium exists because of the 360-degree design patent and their reputation, but you could get 75% of the performance for 50% of the cost with a basic model.

Is stainless steel really better than plastic for pet fountains, or is it just marketing?

Stainless steel genuinely lasts longer—we're talking 3-5 years versus 1-2 years for plastic. Plastic gets scratched, absorbs odors permanently, and degrades in sunlight. But 'lasts longer' doesn't mean 'worth double the price' unless your fountain will actually last that long before your pet loses interest. If you're committing to fountain life long-term, stainless steel wins. If you're testing whether your pets will even use it, buy plastic first.

Does the 360-degree feature actually work, or is it just a gimmick?

It actually works if you have multiple pets with different drinking preferences. Some cats prefer to drink from the side stream, others from the top. Multiple access points reduce competition and encourage drinking in pets that are picky about their water access angle. However, if you have one pet, a standard fountain with a single spout accomplishes the same goal cheaper. The 360 design is best for multi-pet households where conflicts over water access happen regularly.

How often do you need to clean it, and is stainless steel easier to maintain?

Plan on cleaning it 2-3 times per week, same as any fountain. Stainless steel is easier to wipe down and doesn't stain, but mineral deposits and algae still build up in the basin and pump mechanism. The seams on the stainless steel version can actually trap more debris than seamless plastic designs, so 'easier maintenance' is partially a myth. If hard water is your problem, invest in a better filter system rather than assuming material choice fixes it.

Will my pet actually use a fountain, or is it a waste of money?

This is the real risk nobody talks about. About 60-70% of pets naturally prefer running water and will use a fountain more than a bowl. The rest ignore it completely. Before dropping $70+ on a Drinkwell 360, test with a $30-40 plastic fountain first. If your pet uses it daily for two weeks, upgrade to the nicer model. If they ignore it after a week, you've only lost $30-40 instead of $80.
