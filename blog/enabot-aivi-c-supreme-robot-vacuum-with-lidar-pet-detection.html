Frequently Asked Questions

Does it actually work on thick pet hair without getting tangled?

Yes, more reliably than budget models. The brush design includes anti-tangle features that reduce hair wrapping on the roller. That said, you'll still need to clean it weekly if you have a heavy shedder—no robot vacuum eliminates this completely. Monthly deep cleans with scissors are standard maintenance.

How long does the battery last on a full charge?

Approximately 110-120 minutes, which covers most average homes in one pass. If your home is larger than 2,000 sq ft, it may need to dock and recharge mid-cycle, then resume the map. This isn't a dealbreaker, but it's worth knowing before you commit.

Is pet detection actually different from regular motion sensors?

Yes. Traditional sensors just detect movement; pet detection uses image recognition to identify the size and shape of pets specifically. This means it won't scare your dog by chasing it, and it won't waste battery time reacting to every shadow. Pet owners report noticeably less stressed animals with this feature active.

What about noise level? Will it stress my anxious dog?

It runs at approximately 68-72 dB, which is about as loud as a regular conversation. Not silent, but quieter than upright vacuums (80+ dB). Anxious pets will still notice it, but the pet detection feature prevents it from actively hunting them down, which reduces overall stress.

Is this worth the money compared to the Roomba j7+ or Shark models?

Depends on your needs. Roomba j7+ costs $700+ but has better obstacle avoidance and app integration. Shark models around $300-350 lack lidar and pet detection but work fine on medium-shedding pets. The Enabot is the sweet spot if you want pet-specific features without flagship pricing, but it's not objectively better—just different.
