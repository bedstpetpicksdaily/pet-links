What Is the Best Slow Feeder Bowl for Pugs That Eat Too Fast?

A slow feeder bowl is exactly what it sounds like — a specially designed food dish with raised ridges, mazes, or puzzle-like patterns built into the base that force your dog to work around the obstacles to reach their kibble. Instead of vacuuming up an entire meal in 30 seconds flat, your pug has to slow down, use their nose, and eat at a pace that's actually safe for their little body. The best slow feeder bowl for pugs that eat too fast takes things a step further by being designed with brachycephalic breeds in mind, meaning shallow depth, wide surface area, and a flat-friendly layout that works with a pug's smushed snout rather than against it.

Pugs are a brachycephalic breed, which means their shortened airways and flat faces already put them at higher risk for breathing difficulties, bloat, and regurgitation. When they eat too fast, they swallow excessive air along with their food, which can lead to painful gas, vomiting, and in serious cases, a life-threatening condition called gastric dilatation-volvulus (GDV). A properly designed slow feeder bowl addresses all of this by naturally pacing your pug's eating without any frustration or confusion on their end.
