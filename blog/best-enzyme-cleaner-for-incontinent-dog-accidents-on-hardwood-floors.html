    Frequently Asked Questions

    Will enzyme cleaner damage hardwood floors?

    Quality enzyme cleaners are safe for hardwood floors when applied correctly—use minimal liquid and dry the area immediately to prevent water damage. Avoid gel-based products and never soak the wood; spray the cleaner on a cloth first, then wipe the affected area. Always test on an inconspicuous spot before full application.

    What's the best enzyme cleaner for dog urine on wood floors?

    Look for enzyme cleaners specifically formulated for hardwood or sealed wood surfaces, such as Nature's Miracle Hard Surface Enzymatic Cleaner or Rocco & Roxie Professional Strength. These break down uric acid crystals that cause odors and staining while being gentle enough not to warp or discolor wood finishes.

    How long does enzyme cleaner take to work on dog accidents?

    Most enzyme cleaners need 10-15 minutes of contact time to break down urine compounds, though some require up to 24 hours for deep odor elimination. Check your product's instructions and allow it to sit on the affected area without drying out completely for best results.

    Can enzyme cleaner remove old dog urine stains from hardwood?

    Enzyme cleaners work best on fresh accidents, but can help reduce odors from old stains if urine hasn't permanently discolored the wood. For set-in stains, you may need multiple applications or consider refinishing the affected boards; enzyme cleaners alone won't restore the wood's original appearance if the stain has penetrated the finish.
