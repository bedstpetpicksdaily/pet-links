Frequently Asked Questions

How often do filters actually need replacing?

In soft water areas (like Pacific Northwest), filters last 3-4 months. In hard water (Midwest, Southwest), expect 4-6 weeks. The activated charcoal absorbs minerals and odors, not just physically blocks debris. When water starts smelling slightly 'off' despite clear appearance, it's time to change. I've found a calendar reminder more reliable than guessing. Most owners who complain about the product skipped this maintenance—the fountain works fine, the water just gets stale.

Will this work if my pet has never used a fountain before?

Yes, but acclimation takes 5-14 days for skeptical pets. Place their regular bowl beside it initially so they investigate without pressure. The sound and movement trigger curiosity in most cats within a week. Dogs catch on faster—they see flowing water as 'fresher' instinctively. If your pet absolutely refuses after two weeks, fountains genuinely might not be compatible with their behavior, but this is rare with the Platinum's accessible design. The curved lip height (2.5 inches from base to stream) works for pets 8 pounds and up without neck strain.

How noisy is the pump really compared to other fountains?

The Drinkwell Platinum runs quieter than Catit designs and roughly equal to PetDroid models. In a silent room, you'll notice it—a soft humming that most owners describe as 'white noise.' In normal household conditions (TV on, general activity), you won't hear it from across a room. If you're sound-sensitive or have an anxious pet, test in the return window first. The pump isn't adjustable, so this is a fixed variable, unlike some premium models offering flow control.
