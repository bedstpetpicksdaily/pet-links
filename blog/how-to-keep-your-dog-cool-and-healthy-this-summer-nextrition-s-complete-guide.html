Understanding Summer Heat Stress in Dogs

Dogs experience heat differently than humans. They have limited sweat glands and primarily cool themselves through panting, which becomes less effective during extreme heat. Certain breeds, particularly brachycephalic (flat-faced) dogs like Bulldogs and Pugs, are at higher risk of heat-related illness. Signs of heat stress include excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, and loss of appetite. Understanding these warning signs is the first step in protecting your pet during summer months.

Watch for excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, loss of appetite, vomiting, or collapse. If you notice these signs, move your dog to a cool area immediately and contact your veterinarian.
