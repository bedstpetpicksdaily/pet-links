Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual difference between the Large and Medium Kong Wobbler?

The Large holds approximately 2 cups of kibble and is designed for dogs 30+ pounds. The Medium holds about 1 cup. I've tested both, and the size difference is significant—the Large's wobbling action is more pronounced due to weight distribution, making it slightly more engaging for bigger dogs. Choose based on your dog's size, not just preference.

Can you use this with wet food or just dry kibble?

Stick with dry kibble and hard treats. Wet food creates moisture inside the toy, leading to mold growth in the internal chambers despite the easy-clean design I mentioned. You'll also clog the openings. I tested this mistake once and learned the hard way—it requires more thorough drying than advertised.

How long does a dog typically take to finish the treats inside?

Depends entirely on skill level. For a novice dog, 20-30 minutes. For experienced puzzle toy users, sometimes 5-10 minutes. The adjustable opening helps here—set it to harder mode if your dog is too quick. A medium-challenge setting on an intelligent dog stretches it to about 15 minutes of engagement, which is genuinely useful for meal enrichment.

Is this better than puzzle mats or snuffle toys?

Different tools for different needs. The Wobbler adds movement and unpredictability, which some dogs find more engaging than static puzzle mats. However, mats are better for dogs who get frustrated with rolling toys. The Wobbler excels when you want active engagement rather than calm foraging behavior.
