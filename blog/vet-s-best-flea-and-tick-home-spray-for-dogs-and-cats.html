Our Verdict

Vet's Best Flea and Tick Home Spray deserves its 4.3-star rating, but not because it matches the power of prescription or synthetic sprays. Frequently Asked Questions

Will this spray kill fleas on my pet, or is it just for the house?

House and furniture only. The label specifically directs you not to spray directly on pets. You need a separate topical treatment (Advantage, Simparica, etc.) for your dog or cat. This spray handles the environment—your couch, carpets, bedding—to break the flea life cycle. Using both together gives better results than either alone, but they're designed for different jobs.

How long does the smell last and will it bother my cat?

The cedarwood and lemongrass scent typically fades within 2-4 hours once dry, though you'll catch hints for a day or two in enclosed spaces. Cats have sensitive noses and may avoid sprayed areas initially, but it's not dangerous. Some customers note their cats tolerate it better than conventional chemical sprays. If your cat is extremely scent-sensitive, test on one small area first and ventilate well.

How often do I actually need to reapply during summer in July?

Most customers report reapplying every 7-10 days during peak flea season for active prevention, or every 2-3 weeks if your pets are already on monthly preventatives. This is significantly more frequent than synthetic sprays (which can last 30 days) or prescription flea treatments. Budget accordingly—you'll spend more on application frequency even though the per-bottle price is reasonable.

Does it work on ticks as well as fleas?

The formula targets both fleas and ticks, but customer reviews show more consistent results against fleas. Tick effectiveness depends on direct spray contact and immediate exposure. For established tick problems, especially in areas like tall grass or woods, this works better as a preventative than a cure. Prescription tick prevention (oral or topical) is more reliable if ticks are a major concern in your region.
