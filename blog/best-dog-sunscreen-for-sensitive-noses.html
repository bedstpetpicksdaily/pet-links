What to Look For

SPF 50+ Rating

SPF 50 blocks 98% of UVB rays, while lower SPF options are significantly less effective. For dogs with sensitive noses or light coloring, SPF 50+ is non-negotiable. Check that the formula is specifically labeled for pets—human sunscreen ingredients can be harmful if licked off.

Stick Format for Easy Application

Sunscreen sticks are ideal for sensitive noses because they minimize mess, prevent accidental ingestion, and are easy to reapply without wrestling your dog. The solid format also means no dripping during transport or warm weather, making it perfect for on-the-go protection during hikes and beach trips.

Zinc Oxide-Free & Dog-Safe Ingredients

Look for formulas that explicitly state they're free of zinc oxide, avobenzone, and octinoxate—all toxic to dogs if ingested. Safe alternatives include titanium dioxide and pet-approved UV filters. Check for added moisturizers like aloe or vitamin E, especially important for already-sensitive nasal skin.
