Frequently Asked Questions

Does the mapping actually work or is it marketing hype?

It works, but not instantly. The first two or three runs feel random, but by run four the vacuum creates an actual floor plan accessible through the app. You can set room-specific schedules after that. I tested this in a split-level home with different flooring types, and it successfully distinguished between carpet zones and hardwood areas within a week of use.

How often do I need to empty the dustbin with two dogs?

Plan on every 3-4 days if your dogs shed moderately, or every 2-3 days during heavy shedding seasons like spring and summer. The bin holds roughly 0.6 liters, which is smaller than some competitors. I set phone reminders rather than guessing, because an overfull bin reduces suction effectiveness—exactly when you need it most.

Will it get tangled in pet hair like other robot vacuums?

The dual brush design resists tangling better than single-brush models I've tested, but it's not immune. I'd recommend checking the brush weekly during heavy shedding periods and using a brush comb to clear wrapped hair. One tangle per month was my average during testing, versus two or three with cheaper models.

Is the price worth it for pet hair specifically?

At current price points, yes—but only compared to models in the same tier. You're paying for the mapping feature and consistent suction more than for pet-specific engineering. If you find it on sale or at a lower price point, the value proposition improves significantly. I'd compare it directly with three competitors before buying.
