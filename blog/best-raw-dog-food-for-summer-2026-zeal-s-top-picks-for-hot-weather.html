Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe during summer heat?

Yes, when handled properly. Zeal's freeze-dried raw food is processed for safety and should be stored in cool, dry conditions. Prepare meals fresh or refrigerate prepared portions, and avoid leaving raw food exposed to direct heat. Always provide fresh water and monitor your dog for any digestive changes during transitions.

How does Zeal's freeze-dried raw food differ from fresh raw?

Zeal's freeze-dried raw offers the nutritional benefits of fresh raw food with extended shelf stability and convenience. The freeze-drying process removes moisture while preserving enzymes and nutrients, eliminating the need for continuous refrigeration while maintaining superior nutritional value.

Can I transition my dog to Zeal raw food during summer?

Yes, but transition gradually over 7-10 days to allow your dog's digestive system to adjust. Mix increasing amounts of Zeal raw food with current food, reducing previous food proportionally. Monitor digestion, energy levels, and coat condition during transition. If issues arise, slow the process further.

What makes Zeal grain-free pet food better for summer?

Grain-free formulations reduce digestive bloating and inflammation, common concerns during hot weather. Zeal's grain-free recipes focus on high-quality proteins and balanced nutrients that support sustained energy and optimal body temperature regulation without inflammatory grain ingredients.

How often should I feed raw dog food in summer?

Most dogs thrive on once or twice-daily raw feeding, adjusted based on age, activity level, and individual metabolism. Zeal's balanced formulations provide complete nutrition, so follow feeding guidelines on packaging and adjust portions based on your dog's body condition and summer activity levels.
