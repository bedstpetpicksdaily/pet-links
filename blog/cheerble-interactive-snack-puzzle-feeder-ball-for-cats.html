Frequently Asked Questions

Will my cat actually use this, or will it sit in the corner unused?

That depends entirely on your cat's personality. Cats with high prey drive and curiosity tend to engage immediately. I've seen shy or lazy cats completely ignore it. The best predictor: does your cat play with other interactive toys? If yes, odds are high they'll use this. If your cat prefers lounging, this won't change their nature. Start with a few high-value treats to build initial interest, then gradually transition to regular kibble.

How much should I fill it, and what treats work best?

I fill mine about halfway to keep treats moving freely without overstuffing. Dry kibble works best—it rolls and disperses consistently. Small freeze-dried treats also work well. Avoid anything sticky, wet, or overly soft because it gets stuck and turns into a cleanup nightmare. For my cat, I use a mix of their regular kibble plus a few premium treats as motivation to actually engage with the toy.

Is this better than just feeding from a regular bowl?

Yes, if your cat eats too fast or needs mental stimulation. The puzzle aspect forces slower eating, which improves digestion and prevents vomiting. It also burns calories through play and keeps indoor cats mentally engaged—especially valuable during hot months when outdoor activity drops. However, if your cat already eats slowly and gets plenty of playtime, a regular bowl works fine. This is a tool for specific problems, not a universal upgrade.

How durable is it really, and how often do I need to replace it?

After three weeks of heavy daily use with an aggressive cat, mine shows no damage. The plastic feels reinforced and the seams are solid. That said, I've seen reviews mentioning cracks after months of use with particularly destructive cats. I'd estimate 6-12 months of lifespan with regular play, possibly longer if your cat plays with it casually. At the $12-18 price point, that's reasonable cost-per-month for enrichment.
