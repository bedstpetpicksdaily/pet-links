Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Dual-sided design with both a rake and de-shedding blade gives you flexibility for different coat densities without buying two separate tools. The blade side works particularly well on thicker undercoats (Labs, Golden Retrievers, German Shepherds), while the rake side handles more delicate work on mixed-coat breeds.
  • ✓ Ergonomic handle design reduces hand fatigue during longer grooming sessions—critical if you're working with a medium or large dog. Seven minutes of raking feels manageable; thirty minutes actually works without your wrist screaming.
  • ✓ Price point sits between $20–$35 depending on sales, making it accessible for casual groomers who don't want to drop $60+ on professional-grade alternatives like the FURminator or Kong ZoomGroom.
  • ✓ Works effectively on matted undercoat if you're consistent with maintenance. One owner with a Golden Retriever reported pulling out noticeably more loose hair than with a slicker brush alone—this is real-world value.
Cons
  • ✗ The handle can loosen over time with heavy use, especially if you're doing frequent full-body sessions. Several reviewers mentioned needing to retighten after 2–3 months of regular grooming.
  • ✗ It's mediocre on short-haired breeds (Boxers, Pit Bulls, short-coat Huskies). If your dog has a thin or single coat, you're paying for a feature you won't use, and a basic slicker brush often outperforms it here.

Our Verdict

The Arf Pets Undercoat Deshedding Tool is worth buying if you own a double-coated or thick-furred dog and groom at home 1–2 times weekly. At $20–$35, it's solid value for the dual-sided functionality and handles the job without being flimsy. However, it's not a professional-grade tool—your expectations should match the price. If you're grooming a short-haired breed, save your money. If you're grooming daily or have severely matted dogs, look at higher-tier options. For the middle ground (most dog owners), this tool justifies its cost through summer shedding season and beyond.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Arf Pets rake compare to the FURminator Undercoat Deshedding Tool?

The FURminator ($50–$70) has a more aggressive de-shedding blade and sturdier construction—noticeable if you're working with severely matted or thick undercoats daily. Arf Pets at half the price works well for maintenance-level grooming. If you groom 3+ times weekly or deal with professional-level matting, FURminator is the better investment. For casual grooming, Arf Pets delivers 80% of the performance at 40% of the cost.

Will this tool work on my short-haired dog?

Not really, and reviewers consistently report this. Short-haired breeds like Boxers or Beagles have minimal undercoat, so the rake side gets stuck and the blade side risks irritating skin. A rubber curry brush or basic slicker brush will outperform this on short coats. Save the Arf Pets for double-coated dogs (Corgis, Huskies, Labs).

Is the 4.3-star rating (500+ reviews) reliable for this product?

Yes, but context matters. Most 5-star reviews come from double-coat owners who groom regularly. The 3-star and below reviews almost always cite either loose handles or poor performance on short-haired dogs—predictable user mismatch. If you match the product to the right dog type, you're likely in the 4–5 star territory.

How often should I use this tool to see actual shedding reduction?

For visible results (less fur on furniture and clothes), you need consistency: once weekly minimum during shedding season, ideally twice weekly in July and August. One-time or sporadic use won't cut it. Dogs aren't like your car—one wash doesn't last six months. This tool requires a grooming commitment to justify its purchase.
