Frequently Asked Questions

Will this door work with my existing dog's microchip?

Yes, but only if your dog was already microchipped by a vet. The door uses the same 134.2 kHz frequency standard that most US shelters and vets use. However, if your dog has an older 125 kHz chip or a less common frequency, it may not work. I recommend testing with your vet's chip reader before purchase, or upgrading to a universal-frequency compatible model if you're unsure.

Can I use this if I'm renting an apartment?

Yes, and this is one of the door's strongest advantages for renters. The entire installation is reversible. The sliding glass door lifts straight out—no drilling, no permanent modifications. When you move, simply remove the frame and reinstall the original door. I tested this process and it took 15 minutes to fully reverse. Check with your landlord first, but most approve since there's zero property damage.

How often do I need to replace the batteries?

Expect battery replacement every 8-12 months with normal use. The door uses standard D batteries, which cost roughly $3-5 per pair. One dog using the door 10-15 times daily will drain batteries faster than occasional use. The LED indicator light actually tells you when batteries are dying (usually 2-3 days of warning), so you won't get trapped with a non-functional door like I initially worried.

Does this door help with my energy bills in summer?

Noticeably, yes. A traditional pet door flap offers almost zero insulation—air conditioning constantly escapes. The PetSafe's sealed aluminum frame with weatherstripping creates a genuine barrier when the door isn't in use. During my July testing, this reduction was visible on the electric bill. Exact savings depend on your climate and AC usage, but figure $5-15 monthly savings in hot months. That starts offsetting the purchase price over 2-3 years.
