Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best flea treatment for dogs in 2026?

The best flea treatments for dogs in 2026 include oral chewables like NexGard and Simparica, which kill fleas within hours and provide month-long protection, as well as topical options like Frontline Plus and K9 Advantix II for dogs that can't take oral medications. Newer extended-protection options like Bravecto offer up to 12 weeks of coverage in a single dose. The best choice depends on your dog's size, health conditions, and whether you also need tick or heartworm protection.

What kills fleas on dogs instantly?

Oral flea treatments like Capstar (nitenpyram) start killing adult fleas within 30 minutes and can eliminate nearly all fleas within 4 hours, making it the fastest-acting option available. Spinosad-based chewables like Comfortis also work quickly, typically within 30 minutes of administration. For immediate topical relief, a vet-approved flea shampoo can kill fleas on contact during bath time, though it provides no lasting protection.

Is it better to use a flea collar or drops for dogs?

Modern flea collars like Seresto offer up to 8 months of continuous protection and are a strong competitor to monthly topical drops like Frontline or Advantage II, making them a convenient long-term option. Topical drops, however, are often preferred for dogs that swim or bathe frequently, since excessive water exposure can reduce a collar's effectiveness. Oral treatments are generally considered more reliable than both, as they work systemically and aren't affected by bathing or weather.

How do I get rid of fleas on my dog if nothing seems to work?

If flea treatments aren't working, the most likely culprit is re-infestation from your home environment, since flea eggs and larvae can live in carpets, bedding, and furniture for months. You'll need to treat your dog and your entire home simultaneously using a household flea spray or fogger containing an insect growth regulator (IGR) to break the flea life cycle. Switching to a vet-prescribed oral treatment like Simparica Trio or Bravecto, which are harder for fleas to develop resistance to, can also make a significant difference.
