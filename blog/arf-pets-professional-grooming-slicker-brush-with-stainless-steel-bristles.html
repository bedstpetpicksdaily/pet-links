Frequently Asked Questions

How does the Arf Pets brush compare to the FURminator slicker brush?

The FURminator costs $5-8 more but has thicker wire bristles better suited for heavy shedding and dense coats. The Arf Pets is better for general maintenance and daily grooming on standard coat types. If your dog sheds heavily (Labs, Goldens, German Shepherds), the FURminator's bristle density wins. Will stainless steel bristles actually last longer than regular steel?

Yes, measurably so. Regular steel bristles oxidize and lose flexibility within 3-4 months with weekly grooming. Stainless steel resists this degradation for 12+ months under identical use. The real difference appears around month 3-4 when standard brushes start bending rather than gripping the coat. If you groom year-round, you'll replace a cheap brush 2-3 times before replacing stainless steel once.

Is this brush suitable for long-haired cats or just dogs?

Technically yes for cats with standard coat length (8-12 inches), but the bristle tension may be too firm for cats with delicate skin. Start slowly with very light pressure. For long-haired cats requiring frequent grooming, consider a cat-specific slicker with softer bristles. The Arf Pets works better for dogs or short-to-medium haired cats groomed occasionally.

What's the real difference between a slicker brush and other grooming tools?

Slicker brushes are designed for mat removal and undercoat work with tightly-packed short wires. Deshedding tools (like the FURminator) penetrate deeper to pull loose undercoat. Rakes have wider spacing for general smoothing. Use a slicker first to detangle, then a deshedding tool if needed. The Arf Pets slicker handles the first job well; pair it with a undercoat rake for complete summer shedding management.
