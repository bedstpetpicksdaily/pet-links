Frequently Asked Questions

How long does one toy actually last?

Expect 2-4 weeks with regular daily chewing for moderate chewers. Heavy chewers might get 7-10 days. The key variable is your dog's chewing intensity, not the toy's quality. Owners who report 6+ weeks typically have lighter chewers or rotate multiple toys to extend life.

Is this better than Kong Extreme or other premium brands?

They're different tools for different jobs. Kong Extreme is hollow (better for treats, worse for pure chewing), while Nylabone Power Chew is solid nylon. Nylabone typically lasts longer for pure chewers, but Kong wins if you want to stuff it with peanut butter or treats. Many owners buy both and rotate them to extend toy life.

Will it splinter and hurt my dog?

Not in the way cheap toys do. As it wears, you'll see nubs and roughness, but it doesn't create sharp splinters. However, once it's worn down to a nub (usually 4+ weeks in), swap it out rather than leaving it for months—not because it's dangerous, but because it loses its chewing value.

Does the bacon flavor matter, or is it just marketing?

The flavor genuinely affects engagement based on hundreds of owner reports. Dogs return to bacon-flavored toys more often than plain nylon. If your dog ignores plain toys after day two, the flavored version is worth the extra $2-3. If your dog chews anything, flavor matters less.

What size should I buy?

Size doesn't dramatically affect durability—the material does. But buy appropriate to your dog's mouth: too small is a choking risk, too large is awkward. For dogs under 25 lbs, start with the Regular. For 25-60 lbs, go XL. For 60+ lbs, try the XXL. The toy should fit comfortably in their mouth without being a finger-sized choking hazard.
