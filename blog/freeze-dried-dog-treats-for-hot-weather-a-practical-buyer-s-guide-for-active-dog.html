Frequently Asked Questions

Are freeze-dried treats safe for dogs during hot weather?

Yes. Freeze-dried treats are explicitly safer in hot weather than traditional options because they don't melt or spoil, and they're lightweight enough not to tax your dog's digestive system during activity. The freeze-drying process preserves nutrients while removing moisture, creating a shelf-stable product that maintains nutritional integrity without refrigeration.

How should I store freeze-dried treats during summer activities?

Freeze-dried treats don't require active refrigeration during outings, which is their key advantage. However, check expiration dates and storage requirements before purchasing. Bulk orders may arrive in large quantities requiring adequate freezer space at home. Once opened, store in a cool, dry place away from direct heat and humidity.

Can I give my dog freeze-dried treats during or immediately after exercise?

Wait 15-30 minutes after intense exercise before offering treats. This prevents potential digestive upset. Freeze-dried treats are lighter than traditional snacks, making them safer options during active periods, but timing around peak exertion still matters for any treat.

What's the difference between premium freeze-dried treats and standard freeze-dried options?

Premium brands like Smallbatch Pets emphasize whole-food ingredients, detailed sourcing, and minimal processing with no artificial additives. Standard freeze-dried treats still offer the same core benefits—shelf stability, portability, preserved nutrition—but may use more conventional ingredient sourcing. The choice depends on your dog's dietary needs and your budget.

How much freeze-dried treat should I give my dog daily?

Treats should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. Because freeze-dried treats are protein-dense and lightweight, they're easy to overfeed. Check the product's feeding guidelines based on your dog's weight, and account for them in overall daily nutrition planning.

Are freeze-dried treats suitable for all dog breeds and ages?

Generally yes, but check individual product guidelines. For puppies and senior dogs, verify that the treat size and hardness won't pose a choking risk or dental concern. Some freeze-dried products may become too hard if frozen too long, potentially damaging teeth, so storage duration matters.

Do freeze-dried treats help with hydration in hot weather?

No. While freeze-dried treats are safe during hot weather and won't contribute to overheating, they've had moisture removed, so they don't provide hydration. Always ensure your dog has access to fresh water during summer activities, regardless of treat choice.
