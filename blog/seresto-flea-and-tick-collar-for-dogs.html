Frequently Asked Questions

Is Seresto safe for puppies and senior dogs?

Seresto is labeled for dogs eight weeks and older. Senior dogs typically tolerate it fine, but always verify your specific dog's age and weight requirements on the product packaging. If your dog has heart issues, skin conditions, or takes medications, ask your vet first—don't assume a non-prescription product is automatically safe just because it's available on Amazon.

Will it work if my dog gets wet or swims?

Seresto is water-resistant, not waterproof. Light rain and occasional bathing won't destroy effectiveness immediately, but frequent swimming or weekly baths will degrade the collar's lifespan significantly. If your dog is a swimmer, expect the eight-month claim to shrink to four or five months in real conditions.

How does Seresto compare to monthly topical treatments in cost?

Over eight months, a single Seresto collar typically costs less than six to eight monthly treatments like Frontline or Advantage. The math works in Seresto's favor for price, but the topical treatments often show faster knockdown of active infestations and are easier to control if your dog has a reaction. Weigh convenience against the slight premium you'll pay per month with alternatives.
