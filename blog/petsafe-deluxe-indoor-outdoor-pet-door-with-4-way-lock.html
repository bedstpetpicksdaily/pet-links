Frequently Asked Questions

Will a large cat fit through the small size?

Most cats fit fine through small and medium sizes, even hefty ones around 15 pounds. The small is designed for cats specifically. I'd only upgrade to medium if you have a large dog under 25 pounds or a particularly chunky cat. Measure your pet's shoulder width at the widest point—the door flap needs at least 2 inches of clearance on each side.

How secure is the 4-way lock against determined escape artists?

The mechanical locking system is solid for standard pets. However, this isn't Fort Knox. A determined large dog could theoretically force the flaps open with sustained pressure, and clever cats can sometimes squeeze through if the frame sits slightly loose. For aggressive chewers, the plastic edges can degrade over 12+ months. It's secure against normal use and accidental exit, not against a pet actively trying to escape.

Does it help with heating and cooling costs?

Honestly? Marginally. The magnetic closure and flap system reduce air exchange compared to an open door, but this isn't weatherstripping. In summer, you'll notice less air escaping when pets go outside. In winter, draft is more noticeable. If your HVAC runs constantly, this won't transform your bills, but it's better than nothing. The real savings come from not propping open doors all day.

Is it loud or disruptive?

No. The flaps open and close silently. The only sound is occasionally the magnetic latch catching when the door swings. Pet owners with light sleepers or open-concept homes appreciate this—your cat won't wake you with door noise at 3 a.m. like they would with a hinged cat flap.
