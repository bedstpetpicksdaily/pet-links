What We Looked For in Anti-Chew Sprays

To identify the genuinely best anti-chew sprays for furniture, we evaluated dozens of products across four critical criteria:

  • Effectiveness: Does it actually deter chewing? We prioritized sprays with proven bitter compounds (like capsaicin or denatonium benzoate) backed by customer reviews showing real behavior change, not just temporary deterrence.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best anti chew spray for dogs on furniture?

Some of the most effective anti chew sprays for dog furniture include Grannick's Bitter Apple Spray, Fooey! Ultra-Bitter Training Aid, and Rocco & Roxie No Chew Spray, all of which use bitter-tasting ingredients to deter chewing. Bitter Apple is widely considered the gold standard and works for most dogs, though some pups may be unbothered by it. If one formula doesn't work, try switching to a stronger bitter formula like Fooey!, which uses a more intense bitter agent.

Is anti chew spray safe for dogs and furniture?

Most commercially available anti chew sprays are formulated to be non-toxic and safe for both dogs and household furniture when used as directed. However, you should always spot-test the spray on a hidden area of your furniture first, as some sprays can cause slight discoloration or staining on delicate fabrics and wood finishes. Look for sprays that are alcohol-free and made with natural ingredients if you have concerns about your dog's sensitivity.

How do I get my dog to stop chewing on furniture?

Anti chew spray is a great first step, but it works best when combined with providing your dog plenty of appropriate chew toys and ensuring they get enough physical and mental exercise. Consistently redirecting your dog to their chew toys when they approach furniture, and rewarding them for chewing the right items, reinforces good behavior over time. If the chewing is excessive or anxiety-driven, consult your veterinarian, as it may be a sign of separation anxiety or another underlying issue.

How long does anti chew spray last on furniture?

Most anti chew sprays need to be reapplied every 24 to 48 hours, especially on high-traffic areas or surfaces that get wiped down frequently. Some premium sprays claim to last up to a week, but consistent reapplication is key during the early training phase until your dog learns to avoid the furniture. Over time, as the behavior is corrected, you can reduce how often you apply the spray.
