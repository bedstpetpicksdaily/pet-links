The premium pet nutrition market has exploded in 2026, but the options aren't all the same. Cold-pressed dog food, raw frozen formulations, and high-end treat lines each address different nutritional philosophies and pet health goals. This guide compares the actual products available—including Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed options, Zeal Pet Food's raw formulations, and PetTreatery's natural treats—with honest pros and cons for each.

Rather than recommending a single "best" choice, we've evaluated these options across digestibility, ingredient transparency, storage requirements, cost, and suitability for different pet needs. Your choice depends on your pet's age, health status, your freezer space, and your budget.

Nextrition Pet Cold-Pressed Dog Food

Cold-pressing preserves more nutrients than high-heat extrusion while maintaining shelf stability and ease of storage. Cold-pressed options avoid the nutritional loss of conventional kibble production but don't require the freezer space, handling protocols, or temperature-controlled shipping that raw food demands.

Best for: Health-conscious owners committed to raw feeding philosophy, pets needing high bioavailability nutrition, owners with adequate freezer space and shipping logistics capability

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the actual difference between cold-pressed (Nextrition) and raw (Zeal) for my dog?

Cold-pressed uses lower heat than conventional kibble, preserving more nutrients while maintaining shelf stability—no freezer required. Raw provides maximum bioavailability by delivering whole, uncooked foods, but requires freezer storage, careful temperature management during shipping, and transition periods. Cold-pressed is easier logistically; raw is more nutrient-dense but more complex to manage.

Can I just use PetTreatery treats as my dog's primary nutrition?

No. PetTreatery treats are supplements, not complete nutrition. They're designed for training rewards, enrichment, and targeted health goals (allergies, seasonal cooling), but require a primary diet from another source (kibble, Nextrition cold-pressed, Zeal raw, etc.) to meet full nutritional needs.

Is raw pet food actually safe for summer feeding?

Yes, if temperature-controlled properly. Raw food's main risk isn't heat itself but improper storage and shipping. Zeal includes insulated packaging with ice packs for this reason. However, verify adequate freezer space at your home before ordering, and check best-by dates carefully—frozen warehouse stock may have limited remaining shelf life after delivery.

How do I know if premium treats/food are actually better, or just expensive packaging?

Compare actual ingredient lists (not marketing claims), check for artificial additives and fillers, and calculate price-per-ounce across brands. Premium options should list whole proteins first, avoid by-products, and explain their sourcing. If the ingredient quality doesn't justify the cost difference, you're paying for branding. Also watch for novel proteins in premium treats—they're beneficial for allergic pets but can trigger sensitivities in unexposed dogs.

Should I transition immediately to premium food, or gradually?

Always transition gradually, especially with raw or cold-pressed options. Mix 25% new food with 75% current food for 7 days, then shift proportions gradually over 2-3 weeks. This prevents digestive upset and lets you confirm your pet actually tolerates the new food before committing fully. This is especially important with novel proteins in premium treats.

Which option is best for a dog with food allergies?

All three brands address allergies through ingredient control: Nextrition cold-pressed and Zeal raw both offer limited-ingredient and novel-protein formulations; PetTreatery treats specialize in single-protein options. The advantage of treats is lower-risk testing—you can try a novel protein in treat form before committing to a full diet change. Always verify the ingredient list matches your dog's specific sensitivities before purchasing.
