Frequently Asked Questions

Does it actually work with microchips, or do I need to buy special collars?

It works with standard ISO microchips. No collars, no tags. If your pet is already chipped (most rescues and vets do this by default), you're good to go. The sensor reads the chip from about 6 inches away, so entry is fast. The only catch: microchips aren't programmed with location data, so you can't see *which* pet used the door from the chip alone—you need the app and biometric log for that.

How much does installation actually cost if I hire someone?

Expect $200-400 for professional installation depending on whether you're cutting through a door, wall, or using an existing door frame. This can nearly double your total investment. If DIY is possible in your home, you save significant money—the install itself takes 2-4 hours for someone moderately handy.

Is this worth it if I only have one pet?

Probably not. The dual-door feature is overkill, the app monitoring is nice but not essential, and you could get a basic microchip door for $100-150 that does the same access control job. The Hepper shines when you have multiple pets or strong concerns about stray animals. Single-pet households with reasonable neighborhood safety can skip the premium.

How long do the batteries last and what happens when they die?

Expect 4-8 months per battery set depending on door traffic. When batteries die, the door reverts to manual-only operation—it won't lock or unlock automatically, but your pet can still physically use it. You get low-battery warnings via the app, so you can replace them before that happens. Keep backup batteries on hand.

What's the real difference between this and a $100 microchip door?

The Hepper adds app connectivity, activity logging, and slightly better weatherproofing. A basic microchip door gives you the core function—microchip recognition that locks strangers out. Both keep stray cats out and let your cat in. The question is whether activity logs and remote monitoring justify the extra $200-300 to you. For most people, they don't.
