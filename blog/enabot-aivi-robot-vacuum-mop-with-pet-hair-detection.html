Frequently Asked Questions

How often do you actually need to empty the dustbin with multiple pets?

Real usage reports from owners with 2+ pets show 3-4 empties per week with standard weekly cleaning schedules. If you run daily cycles or have long-haired breeds (huskies, golden retrievers), expect every 2-3 days. The dustbin capacity is roughly half that of premium competitors, which is a meaningful trade-off if your selling point is "set it and forget it."

Does the pet hair detection actually reduce clogs, or is that marketing?

It genuinely reduces them. The increased suction during high-fur detection passes helps pull tangled hair before it wraps around the brush roller. However, you still need to clean the brush every 2-3 weeks in a multi-pet home—the detection prevents catastrophic blockages, not all maintenance. Think of it as damage reduction rather than elimination.

Is the mopping function worth using, or should I skip it?

Use it for maintenance between traditional mopping, not as a replacement. The water tank holds barely enough for 50-75% of an average home before needing refills. It's excellent for quick paw-print cleanups or daily dust-mop passes, but if you need deep wet mopping monthly, stick with a traditional mop. The hybrid function adds value for pet homes specifically because of frequent light cleaning needs.

How does it compare to the Roborock S7 or Shark AI at similar price points?

Roborock S7 has better mopping pressure and self-emptying options at higher tiers, but lacks pet-specific detection—you're paying more for fewer pet-relevant features. Shark AI undercuts the Aivi on price but strips the mopping entirely. The Aivi's strength is the *combination* of pet detection + dual function at a reasonable price, not dominance in any single category. It's the generalist in a field of specialists.
