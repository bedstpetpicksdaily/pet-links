This guide compares two standout contenders—Nextrition Pet and Gnarly Pet—against each other and the broader competitive landscape, looking at actual ingredient sourcing, processing methods, price-per-serving, and the specific health outcomes pet owners report.

Rather than declaring one "winner," we'll show you which option makes sense for different use cases: digestive support, training efficiency, daily snacking, or budget-conscious premium shopping.

Nextrition Pet (Cold-Pressed Dog Food)

Nextrition Pet specializes in cold-pressed dog food, a processing method that preserves nutrients and digestive enzymes typically destroyed by traditional high-heat extrusion. Their formula emphasizes whole-food ingredients, added probiotics, and digestive enzymes designed to support gut health and natural wellness rather than relying on synthetic nutrient supplementation.

This is a complete meal replacement, not a treat or supplement—positioned for daily feeding with emphasis on supporting digestive health and nutrient bioavailability.

Best for: Dogs with digestive sensitivities, owners prioritizing gut health, pet parents willing to pay premium prices for processing method benefits

Gnarly Pet (Treats, Chews & Apparel)

Gnarly Pet built its brand around natural dog treats, protein-rich chews, jerky, and training treats made without artificial additives, fillers, or questionable byproducts. Their product line also includes pet collars and apparel emphasizing durability and comfort, making them a multi-category brand rather than a single-product specialist.

Their training treats are specifically sized for frequent reinforcement during training sessions while maintaining high protein density. The brand emphasizes ingredient sourcing transparency and third-party testing certifications.

Best for: Training-focused pet owners, dogs needing high-protein snacks, owners valuing ingredient transparency and multi-category pet product needs

The Farmer's Dog (Fresh Food Competitor)

Mentioned as a key competitor in the fresh food category, The Farmer's Dog represents the fresh-cooked meal alternative to both cold-pressed kibble and traditional premium dry food. Fresh food brands emphasize minimal processing beyond gentle cooking, raw ingredient visibility, and customization for individual dogs.

This category typically costs more per serving than kibble (including cold-pressed) but less than raw frozen diets, and appeals to owners skeptical of any processed pet food.

Best for: Owners wanting recognizable fresh ingredients over any processing method, dogs with severe kibble sensitivities, budget-flexible households

Raw and Frozen Food Competitors

Raw and frozen diets represent the opposite end of the processing spectrum from kibble, claiming to mirror ancestral canine diets with minimal handling. These brands compete directly with both cold-pressed and fresh food options in the premium segment.

This category typically costs the most per serving and requires freezer storage, but appeals to owners believing processing itself compromises nutrition regardless of method.

Best for: Owners philosophically committed to raw feeding, dogs with multiple allergies to processed ingredients, households with freezer space and budget flexibility

Stella & Chewy's and Zuke's (Established Premium Treats)

These established brands represent the mid-to-premium traditional treat market, offering training-specific and snacking options. They compete directly with Gnarly Pet's positioning on ingredient quality and training treat sizing.

Both brands have significant market presence and veterinary recognition, though they lack the newer emphasis on third-party testing transparency that emerging brands like Gnarly highlight.

Best for: Pet owners familiar with established premium brands, training scenarios where product availability matters, budget-conscious premium buyers

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed method preserves heat-sensitive nutrients and enzymes better than traditional kibble, potentially improving digestive health for sensitive dogs
  • ✓ Gnarly Pet's training treats are sized specifically for frequent reinforcement, solving a real problem with oversized premium treats in training sessions
  • ✓ Gnarly Pet's multi-category approach (treats, chews, collars, apparel) simplifies shopping for brand-committed buyers seeking consistent ingredient philosophy
  • ✓ Cold-pressed and fresh food options provide genuine transparency into processing methods and ingredient sourcing, unlike traditional premium kibble
Cons
  • ✗ Cold-pressed dog foods cost significantly more per serving than traditional premium kibble with unclear long-term digestive ROI for dogs without existing digestive issues
  • ✗ Fresh and raw food competitors often cost 2-3x more per serving than cold-pressed kibble and require freezer space and planning
  • ✗ Gnarly Pet's apparel and non-treat products lack the specialized expertise of dedicated pet apparel brands, potentially sacrificing durability or comfort for brand consistency
  • ✗ Established competitors like Stella & Chewy's and Zuke's offer similar training treat benefits at comparable or lower prices without the premium positioning

Our Verdict

For complete meal replacement: Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed approach genuinely differentiates itself from traditional premium kibble through processing method, making sense for dogs with documented digestive issues or owners philosophically committed to enzyme preservation. However, unless your dog shows digestive sensitivity, the premium price over quality traditional kibble may not deliver measurable ROI. Fresh food competitors like The Farmer's Dog offer a viable alternative if you want to avoid any processing, though at higher cost.

For training and treats: Gnarly Pet's training-specific sizing and high protein density address real training needs better than oversized premium treats. However, established brands like Zuke's and Stella & Chewy's deliver comparable results at competitive prices—Gnarly Pet's advantage lies primarily in its ingredient sourcing transparency and third-party testing claims rather than superior formulation.

Practical recommendation: Match the product category to your actual need: (1) if digestive health is the concern, cold-pressed addresses a real gap that traditional kibble doesn't; (2) if training efficiency matters, sizing-appropriate training treats from either Gnarly Pet or Zuke's will outperform oversized premium treats; (3) if ingredient sourcing transparency matters most, verify third-party testing claims independently rather than assuming premium pricing guarantees verification.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is cold-pressed dog food better than kibble?

Cold-pressed preserves heat-sensitive nutrients and digestive enzymes that traditional high-heat extrusion destroys, providing a genuine processing advantage. However, this translates to measurable health benefits primarily for dogs with existing digestive sensitivities. For healthy dogs on quality traditional premium kibble, the cost difference may not justify the upgrade. Verify ingredient quality and protein content matter more than processing method for most dogs.

How does Nextrition Pet compare to The Farmer's Dog?

Nextrition Pet is cold-pressed kibble emphasizing enzymes and probiotics; The Farmer's Dog is fresh-cooked meals with minimal processing. Cold-pressed costs less per serving and requires no freezer storage. Fresh food offers greater ingredient visibility and customization but costs significantly more and requires planning. Choose based on budget, freezer space, and whether you want kibble texture or fresh meals.

Are Gnarly Pet training treats actually better than Zuke's for training?

Both brands offer training-appropriate sizing and palatability. Gnarly Pet emphasizes ingredient sourcing transparency and third-party testing claims, while Zuke's has more market recognition and availability. Neither offers dramatically superior training effectiveness—sizing, protein density, and your dog's individual preference matter more than brand. Verify actual protein percentages and ingredient sourcing claims rather than assuming premium pricing guarantees superiority.

Should I switch to premium brands if my dog isn't showing digestive issues?

Not necessarily. Healthy dogs eating quality traditional premium kibble (20%+ protein, recognizable ingredients) don't require cold-pressed or fresh food from a health standpoint. Premium brand choice becomes a philosophy and ingredient sourcing preference rather than a health necessity. If cost is a factor, traditional kibble from reputable brands delivers equivalent nutrition at lower price.

How do I evaluate ingredient sourcing claims from premium brands?

Compare actual ingredient lists and guaranteed analysis (protein, fat, fiber percentages) across brands at the same price point. Request third-party testing documentation rather than assuming premium pricing guarantees verification. Check customer reviews specifically mentioning your dog's age and any dietary sensitivities, as formulation differences directly impact digestibility regardless of brand reputation.
