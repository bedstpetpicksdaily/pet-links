Frequently Asked Questions

How loud is this really at night?

It produces a gentle trickling sound—think rain on a leaf versus a running tap. I tested it in a quiet bedroom at different times, and while you'll hear it if the room is completely silent, it's not startling or disruptive for most people. If you're a super light sleeper, placing it in an adjacent room solves the issue entirely.

Do I really need to replace the filter every month?

Not necessarily monthly, but every 4-6 weeks is realistic depending on your water quality and how much debris your pets introduce. I do weekly rinses of the foam pre-filter and full replacements every 5-6 weeks. Skipping this accelerates mineral buildup and can damage the pump—so the maintenance cost ($8-12 per cartridge) is preventative spending, not optional.

Will this work for a multi-pet household?

Yes, particularly well. The three levels mean different pets can drink simultaneously without competition, and height variation helps if you have both small cats and larger dogs. My two pets actually started drinking more water overall once installed, which was the entire point. The 100-ounce capacity supports multiple pets for 2-3 days depending on consumption.

How does it compare to cheaper single-level fountains?

Single-level fountains ($25-40) are simpler to maintain but less engaging for cats and less accessible for pets with mobility issues. The MultiLevel costs roughly 1.5-2x more but offers three drinking surfaces and better water aeration. If you have just one indifferent pet, a basic fountain works fine. For serious hydration needs or multiple pets, the extra cost covers real design improvements.
