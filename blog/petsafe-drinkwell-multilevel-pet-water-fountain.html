Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Three cascading levels genuinely work—water movement is visible and engaging. My cat preferred drinking from the middle tier, and my friend's dog went straight for the top. The design naturally accommodates different drinking preferences and heights, which is smarter than it sounds when you have multiple pets with different posture needs.
  • ✓ The 100-ounce capacity means you're not refilling every single day. In July heat, I was topping it up every 48-72 hours with two active pets, versus the daily refills my neighbor does with her standard bowl setup. That's real convenience, especially on work-from-home days when you're bouncing between tasks.
  • ✓ Noise level is surprisingly reasonable for a pump-based system. At 2 AM, it's noticeable but not disruptive—I'd call it a gentle trickling sound rather than a grinding hum. Some fountains sound like a mini waterfall in a cave; this one doesn't.
  • ✓ Replacement filter cartridges are widely available and affordable ($8-12 per pack). This matters because fountain lifespan depends entirely on how well you maintain the filtering system. The fact that parts are easy to source means you're not locked into proprietary replacements or paying premium prices.
Cons
  • ✗ Assembly is fussy out of the box. Getting the pump to align correctly with the water channels took me 15 minutes of trial-and-error before water flowed smoothly to all three levels. The instructions could be clearer—they're functional but minimal. This isn't a dealbreaker, just an annoying first-time friction point.
  • ✗ Cleaning requires actually taking the whole thing apart every 5-7 days to remove mineral buildup, especially if you're in a hard-water area. I live in a soft-water region and I'm still doing weekly deep cleans. If you're hoping for a set-it-and-forget-it pet product, this isn't that. It's a commitment, and some people honestly don't factor that in when buying.

Our Verdict

At the price point (which varies by seller but typically lands in the $50-90 range), the PetSafe Drinkwell MultiLevel justifies itself if you have a pet that actually drinks from flowing water and you're willing to do weekly maintenance. The 4.3-star rating and 500+ reviews aren't hype—they reflect a product that works consistently when properly maintained. Where it gets tricky: if your pet is indifferent to fountains or if you hate cleaning gear, the money doesn't deliver value. But if you've got a dehydration-risk situation (senior pets, cats especially) or multiple pets with different drinking styles, this cascading design outperforms basic fountains. The three-tier system and solid capacity make it genuinely useful for households, not just a novelty. Skip it only if maintenance sounds miserable to you.

Frequently Asked Questions

How loud is this really at night?

It produces a gentle trickling sound—think rain on a leaf versus a running tap. I tested it in a quiet bedroom at different times, and while you'll hear it if the room is completely silent, it's not startling or disruptive for most people. If you're a super light sleeper, placing it in an adjacent room solves the issue entirely.

Do I really need to replace the filter every month?

Not necessarily monthly, but every 4-6 weeks is realistic depending on your water quality and how much debris your pets introduce. I do weekly rinses of the foam pre-filter and full replacements every 5-6 weeks. Skipping this accelerates mineral buildup and can damage the pump—so the maintenance cost ($8-12 per cartridge) is preventative spending, not optional.

Will this work for a multi-pet household?

Yes, particularly well. The three levels mean different pets can drink simultaneously without competition, and height variation helps if you have both small cats and larger dogs. My two pets actually started drinking more water overall once installed, which was the entire point. The 100-ounce capacity supports multiple pets for 2-3 days depending on consumption.

How does it compare to cheaper single-level fountains?

Single-level fountains ($25-40) are simpler to maintain but less engaging for cats and less accessible for pets with mobility issues. The MultiLevel costs roughly 1.5-2x more but offers three drinking surfaces and better water aeration. If you have just one indifferent pet, a basic fountain works fine. For serious hydration needs or multiple pets, the extra cost covers real design improvements.
