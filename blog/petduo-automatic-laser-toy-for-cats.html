Frequently Asked Questions

Is the laser safe for cats' eyes?

Yes, when used as designed. The PetDuo uses a Class 3R laser (low-power, FDA compliant) that's safe for brief eye exposure. The real concern isn't the laser itself—it's obsessive behavior. If your cat becomes fixated on laser play to the point of compulsive behavior or anxiety when the toy is off, you should limit sessions to 10-15 minutes maximum, which this toy's timer actually enforces. Don't let your cat chase the laser for hours.

How long do the batteries actually last?

Expect 2-4 weeks of regular use (daily 10-15 minute sessions) before needing replacements. If you use the timer feature and let it auto-shut off, you'll get closer to 3-4 weeks. If you're manually turning it on and off multiple times daily or leaving it on for extended periods, batteries drain faster. Use rechargeable AAs to save money long-term—they work fine in this device and pay for themselves within 2-3 battery replacements.

Does it work for multiple cats or just one?

It works fine for 2-3 cats in the same room since the laser covers a decent floor area. However, competitive cats might fight over who 'catches' the dot, which can create tension. In multi-cat households, you might need to supervise or run separate sessions. Single-cat homes get the full benefit without behavioral complications.

Can I use this instead of regular playtime?

No. Laser toys should supplement interactive play, not replace it. Cats need the satisfaction of physically catching and holding prey (or a toy substitute). Laser play is frustrating in that regard because there's nothing to actually catch. Use the PetDuo for quick daily enrichment bursts, but keep a few physical toys available for interactive sessions where your cat can 'win.' Aim for at least one 5-10 minute interactive session weekly with you.
