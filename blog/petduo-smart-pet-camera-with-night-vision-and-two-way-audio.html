Frequently Asked Questions

How does the PetDuo compare to cheaper pet cameras under $50?

Cheaper alternatives often sacrifice night vision quality or two-way audio responsiveness. The PetDuo's 4.3-star rating—built on 500+ reviews—reflects fewer complaints about both features failing after 2–3 months. Budget cameras save you $30–40 upfront but often cost more in frustration when video cuts out or audio lags by 5+ seconds. July is actually a good time to invest in this tier because you're home more, so you'll notice failures before returning it becomes difficult.

Does it work with both Android and iPhone?

Yes, the mobile app functions on both platforms. Real user feedback confirms the Android version occasionally requires a reconnect after a few hours of inactivity, while iOS seems more stable. This isn't a dealbreaker, but if you're an Android user planning to check on your pet hourly, test it first—return window exists for a reason.

Can you actually watch live without lag, or is it streaming delayed video?

Live video streams with roughly 2–3 second delay, which is acceptable for monitoring but means you can't use it for real-time interaction during critical moments (like stopping a dog from eating something dangerous). Two-way audio responds faster (under 1 second), so you can correct behavior, but you're watching a slight delay. This matters if your expectation was instantaneous video; it doesn't matter if you just want to check whether your cat's awake.

Is the night vision actually better than competitors at this price point?

Among cameras in the $60–90 range, the PetDuo's infrared produces clearer detail than most alternatives, but it's not superior to cameras double the price. You'll see your pet clearly—which fur patterns, which eye they're favoring, rough activity level—but not minute details like whether they're limping. For the purpose of identifying whether your dog got into trouble, it delivers. For medical monitoring, it falls short.
