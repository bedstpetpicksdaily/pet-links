The Importance of Hydration During Summer Months

Cats don't naturally drink as much water as they should, which becomes even more critical during hot summer weather. Dehydration can lead to serious health issues including urinary tract problems and kidney stress. During summer, encourage your cat to drink more by providing fresh water throughout the day, using water fountains to attract their interest, and offering moisture-rich treats.

Natural treats from premium brands like PetTreatery can serve dual purposes—providing hydration and nutrition simultaneously. Their carefully crafted cat treats often contain natural ingredients that support fluid intake while delivering essential nutrients your cat needs year-round.

Natural Cooling Treat Ideas for Cats

Freeze-dried treats and moisture-packed snacks are excellent summer options for cats. Consider offering treats with natural ingredients like fish, poultry, or meat that naturally contain higher moisture content. PetTreatery's selection of natural cat treats includes options specifically formulated to be both nutritious and hydrating, making them perfect for summer enjoyment.

You can also create homemade cooling treats by freezing small portions of cat-safe ingredients like plain cooked chicken broth or tuna water in ice cube trays. Combine these DIY options with premium treats from trusted brands to give your cat variety while maintaining nutritional balance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can cats eat frozen treats during summer?

Yes, frozen treats can be wonderful for summer cooling. Ensure any frozen treats are made from cat-safe ingredients and supervise your cat while they enjoy them. Natural freeze-dried treats from brands like PetTreatery can be served as-is or moistened for added hydration.

How often should I give my cat treats during summer?

Treats should comprise no more than 10% of your cat's daily caloric intake. During summer, you can offer hydrating treats more frequently as part of their wellness routine, but always maintain portion control and monitor their overall diet.

What are the signs my cat is dehydrated?

Signs of dehydration include excessive lethargy, dry mouth, sunken eyes, and decreased skin elasticity. If you notice these symptoms, contact your veterinarian immediately and ensure fresh water is always available. Hydrating treats can help, but they're not a substitute for proper water intake.

Are PetTreatery cat treats suitable for all ages?

PetTreatery offers treats formulated for different life stages. Always check the product packaging to ensure you're selecting age-appropriate treats for your kitten, adult, or senior cat.
