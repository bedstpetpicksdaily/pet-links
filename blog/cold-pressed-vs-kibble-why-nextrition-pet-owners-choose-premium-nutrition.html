Understanding Cold-Pressed Dog Food vs. Traditional Kibble

Traditional kibble is created through high-temperature extrusion, a process that heats ingredients to extreme temperatures for rapid production and extended shelf life. While this method is cost-effective and convenient, it destroys many heat-sensitive nutrients, enzymes, and beneficial probiotics that dogs need for optimal health. The high temperatures can also create harmful compounds that may compromise digestive function and nutrient absorption.

Cold-pressed dog food, by contrast, uses low-temperature pressing technology that gently processes ingredients without destroying their nutritional integrity. Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed formulas maintain the bioavailability of essential vitamins, minerals, and amino acids, ensuring your dog receives maximum nutritional value from every bite. This method also preserves natural enzymes that aid in digestion and support a healthy gut microbiome.

How should I store Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed dog food?
