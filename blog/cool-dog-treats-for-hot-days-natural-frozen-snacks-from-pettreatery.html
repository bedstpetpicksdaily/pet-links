During summer months, dogs are susceptible to overheating and dehydration. Frozen treats serve a dual purpose: they help regulate body temperature while keeping your dog hydrated and entertained. Unlike regular treats that can contribute to discomfort in hot weather, frozen snacks provide a cooling effect that's especially beneficial for active dogs, seniors, and breeds prone to heat sensitivity. How to Serve PetTreatery Frozen Treats Safely

While frozen treats are wonderful for cooling down, it's important to serve them appropriately. Never give your dog a frozen treat that's too hard or large, as it can cause dental damage or become a choking hazard. PetTreatery's frozen snacks are designed in convenient bite-sized portions that are safe for dogs of all sizes. Always supervise your dog while they enjoy frozen treats, and remember that treats should only comprise about 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. Frequently Asked Questions

Are PetTreatery frozen treats safe for all dog breeds?

Yes, PetTreatery frozen treats are formulated to be safe for dogs of all breeds and sizes. However, always supervise your dog during treat time and follow portion guidelines based on your dog's weight and age. If your dog has specific health conditions or allergies, consult your veterinarian before introducing new treats.

How should I store PetTreatery frozen treats?

PetTreatery frozen treats should be stored in your freezer at 0°F (-18°C) or below. Most frozen treats can be kept for several months when properly stored. Always check the packaging for specific storage instructions and expiration dates to ensure freshness and safety.

Can frozen treats replace regular meals?

No, frozen treats should only be given as occasional snacks and should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. They're designed to supplement a balanced diet, not replace regular meals. Always ensure your dog has access to fresh water, especially when consuming frozen treats.
