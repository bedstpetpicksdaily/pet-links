Raw vs. Freeze-Dried Dog Food: How Zeal, Primal, and Stella & Chewy's Compare in 2026

⏱10 min read
By Best Pet Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

The premium natural pet food market has expanded rapidly, with raw and freeze-dried options now mainstream among health-conscious dog owners. But choosing between formats—and between brands—requires understanding real tradeoffs in nutrition, convenience, storage, and cost.

This guide compares the two dominant premium formats (raw vs. freeze-dried) and examines how leading brands including Zeal Pet Food, Primal, and Stella & Chewy's deliver on protein quality, digestibility, and summer suitability. Rather than crowning one winner, we'll help you match the right option to your dog's age, activity level, and your household's lifestyle.

Raw Dog Food (Fresh/Refrigerated Format)

Fresh raw dog food requires refrigeration and typically arrives frozen with gel packs or insulated shipping. Brands like Primal offer formulations designed to mimic wild canine diets with whole meats, organs, and bone content. The bioavailability of nutrients is highest in this format, meaning dogs absorb and utilize nutrition more efficiently.

Summer storage becomes a consideration: raw food requires proper temperature control during delivery in warm months to prevent bacterial growth. Dogs on raw diets often show faster digestion and reduced appetite variation, important during hot months when heat naturally suppresses feeding.

Best for: Dogs with strong digestive systems, owners with freezer space, and those willing to manage storage/shipping logistics. Ideal for dogs with reduced summer appetites who benefit from concentrated nutrition in smaller portions.

Freeze-Dried Raw Dog Food

Freeze-dried formats remove moisture from raw ingredients while preserving nutritional density and bioavailability. Brands like Stella & Chewy's and Zeal Pet Food offer this option, which rehydrates quickly with water and requires no special storage beyond a cool, dry pantry. This format eliminates temperature-control concerns during summer shipping and storage.

Convenience is the major advantage: no thawing, no freezer space needed, longer shelf life. The tradeoff is higher cost per serving due to processing technology, though the concentrated nutrition means smaller daily portions than traditional kibble.

Best for: Busy pet parents, travel-heavy owners, those without dedicated freezer space, and dogs needing convenient premium nutrition during summer. Also suitable for multi-pet households transitioning gradually to raw feeding.

Zeal Pet Food (Grain-Free Raw & Freeze-Dried Range)

Zeal positions itself as a premium natural brand offering both raw and freeze-dried options in grain-free formulations. The brand emphasizes ingredient transparency and bioavailable protein sourcing. Zeal's freeze-dried line specifically targets owners seeking raw nutrition without refrigeration logistics, while their raw products cater to traditionalists prioritizing maximum freshness.

Zeal differentiates through commitment to natural, grain-free formulations designed to support digestive health and energy levels. However, pricing and specific protein percentages were not detailed in available product materials, requiring direct comparison with Primal and Stella & Chewy's on actual product labels.

Best for: Owners seeking a premium alternative with multiple format options, those wanting to test raw feeding via freeze-dried before committing to fresh raw, and dogs with grain sensitivities.

Primal Pet Food (Raw Format Specialist)

Primal focuses exclusively on raw dog food in refrigerated/frozen formats. Their ingredient philosophy emphasizes whole meats and organ meats balanced for complete nutrition. Primal's raw approach delivers the highest bioavailability but requires reliable freezer storage and careful attention to shipping logistics during summer months.

Primal positions as a heritage brand in the raw feeding space, appealing to owners who prioritize maximum freshness and traditional raw feeding philosophy. The raw format means shorter shelf life once thawed and requires disciplined rotation and storage practices.

Best for: Purist raw feeders with adequate freezer space, owners in cool climates, dogs with sensitive digestion who need the highest bioavailability, and those willing to manage thawing/storage logistics.

Stella & Chewy's (Freeze-Dried Market Leader)

Stella & Chewy's pioneered the freeze-dried raw market and maintains strong brand recognition. Their freeze-dried formulas deliver raw nutrition benefits without refrigeration requirements. The brand offers convenient serving and is widely available through major retailers, making it more accessible than specialty raw brands.

As an established player, Stella & Chewy's has extensive product lines and flavor variety. Freeze-dried format means no shipping temperature concerns and extended pantry life, though costs run higher than comparable kibble due to processing technology and established brand premium.

Best for: Convenience-seeking owners, those new to raw feeding wanting low-commitment entry, buyers who prioritize retail accessibility, and multi-pet households needing flexible serving options.

Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Raw formats (Primal, fresh Zeal) deliver maximum nutrient bioavailability—dogs absorb nutrition more efficiently, reducing portion sizes needed and supporting energy during summer heat.
  • ✓ Freeze-dried options (Stella & Chewy's, Zeal freeze-dried) eliminate refrigeration logistics, eliminate summer shipping temperature-control stress, and require only cool, dry pantry storage.
  • ✓ Grain-free formulations across Zeal and competitors support dogs with grain sensitivities and digestive issues that kibble-based diets often trigger.
  • ✓ Raw and freeze-dried formats lead to improved digestion and more consistent appetite, particularly valuable for dogs with heat-suppressed summer appetites.
Cons
  • ✗ Fresh raw formats require dedicated freezer space (significant for multi-dog households) and demand careful attention to thawing, rotation, and bacterial safety—especially challenging during summer months requiring insulated shipping.
  • ✗ Freeze-dried and premium raw formats cost significantly more per serving than traditional kibble; budget-conscious owners may find the price premium unsustainable long-term.
  • ✗ Limited transparency in available materials on actual protein percentages, fat content, and organ meat ratios—direct label comparison needed to verify quality claims between Zeal, Primal, and Stella & Chewy's.
  • ✗ Transitioning dogs to raw or freeze-dried diets requires gradual implementation over weeks to avoid digestive upset; immediate switches can cause gastrointestinal stress.

Our Verdict

For summer feeding specifically: Freeze-dried formats (Stella & Chewy's, Zeal freeze-dried) win on practicality. They eliminate shipping temperature risks in warm months, require no freezer space, and deliver concentrated nutrition for dogs with heat-reduced appetites. Cost is higher, but convenience and food-safety tradeoffs favor freeze-dried in hot climates.

For maximum nutrition and lowest ongoing cost: Fresh raw from Primal or Zeal works best if you have freezer capacity and live in a cool climate. Bioavailability is highest, and per-serving nutrition density is superior. However, summer logistics and storage demands make this option challenging for many owners.

For transition/testing: Start with Stella & Chewy's freeze-dried to evaluate whether your dog thrives on raw nutrition without committing to fresh raw logistics. If your dog shows clear digestive improvement, you can later transition to fresh raw (Primal or Zeal) if freezer space and storage logistics allow.

Bottom line: This isn't about one brand being "best"—it's about matching format (raw vs. freeze-dried) to your logistics, then choosing a brand based on actual protein percentages, fat content, and ingredient sourcing visible on product labels. For most owners, freeze-dried options reduce friction in summer feeding while delivering genuine nutritional advantages over kibble. If you have freezer space and live in a cool climate, fresh raw offers superior bioavailability, but the logistics advantage favors freeze-dried in warm months.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe during hot summer months?

Fresh raw food requires proper temperature control during shipping and storage to prevent bacterial growth—this becomes difficult in summer heat. Freeze-dried raw formats eliminate this concern by removing moisture, making them safer and more practical for warm-weather feeding. If choosing fresh raw in summer, verify the supplier offers insulated shipping with gel packs and plan for immediate refrigeration upon arrival.

How do raw and freeze-dried formats differ nutritionally?

Both deliver higher bioavailability than kibble, meaning dogs absorb nutrients more efficiently. Fresh raw (Primal, Zeal raw) has marginally higher bioavailability since no processing occurs. Freeze-dried formats (Stella & Chewy's, Zeal freeze-dried) are nearly equivalent after rehydration, with the tradeoff being convenience and storage versus fractionally higher nutrient density in fresh raw.

What's the difference between Zeal, Primal, and Stella & Chewy's formats?

Primal specializes in fresh refrigerated raw only. Stella & Chewy's dominates freeze-dried raw. Zeal offers both formats, making it flexible for testing. Format choice matters more than brand—your decision should prioritize whether you can manage fresh raw logistics or need the convenience of freeze-dried.

Can I mix freeze-dried raw with kibble?

Yes. Freeze-dried raw can be mixed with kibble for gradual transitions or to supplement traditional diets. Start at 10-25% freeze-dried mixed with 75-90% kibble, then gradually increase the raw proportion over 2-3 weeks to allow digestive adaptation and prevent upset.

How should I store raw and freeze-dried dog food?

Fresh raw requires freezer storage and must be thawed in the refrigerator before feeding. Freeze-dried raw needs only a cool, dry pantry—no refrigeration required. Once rehydrated with water, freeze-dried should be used within 24 hours, similar to fresh raw thawed portions.

How do I know which brand actually has the highest protein quality?

Check each brand's ingredient list and nutrition facts panel for protein percentage and primary protein sources. Premium raw brands use whole meats (chicken, beef, organ meats) in higher percentages; lower-quality options rely heavily on bone meal or byproducts despite similar pricing. Without specific percentages in marketing materials, compare actual product labels directly.
