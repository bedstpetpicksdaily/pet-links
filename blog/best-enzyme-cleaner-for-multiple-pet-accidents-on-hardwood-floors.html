  • Non-toxic, pet-safe ingredients with quick drying: Since your pet will walk on these floors repeatedly, you need a formula that's safe if they lick it and dries fast enough to prevent re-soiling. Avoid cleaners that leave sticky residue or take hours to dry.

Frequently Asked Questions

What enzyme cleaner works best for pet urine on hardwood floors?

Look for cleaners containing protease and amylase enzymes specifically formulated for hardwood, such as Nature's Miracle or Rocco & Roxie, as these break down uric acid crystals that cause odors and stains. Avoid cleaners with excess moisture that can warp wood—opt for enzymatic sprays rather than liquids, and always test on an inconspicuous area first.

Can you use enzyme cleaner on sealed hardwood floors?

Yes, enzyme cleaners are safe on sealed hardwood floors when applied correctly—spray lightly, let sit for 10-15 minutes, then wipe thoroughly with a damp cloth to prevent water damage. The key is minimizing moisture exposure while allowing the enzymes enough time to break down organic matter.

How many times should I treat old pet stains on hardwood with enzyme cleaner?

Multiple treatments are typically needed for old, set-in stains—apply enzyme cleaner 2-4 times over several days, allowing 24 hours between applications for the enzymes to work. Stubborn stains may require repeat treatments over 1-2 weeks until the odor is completely eliminated.

Will enzyme cleaner remove pet stains and odors from hardwood permanently?

Enzyme cleaners eliminate odors by breaking down the bacteria and urine crystals causing the smell, providing permanent results if applied thoroughly to all affected areas. However, if urine has soaked deep into the wood, you may need professional restoration or localized sanding and refinishing for severe cases.
