Frequently Asked Questions

Will my cat actually use an automated litter box?

Most cats adapt within a few days, but not all. Younger cats and those raised around electronics tend to accept it faster. Older cats and those with strong preferences for specific litter textures sometimes reject it permanently. The safest approach is keeping your traditional box available for the first week while your cat investigates the new one. If they're using it regularly by day five, you've likely got a convert.

How often do you actually need to empty the waste drawer?

With a single cat, we emptied the drawer roughly every 10-14 days. With multiple cats, plan on weekly emptying. The drawer capacity is reasonable but not huge—if you ignore it for three weeks, you might encounter a full drawer issue. The good news: emptying is cleaner than scooping since waste is compacted and enclosed.

Is the app control actually useful or just a gimmick?

It's genuinely useful if you have multiple cats or cats with health concerns. You can see exactly when each cat used the box, how long they spent there, and receive alerts if litter is low or the unit needs maintenance. For single-cat households without health monitoring needs, it's a nice-to-have rather than essential. The automatic cleaning happens regardless of app status, so it's not required for the core function.

How much money do you actually save on litter?

Crystal litter cartridges cost around $25-30 each and last 2-3 weeks per cat, translating to roughly $40-50 monthly for single-cat households. Traditional clay litter costs $10-15 monthly. You're spending more per month, but the trade-off is time saved and reduced odor control expenses. Over 18-24 months, the system pays for itself if you value your time at any reasonable rate.
