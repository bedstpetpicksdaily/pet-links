Frequently Asked Questions

Do cooling mats for dogs actually work?

Yes, cooling mats work by using gel or water-based technology that absorbs and disperses your dog's body heat without electricity. They're particularly effective for senior dogs, breeds with thick coats, and during hot weather, typically reducing surface temperature by 10-15 degrees below ambient temperature.

How long do dog cooling mats stay cool?

Most gel cooling mats stay cool for 3-8 hours depending on the brand, room temperature, and your dog's weight, as they work by absorbing heat rather than actively cooling. Pressure-activated gel mats recharge as soon as your dog gets off them, while others may need refrigeration between uses for extended cooling periods.

What size cooling mat do I need for my dog?

Measure your dog lying down in their natural sleeping position and choose a mat that's at least 4-6 inches longer and wider than their body. Most brands offer sizes ranging from small (24x16 inches) for dogs under 25 lbs to extra-large (40x30 inches) for dogs over 75 lbs.

Are cooling mats safe for dogs to chew on?

Most quality cooling mats are made with durable, non-toxic materials and are designed to withstand moderate chewing, though they're not chew toys. If your dog is an aggressive chewer, look for mats with reinforced edges or consider placing it in their crate, and always supervise use to prevent ingestion of gel if the mat is punctured.
