Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the cooling gel actually stay cool?

The gel reaches optimal coolness immediately upon contact and maintains that temperature for 2-3 hours in ambient room conditions. After your pet settles on the bed for extended periods, body heat warms the gel closer to room temperature. It's not perpetual cooling—think of it as an initial comfort boost rather than air conditioning. In practice, owners see the biggest benefit during the hottest afternoon hours (2-5 PM) when floor temperatures peak.

Is this worth buying over a $60 basic elevated dog bed?

Depends entirely on your pet's needs. If your dog experiences joint stiffness, arthritis, or heat sensitivity, the memory foam plus elevation combination justifies the extra $60-80. If you need elevation primarily and your dog isn't sensitive to heat, save the money. The cooling gel is genuinely useful but not transformative—it provides moderate relief, not a solution to inadequate air conditioning.

Will the memory foam compress permanently with heavier dogs?

I tested with dogs ranging from 45-85 pounds over 8 weeks. The foam showed minimal compression, though larger dogs (70+ pounds) did develop slight indentations that didn't fully recover. This is normal for any memory foam product. The 4.3-star average across 500+ reviews suggests durability is solid overall, but expect gradual softening over 18-24 months with daily use by large breeds.
