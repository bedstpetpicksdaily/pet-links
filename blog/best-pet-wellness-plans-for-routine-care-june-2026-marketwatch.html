Frequently Asked Questions

what is the best pet wellness plan for routine care in 2026

As of June 2026, top-rated pet wellness plans include Banfield Optimum Wellness Plans, PetWellClinic memberships, and Fetch by The Dodo's wellness add-ons, which cover annual exams, vaccinations, and bloodwork at predictable monthly costs. The best plan depends on your pet's age, breed, and how often you visit the vet, so comparing per-visit savings against monthly fees is essential. Plans from Petco Vital Care and AKC Pet Insurance wellness riders are also strong contenders for value and network availability.

is a pet wellness plan worth it or should I just pay out of pocket

A pet wellness plan is generally worth it if your pet is due for multiple routine services like vaccines, dental cleanings, and annual bloodwork, since bundling these can save $100–$300 annually compared to paying individually. However, if your pet is young and healthy with minimal vet visits planned, paying out of pocket may cost less than ongoing monthly membership fees. Review the plan's included services carefully and calculate your expected annual vet spending before committing.

what does a pet wellness plan typically cover

Most pet wellness plans in 2026 cover core preventive care such as annual or bi-annual wellness exams, core vaccinations, flea and heartworm prevention, and routine bloodwork or urinalysis. Some premium tiers also include dental cleanings, spay/neuter discounts, and microchipping services. It's important to note that wellness plans are not pet insurance and do not cover accidents, illnesses, or emergency veterinary visits.

what is the difference between pet insurance and a pet wellness plan

Pet insurance is designed to cover unexpected costs like accidents, surgeries, and illnesses, typically reimbursing a percentage of the vet bill after a deductible is met. A pet wellness plan, on the other hand, is a prepaid or subscription-based program specifically for routine and preventive care like checkups and vaccines. Many pet owners in 2026 choose to use both together — a wellness plan for predictable routine costs and insurance for unexpected emergencies — to get the most comprehensive coverage.
