Frequently Asked Questions

What is included in a complete dog anxiety kit?

A complete dog anxiety kit typically includes a combination of tools such as a calming wrap or thunder shirt, natural supplements like CBD chews or melatonin treats, a pheromone diffuser or calming spray, and sometimes a white noise machine or calming music playlist. Some kits also include a comfort toy, anxiety-reducing chew, and a step-by-step guide for identifying and managing triggers. The goal is to provide multiple layers of relief so you can find what works best for your specific dog.

Do dog anxiety kits actually work?

Dog anxiety kits can be highly effective, especially when they use a multi-modal approach that combines physical comfort tools, calming scents, and supplements together rather than relying on one method alone. Results vary depending on the severity of your dog's anxiety and the specific triggers involved, such as thunderstorms, separation, or loud noises. Many pet owners report noticeable improvement within the first few uses, though consistency and pairing the kit with positive reinforcement training tends to produce the best long-term results.

What can I give my dog for anxiety immediately?

For immediate relief, a calming wrap like a Thundershirt can work within minutes by applying gentle, constant pressure similar to swaddling a baby. Fast-acting calming chews containing ingredients like L-theanine, chamomile, or melatonin can also take effect within 30 to 60 minutes and are a popular option in most anxiety kits. Spraying a lavender or dog-appeasing pheromone (DAP) spray on your dog's bedding or collar is another quick option you can use alongside other tools for faster results.

How do I know if my dog has anxiety and needs a kit?

Common signs that your dog may have anxiety include excessive barking or whining, destructive chewing, pacing, trembling, accidents in the house, or trying to escape when left alone or during loud events. If your dog displays these behaviors consistently during specific triggers like thunderstorms, fireworks, car rides, or when you leave the house, an anxiety kit is worth trying before pursuing prescription medication. Consulting your veterinarian is always a good first step, as they can help rule out medical causes and recommend whether a natural anxiety kit or additional behavioral therapy is the right approach.
