Frequently Asked Questions

What do I need to buy before bringing a new dog home?

Before bringing your new dog home, you should have a collar with ID tags, a 6-foot leash, food and water bowls, age-appropriate dog food, a crate, and a comfortable bed. You'll also want to pick up poop bags, a brush suited to your dog's coat type, and a few chew toys to keep them occupied. Having everything ready before day one reduces stress for both you and your new pup.

How much does it cost to set up everything for a new dog?

The initial starter kit for a new dog typically costs between $200 and $500, covering essentials like a crate, bedding, leash, collar, bowls, and basic grooming tools. Higher-end options or larger breeds can push costs closer to $700 or more when you factor in quality food, training aids, and vet visit supplies. Budgeting an additional $100–$300 for your first vet appointment, vaccinations, and flea/tick prevention is also highly recommended.

What should I feed my new dog and how much?

You should feed your new dog a high-quality food appropriate for their life stage — puppy, adult, or senior — with a named protein like chicken or beef listed as the first ingredient. Portion sizes vary by weight and breed, so follow the feeding guide on the bag and adjust based on your vet's advice. If your dog was on a different food before adoption, transition gradually over 7–10 days by mixing the old and new food to avoid digestive upset.

What vaccinations does a new dog need right away?

New dogs, especially puppies, need core vaccines including distemper, parvovirus, adenovirus (DAP), and rabies, which are considered essential for all dogs regardless of lifestyle. Your vet will set up a vaccination schedule, with puppies typically needing booster shots every 3–4 weeks until around 16 weeks of age. You should schedule a vet appointment within the first week of bringing your dog home to confirm their health status and get their vaccine plan started.
