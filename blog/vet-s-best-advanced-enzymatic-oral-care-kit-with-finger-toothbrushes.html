Frequently Asked Questions

How long does one kit last if used daily on one pet?

The paste lasts roughly 2-3 months with daily use on a single dog or cat, depending on how much you apply per brushing. Most kits include enough finger brushes (typically 4-6) to last 6+ months even if one wears out or gets lost. Budget for replacing the paste every quarter if you're committed to daily brushing.

Does this work for cats, or just dogs?

It works for both, but implementation differs significantly. Cats tolerate the finger brush better than rigid toothbrushes due to the smaller, softer contact, but even cooperative cats rarely accept daily brushing. If you have a cat, expect to use this 3-4 times per week realistically. The enzymatic formula is safe for both species, so there's no concern there.

Can I use this if my pet already has tartar buildup, or only for prevention?

It's primarily preventative—the enzymes prevent plaque from calcifying into hard tartar, but they won't dissolve existing tartar buildup. If your pet already has visible yellow or brown buildup on the teeth, you need a professional cleaning first. After the cleaning, this kit shines because it prevents rapid reaccumulation. Using it before professional cleaning is like mopping a dirty floor without sweeping first.

How does this compare to dental treats and water additives?

Dental treats and water additives are passive—your pet gets the benefit without your effort, but they're significantly less effective than mechanical brushing. Enzymatic paste plus physical brushing with the finger tool actually removes plaque. Treats and additives reduce tartar formation by maybe 20-30%. If you want real results, you need the brushing component. However, combining all three (treats + additives + this kit) gives you the strongest preventative approach short of professional cleaning.
