Why Cooling Treats Matter in Summer

During hot summer months, dogs are prone to overheating and dehydration. Cooling treats serve a dual purpose: they help regulate your dog's body temperature while providing mental stimulation and enjoyment. Natural cooling treats made with wholesome ingredients are far superior to sugary alternatives, as they won't upset your dog's stomach or cause unwanted health issues. The best options include fruits, vegetables, and specially formulated frozen treats that contain beneficial nutrients.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are cooling dog treats safe for all dogs?

Most cooling treats are safe for healthy dogs, but those with specific dietary needs or allergies should consult their veterinarian first. PetTreatery clearly labels all ingredients to help you make the best choice for your dog.

How often can I give my dog cooling treats?

Treats should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. Use them as occasional rewards or during hot afternoons. PetTreatery's packaging includes feeding guidelines to help you portion appropriately.

What ingredients make a cooling treat effective?

Natural cooling ingredients include watermelon, cucumber, pumpkin, coconut, and yogurt. These provide hydration and nutrients while naturally cooling your dog. PetTreatery uses premium versions of these ingredients in their formulations.

Can frozen treats harm my dog's teeth?

While extremely hard frozen treats can damage teeth, most quality cooling treats are soft enough to eat safely. PetTreatery designs their treats with dental health in mind, offering safe frozen options.
