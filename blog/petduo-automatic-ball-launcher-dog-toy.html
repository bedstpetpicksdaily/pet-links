Frequently Asked Questions

Is the PetDuo Automatic Ball Launcher worth buying vs. a manual ball thrower?

Manual throwers cost $10-$20 and require zero batteries, but they demand you participate in every throw. If you have limited mobility, arthritis, or a dog that wants 100+ throws daily, the automatic launcher justifies the higher price. For casual fetch sessions 2-3 times weekly, stick with manual.

How durable is it based on customer reviews?

The 4.3-star rating suggests decent durability overall, but scan the actual 3-star and 4-star reviews (not just 5-star praise). Many owners report the launcher lasting 6-12 months of regular use. Some mention jamming issues with non-standard balls. Buy replacement parts availability before purchase.

Does it work for small dogs or only large breeds?

Most automatic launchers, including PetDuo models, have adjustable distance settings. Small dogs can often use the shortest launch distance setting, but check the specific model's minimum setting. Some models struggle with tiny breeds because the ball is still too large or throws too far even on the lowest setting.

What's a cheaper alternative if the PetDuo price is too high?

A manual ball thrower ($12-$20) handles 90% of what the automatic does for 75% less money. If you need automatic operation specifically, check budget brands on Amazon—ratings dip slightly, but many offer comparable features at $40-$60 versus higher-tier PetDuo models.

Does it actually tire out high-energy dogs or just distract them?

Depends on your dog and play duration. 15-20 minute sessions with an automatic launcher can genuinely exhaust a medium-energy dog. For high-drive breeds (Border Collies, Australian Shepherds), combine it with training and mental puzzles—fetch alone won't fully satisfy them, no matter the tool.
