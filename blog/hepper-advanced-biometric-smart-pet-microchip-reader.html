Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Scanning too quickly: Many pet owners rush the scanning process, passing the reader over the chip too fast. Take 2-3 seconds and move slowly across the microchip area (usually between the shoulder blades) to ensure a complete read.
  • Assuming your pet's chip is registered: A microchip is only useful if your contact information is registered in a database. Verify your pet's chip details are current and up-to-date with your vet or the microchip company immediately after implantation.
  • Not keeping backup power sources: Battery failure means no scans. Keep spare batteries on hand or invest in a rechargeable model so you're always ready for an emergency scan situation.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Create a scanning protocol: If you manage multiple pets, establish a routine—scan each animal monthly to confirm their chip is still functioning properly. This catches problems early before they become critical during a lost-pet emergency.
  • Keep detailed records: Document your pet's microchip number, registration details, and the date of your last successful scan. Store this information both digitally and on paper so you have instant access during stressful situations.
  • Position your pet correctly: For best results, have the pet remain still and calm during scanning. Hold the reader perpendicular to the body and scan from multiple angles if the first attempt doesn't register—some chips respond better to slightly different positioning.
