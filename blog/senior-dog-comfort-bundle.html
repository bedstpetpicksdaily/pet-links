Frequently Asked Questions

What should be included in a senior dog comfort bundle?

A senior dog comfort bundle typically includes an orthopedic memory foam bed to relieve joint pressure, a heated blanket or pad for warmth, and joint supplement chews containing glucosamine and chondroitin. You may also want to add a non-slip mat, raised food and water bowls to ease neck strain, and calming aids like lavender-infused toys or CBD treats designed for older dogs.

At what age is a dog considered a senior and needs extra comfort?

Most dogs are considered seniors between 7 and 10 years old, though larger breeds like Great Danes can enter their senior years as early as 5 or 6. Once your dog reaches this stage, you may notice signs like stiff joints, slower movement, or increased sleep, which are all signals that extra comfort and support products are needed.

What is the best bed for an old dog with arthritis?

An orthopedic memory foam dog bed is widely considered the best option for arthritic senior dogs because it conforms to the body and reduces pressure on painful joints. Look for beds with a low entry point or bolster sides that make it easy for your dog to get in and out without jumping, and choose a waterproof liner since older dogs can be prone to accidents.

How can I make my senior dog more comfortable at home?

You can make your senior dog more comfortable by adding orthopedic bedding in their favorite resting spots, placing non-slip rugs on hardwood or tile floors to prevent slipping, and using ramps or steps to help them access furniture or cars without straining their joints. Maintaining a consistent routine, keeping them warm, and scheduling regular vet checkups to manage pain or mobility issues will also significantly improve their daily quality of life.
