Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Arf Pets Self-Cleaning Slicker Brush suitable for all coat types?

This brush works best on short to medium-length coats and curly hair. For long-haired breeds, you may need additional grooming tools to reach deeper into the coat.

How often should I use the self-cleaning button?

Press the self-cleaning button whenever hair accumulates on the brush, typically after every few strokes depending on your pet's shedding level.

Can the Arf Pets brush be used on sensitive skin?

Yes, when used gently, the fine wires are suitable for sensitive skin. Always brush with light pressure and avoid aggressive strokes on tender areas.

How do I maintain the self-cleaning brush?

Rinse the brush under running water occasionally and allow it to air dry. Avoid submerging the button mechanism to preserve its functionality.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Brushing wet or damp fur — always wait until your dog's coat is completely dry, as damp hair can mat more easily and the brush won't glide smoothly through the coat.
  • Pressing too hard or using harsh strokes — let the slicker brush do the work with gentle, flowing motions; excessive pressure can irritate sensitive skin and actually cause breakage.
  • Neglecting to clean the brush regularly — failing to empty the self-cleaning mechanism means matted hair buildup, which reduces effectiveness and can harbor bacteria.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Brush in the direction of hair growth using long, smooth strokes rather than short jabbing motions — this prevents tugging and keeps your dog calm while distributing natural oils throughout the coat.
  • Start with a metal comb after brushing to catch any remaining mats or tangles you might have missed, especially in high-friction areas like armpits and behind ears.
  • Establish a consistent grooming routine (2-3 times weekly for long-haired breeds) so you're always working with fresh, manageable coats rather than playing catch-up with serious matting.
