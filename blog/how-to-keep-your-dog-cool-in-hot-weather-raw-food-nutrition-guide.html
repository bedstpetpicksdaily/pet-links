The Connection Between Raw Nutrition and Temperature Regulation

Raw dog food contains natural moisture content that processed kibble simply cannot match. When dogs consume whole prey-based raw meals, they're absorbing nutrients in their most bioavailable form, which supports efficient metabolism and natural cooling mechanisms. Unlike heat-processed kibble that requires additional water intake to digest, raw meals from Smallbatch Pets' frozen and freeze-dried lines deliver moisture-rich nutrition that aids in hydration without taxing your dog's digestive system during hot weather.

The high protein quality in raw diets also supports lean muscle maintenance, which is essential for temperature regulation. Fresh, uncooked meals preserve heat-sensitive amino acids and enzymes that commercial pet foods lose during manufacturing. Smallbatch Pets' commitment to minimal processing ensures your dog receives complete nutritional profiles that naturally support their body's cooling processes.

Hydration Strategy: Raw Food as a Water Source

One of the most significant advantages of raw dog food during summer is its inherent moisture content. Whole muscle meat, organs, and bone broth contain 70-80% water, making each meal a hydration event. Smallbatch Pets' frozen raw meals and bone broth products are specifically designed to maximize this benefit, providing both nutrition and fluids in every serving. Their bone broth, in particular, offers electrolytes and collagen that support gut health while encouraging natural water intake through appealing, nutrient-rich broths.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe during hot weather?

Yes, raw dog food is safe year-round when handled properly. In fact, its high moisture content and nutrient density make it ideal for summer. Keep frozen meals properly stored and follow food safety guidelines. Smallbatch Pets' products are designed with safety protocols that maintain quality throughout storage.

How much should I feed my dog raw food in summer?

Feed your dog the same portion size as you would any diet—typically 2-3% of body weight daily. However, monitor your dog's energy levels and appetite in heat; some dogs naturally eat less when hot. Smallbatch Pets provides feeding guidelines for each product to help you maintain optimal nutrition.

Can I mix raw food with bone broth for extra hydration?

Absolutely. Mixing Smallbatch Pets' bone broth with their raw meals or freeze-dried options increases moisture content and encourages water intake. This combination provides both nutrition and hydration support, making it an excellent summer feeding strategy.

Are freeze-dried raw meals as nutritious as frozen raw food?

Freeze-dried raw meals retain the same nutritional profile as frozen raw food—they're simply dehydrated for convenience. Smallbatch Pets' freeze-dried products maintain nutrient density while offering easier storage and travel, making them perfect for summer trips.

How do I transition my dog to raw food before summer?

Introduce raw food gradually over 7-10 days, mixing increasing amounts with their current diet. Start with Smallbatch Pets' lightly cooked meals if your dog has a sensitive stomach, then transition to raw. Gradual introduction allows digestive systems to adjust properly.
