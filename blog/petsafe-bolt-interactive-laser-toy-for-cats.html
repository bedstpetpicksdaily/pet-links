Is the automatic mode actually useful or just a gimmick?

Legitimately useful if you work longer hours or have multiple cats competing for attention. The automatic mode lets your cat exercise independently—important for weight management and reducing destructive behavior. However, many cats tire of unattended laser play after 5-10 minutes. Do vets actually recommend laser toys, or is this a waste of money?

Vets are split. Lasers provide excellent cardio stimulation and engage prey drive, but they can trigger obsessive behavior in some cats and provide no 'catch' satisfaction (the laser always escapes). Best practice: use laser toys as one toy in a rotation, not the only option. Combine with toys your cat can actually catch and kill (feather wands, mice toys) to satisfy the complete hunting sequence. The PetSafe Bolt is a solid tool, but it's one piece of a complete enrichment strategy, not a standalone solution.
