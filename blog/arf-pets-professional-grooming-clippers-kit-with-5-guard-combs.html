Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to cordless clippers at the $80-120 price range?

The motor consistency matters more than the price gap suggests. Most clippers under $80 stutter under thick coat resistance—you're doing multiple passes, which stresses your dog and dulls blades faster. The Arf Pets kit maintains torque through single passes on dense undercoats. You're not paying for a brand name; you're paying for a motor that doesn't give up halfway through a grooming session.

Can you actually use all 5 guard combs regularly, or do most people stick with 1-2?

Realistic answer: most owners use 3-4 guards regularly. The 10mm (shortest) handles cleanup around paws and face. The 19mm and 22mm handle body work on larger dogs. The middle guards feel redundant until you groom a cat with a specific coat or need precision on a dog's sides versus back. You won't use all five equally, but having options prevents the frustration of 'the one guard I need doesn't exist.'

What's the actual battery life, and does performance drop as the battery drains?

Expect 60-90 minutes of runtime depending on coat thickness and blade engagement. Performance stays stable until roughly 15% battery remains, then you'll notice a subtle drop in cutting speed—not dramatic, but noticeable on thick coats. This is normal for lithium-ion technology. Owners typically charge between dogs rather than waiting for a full drain, so the battery lifespan extends compared to always running it flat.
