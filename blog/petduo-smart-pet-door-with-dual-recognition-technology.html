Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a microchip in my pet already, or does PetDuo provide one?

PetDuo works with existing microchips—it doesn't provide one. If your pet isn't chipped yet, you'll need a vet to implant a standard 134.2kHz microchip (common in the US). This is actually a strength because it prevents vendor lock-in. The reader recognizes any standard chip, not proprietary ones. Plan $50-100 at your vet if your pet needs chipping.

What happens if WiFi goes down? Does my pet get locked out?

The microchip reader works independently of WiFi, so your pet can always enter if they're chipped. You just lose remote notifications and app access. Local operation is the backup system, which makes sense. I tested this by turning off my router deliberately—Milo had zero issues entering. The WiFi component is bonus monitoring, not the core function.

Can the microchip reader be fooled or jammed?

The frequency used is relatively secure, but electromagnetic interference is theoretically possible in rare circumstances. I haven't encountered jamming issues in regular residential use. The bigger practical concern is dead battery—if the unit dies, you lose the smart functions and fall back to it being a non-functional flap. This is why that 10-14 day summer charging cycle matters. Set a calendar reminder, and it's a non-issue.

Is this appropriate for both cats and small dogs?

Yes, with caveats. The door opening is adjustable, and PetDuo markets it for both. I tested it with Milo (cat) exclusively, so I can't speak to consistent dog performance. The microchip recognition itself works regardless of pet type. Size-wise, it suits cats and dogs under 25 pounds comfortably. Larger dogs might struggle with the flap mechanism design.
