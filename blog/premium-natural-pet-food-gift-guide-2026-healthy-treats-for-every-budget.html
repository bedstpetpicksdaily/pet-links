Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between raw, freeze-dried, and grain-free pet food?

Raw pet food contains uncooked whole ingredients that preserve enzymes and nutrients but require careful handling. Freeze-dried food uses raw ingredients that are frozen and dehydrated, maintaining nutritional benefits with extended shelf life and convenience. Grain-free formulations eliminate grains like wheat and corn, replacing them with alternative carbohydrates or protein sources, which can benefit pets with grain sensitivities.

Is premium natural pet food worth the investment?

Yes, premium natural pet food typically results in improved coat quality, better digestion, increased energy levels, and reduced vet visits for diet-related issues. While the initial cost is higher, many pet parents find they save money long-term through fewer health complications and better overall wellness.

How do I transition my pet to Zeal Pet Food or other premium natural brands?

Gradually mix increasing amounts of the new food with your pet's current food over 7-10 days. Start with 25% new food and 75% old food, increasing new food proportions every few days. This slow transition prevents digestive upset and allows your pet's system to adapt to higher-quality ingredients.

Are freeze-dried pet foods as nutritious as raw?

Freeze-dried foods maintain approximately 95% of raw food's nutritional value while being safer and more convenient. The freeze-drying process removes water without using heat, preserving proteins, vitamins, and beneficial enzymes that make raw feeding attractive.

Can cats and dogs eat the same premium natural food?

No, cats and dogs have different nutritional requirements. Cats are obligate carnivores requiring higher protein and taurine, while dogs are omnivores. Quality brands like Zeal offer species-specific formulations designed to meet each animal's unique biological needs.
