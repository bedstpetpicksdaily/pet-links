Persian cats are known for their luxurious coats and finicky personalities, but one of the most frustrating challenges many owners face is getting them to drink enough water. What to Look For

  • Ultra-quiet operation: Persians are sensitive to noise and vibrations, so a fountain with a whisper-quiet motor is essential. Many Persians will actively avoid loud fountains, defeating the entire purpose of getting one in the first place.
  • Gentle, slow water flow: Unlike some cats that enjoy fast-moving water, Persians often prefer a subtle, calm flow that won't disturb their facial hair or create splashing that gets their face wet. Look for fountains with adjustable flow settings.
  • Easy to clean components: Given their long coats, Persians shed significantly, and their fountains need frequent cleaning to prevent hair clogs and bacteria buildup. Dishwasher-safe or tool-free assembly parts will save you time and frustration.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do cats prefer running water?

Cats instinctively prefer moving water because in nature, still water was more likely to be contaminated. Running water triggers their instinct to drink more, which is important for preventing urinary tract issues.

How often should you clean a pet water fountain?

Clean the fountain and change the filter every 2-4 weeks. In multi-pet households, clean weekly. Bacteria and biofilm build up quickly in stagnant areas.

Do pet water fountains use a lot of electricity?

No. Most pet water fountains use 2-5 watts — about the same as a phone charger. The annual electricity cost is typically under $5.

Are stainless steel or plastic pet fountains better?

Stainless steel is generally better — it resists bacteria growth, doesn't develop scratches that harbor germs, and is more durable. Plastic can cause chin acne in some cats.
