Frequently Asked Questions

How does the night vision actually work in complete darkness?

The Furbo uses infrared LEDs that emit light invisible to humans but visible to the camera sensor. In completely dark rooms, you get a clear grayscale image without any visible red glow that spooks dogs. The range is solid for a typical bedroom or crate area—roughly 15-20 feet with usable detail. It's not thermal imaging, so you're not seeing heat signatures, but you get structure, movement, and enough detail to identify what your dog is doing.

Does the two-way audio actually work for training, or is it gimmicky?

It works better than you'd expect. The 1-2 second latency is real and means it's not ideal for split-second timing, but it's plenty fast enough to interrupt unwanted behavior (barking, chewing) or call attention during the act. Many owners use it to interrupt counter-surfing or excessive barking before it escalates. It's not a replacement for in-person training, but it's genuinely useful for behavior management when you're remote.

What's the WiFi requirement? Will it work in older homes with weak signals?

The Furbo needs a stable 2.4GHz WiFi connection (not 5GHz). It's power-hungry for a camera, so weak signals cause lag or disconnections. If your router is in the basement and the camera is upstairs, you might struggle. Solution: either move your router, use a WiFi extender near the camera, or know that you'll accept occasional 5-10 second delays. It's not 4G-friendly if you're trying to use it on mobile data only—it needs home WiFi.
