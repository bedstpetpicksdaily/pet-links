Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work for kittens or only adult cats?

I'd recommend starting with the easiest difficulty level (Level 1) for cats under one year old, as kittens can get frustrated easily. The Catit Design is durable enough to grow with your kitten—just adjust the difficulty as they learn. Adult cats typically progress faster to higher levels.

Does this actually prevent overeating and weight gain?

Yes, but with a caveat: it slows eating speed dramatically (I observed 3–4x slower consumption), which helps prevent bloating and gives satiety signals time to register in the brain. However, it won't magically reduce portion size if you're still dumping excessive food. Use it alongside proper portion control for best results.

How much messier is this compared to a regular bowl?

Honestly? About the same, maybe slightly less because the maze design contains kibble better than a shallow dish would. Some cats do push pieces out while searching through the maze, but nothing unmanageable. A placemat underneath solves any scatter issues.

Can you use wet food in the Catit Senses 2.0?

Not recommended. The maze design is built specifically for kibble and treats. Wet food will clog the passages and create a food safety issue when it sits in the crevices. Stick to dry kibble or the occasional freeze-dried treat for best results.
