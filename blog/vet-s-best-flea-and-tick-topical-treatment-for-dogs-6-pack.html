How does Vet's Best compare to Advantage II or Frontline?

Advantage II and Frontline use imidacloprid (a neonicotinoid) that hits harder and faster on established infestations, while Vet's Best relies on essential oils for a gentler approach. For dogs with sensitive skin or as prevention, Vet's Best wins. For active severe infestations, the prescription options work more aggressively. Price-wise, Vet's Best is typically 40-50% cheaper per dose, but that reflects the ingredient potency difference.

Is this safe for puppies under 12 weeks?

The product is labeled for dogs 8 weeks and older, but the essential oil base means it's milder than many alternatives. Still, if your puppy is that young, a vet consultation is non-negotiable—some puppies react to even natural oils. Check the exact label on your pack; formulations can vary slightly by batch.

Does the 6-pack really last 6 months at one application per month?

Yes, mathematically. One dose per month for six months equals six applications. July through September (peak season) is where the math gets tested hardest. Budget for potential re-purchase mid-summer if you're in a high-parasite area.

Will my dog get greasy or matted coat from the topical application?

Minimal. The formula is designed to absorb into the skin rather than sit on the coat.
