Frequently Asked Questions

What weight can a dog actually carry in this backpack?

Generally, healthy dogs can safely carry 10-25% of their own body weight, though this varies by age, fitness level, and breed. A 60-pound Lab could reasonably handle 6-15 pounds; a 25-pound Cocker Spaniel should carry no more than 4-6 pounds. Ruffwear's saddlebag design helps distribute that weight, but overloading will cause joint strain and fatigue. Start light on your first hike and watch your dog's gait—if they're lagging or seem uncomfortable, reduce the load.

How does this compare to cheaper dog backpacks on Amazon?

Budget packs ($25-50) use thinner materials, weaker stitching, and poor weight distribution that can dig into your dog's shoulders. They work fine for short walks carrying a toy or treat pouch, but fail on rough terrain or longer hikes. Ruffwear's ballistic fabric and reinforced construction actually cost more to make, which is why the price is higher. If you're hiking 10+ miles or doing technical terrain, the durability difference is worth paying for. For neighborhood strolls, save the money.

Is this backpack waterproof or water-resistant?

It's water-resistant but not waterproof. The ballistic fabric sheds light rain and morning dew without soaking through immediately, but submerging it or exposing it to heavy rain will eventually let water seep in. If you're hiking in wet conditions, pack your dog's gear in waterproof stuff sacks inside the backpack, or invest in a rain cover if you're regularly hiking in serious precipitation.

Does my dog need to be trained to wear a backpack?

Most dogs adapt to it within a few wears, but some take longer. Start by letting them wear it empty around the house for 15-20 minutes to adjust to the feeling. On your first actual hike, keep the load very light—just 1-2 pounds—so they don't associate it with discomfort. Positive reinforcement (treats, praise) helps. If your dog is anxious, reactive, or has never worn a pack, budget an extra week or two of introduction before tackling a serious trail.
