3. Natural Options Have Finally Gone Mainstream

What to Look For

Essential Buying Criteria

  • Essential Oil Ingredients: Look for collars using cedarwood, lavender, eucalyptus, or lemongrass. These naturally repel fleas and ticks without neurotoxins. Check that they're derived from pure essential oils, not synthetic fragrances.
  • Dual Protection: The best natural collars work against both fleas AND ticks. Single-pest solutions leave gaps in protection—especially during August when tick activity peaks alongside flea season.
  • Duration & Coverage: Verify how long the collar remains effective (8 weeks minimum recommended) and whether it covers both cats and dogs. Some are species-specific due to sensitivity differences.
  • Safety Certifications: Look for third-party testing, veterinary approval, and non-toxic ingredient lists. With pets wearing these around their necks, you want documented safety standards, especially for households with children.
