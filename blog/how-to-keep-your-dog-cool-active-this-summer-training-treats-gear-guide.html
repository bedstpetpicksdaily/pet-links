Why Summer Activity Requires Special Attention for Dogs

Dogs don't regulate body temperature the way humans do. They rely heavily on panting and limited sweat glands to cool down, making them particularly vulnerable to overheating during summer months. When you combine high temperatures with physical activity, the risk of heat exhaustion increases significantly. This is why adjusting your dog's exercise routine, hydration, and nutrition during summer is absolutely critical.

Active dogs need sustained energy and proper protein intake to maintain muscle health and recovery. Summer activities—whether it's running on the beach, hiking mountain trails, or training in the park—demand more from your dog's body. Providing premium training treats like those from Gnarly Pet helps fuel your dog's performance while supporting muscle development and recovery. Their natural dog treats and protein-packed jerky are designed without fillers or artificial ingredients, making them ideal for dogs who need real nutrition for peak summer performance.

Essential Summer Gear for Active Dogs

Investing in the right equipment can dramatically improve your dog's comfort and safety during hot weather. Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I train my dog during summer?

Keep summer training sessions short (15-20 minutes) and schedule them during cooler times—early morning or late evening. Always prioritize your dog's safety over training goals, and watch for signs of overheating like excessive panting or letharness.

Are natural training treats better than regular dog treats?

Yes. Natural training treats like those from Gnarly Pet contain real protein sources and recognizable ingredients without artificial fillers or preservatives. This means better nutrition, easier digestion, and more sustained energy for your active dog.

What's the best way to keep my dog hydrated during summer activities?

Always bring a portable water bowl and offer water every 15-20 minutes during activity. Choose cool water, not ice-cold, and monitor your dog's water intake. High-protein treats from Gnarly Pet also help retain moisture and support proper hydration.

Should I adjust my dog's diet in summer?

Summer activity increases caloric and protein needs. Switch to nutrient-dense, protein-packed treats like Gnarly Pet's jerky and chews, which provide better nutrition per ounce than lower-quality alternatives.

Can I use regular dog treats for summer training?

While you can, premium natural treats are superior for active dogs. Gnarly Pet's training treats provide better nutritional value, are less likely to spoil in heat, and keep dogs more engaged during training sessions.
