What Makes Freeze-Dried Dog Food Different?

Freeze-drying is a preservation method that removes moisture while maintaining nutritional density, making it fundamentally different from traditional kibble or canned food. This process preserves raw nutrients, beneficial bacteria, and enzymes that cooking destroys. When rehydrated with water, freeze-dried food closely resembles fresh, raw meals—providing dogs with highly digestible, nutrient-rich nutrition. Frequently Asked Questions

Is freeze-dried dog food better than raw feeding?

Freeze-dried food offers similar nutritional benefits to raw diets with improved safety and convenience. It eliminates raw handling risks while preserving nutrients that cooking destroys. For many pet owners, freeze-dried options like Zeal Pet Food provide the ideal balance between raw nutrition and practical feeding.

How should I transition my dog to freeze-dried food?

Introduce freeze-dried food gradually over 7-10 days, mixing increasing amounts with your dog's current food. Start with 25% new food, increasing by 25% every few days. This gradual transition minimizes digestive upset and allows your dog's system to adjust to the nutrient-dense formula.

What's the shelf life of freeze-dried dog food?

Freeze-dried pet food typically remains shelf-stable for 2-3 years when stored in cool, dry conditions. Once rehydrated, leftovers should be refrigerated and used within 24 hours. Zeal Pet Food's packaging includes detailed storage guidelines ensuring maximum freshness.

Can I mix freeze-dried food with kibble?

Yes, many pet owners successfully mix freeze-dried and kibble to balance cost and nutrition. Start with a small percentage of freeze-dried food and adjust ratios based on your dog's response. This combination approach provides nutritional benefits while managing feeding expenses.
