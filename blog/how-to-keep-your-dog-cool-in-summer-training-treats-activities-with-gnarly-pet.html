Understanding Summer Heat & Dog Safety

Dogs are more susceptible to heat stress than humans because they can't regulate their body temperature as efficiently. Breeds with thick coats, flat faces, or underlying health conditions are particularly vulnerable. Signs of overheating include excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, and disorientation. The key to summer dog care is prevention: provide constant access to fresh water, create shaded resting areas, and limit intense exercise during peak heat hours (10 AM–4 PM). When you do take your dog out, consider investing in cooling gear like Gnarly Pet's dog collars and pet apparel, designed with both style and function in mind.

Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is too hot for dogs to exercise?

Avoid strenuous exercise when temperatures exceed 85°F (29°C), especially for seniors, puppies, or flat-faced breeds. Stick to early morning or late evening activity, and always watch for signs of overheating like excessive panting and lethargy.

Are Gnarly Pet treats safe for daily use during summer?

Yes, Gnarly Pet's natural training treats and chews are formulated for regular use as part of a balanced diet. Their protein-packed formula makes them ideal for frequent training rewards, especially during summer sessions. Just monitor overall calorie intake and ensure plenty of fresh water.

Can I freeze Gnarly Pet treats for summer enrichment?

Absolutely! Freezing Gnarly Pet's jerky treats or serving them in a frozen format provides extra cooling benefits and extended enrichment value. This is a great way to keep dogs engaged indoors while helping them stay cool during hot afternoons.

How do I know if my dog is overheating?

Signs of overheating include excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, vomiting, and disorientation. If you notice these symptoms, move your dog to a cool, shaded area immediately, offer fresh water, and contact your veterinarian if symptoms persist.
