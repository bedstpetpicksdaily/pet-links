Understanding Rawhide Chews: What You Should Know

Rawhide chews have been a staple in the dog treat market for decades. Made from the inner layer of cow or horse hides, rawhide is heavily processed and often bleached to achieve its signature appearance. While these chews are inexpensive and widely available, they come with several concerns that modern pet owners should understand.

The primary issues with rawhide include potential choking hazards, digestive blockages, and the use of harsh chemical treatments during processing. Many veterinarians have begun recommending alternatives, particularly for aggressive chewers or dogs with sensitive digestive systems.

Gnarly Pet dog jerky represents a new generation of high-protein dog treats designed with your pet's wellbeing in mind. Each treat is packed with premium protein to support muscle development and maintain healthy energy levels.

What sets Gnarly Pet apart is their commitment to transparency and quality. Nutritional Comparison: Protein Content and Benefits

When comparing nutritional profiles, Gnarly Pet dog jerky consistently outperforms traditional rawhide chews. Rawhide typically offers minimal nutritional value—it's primarily collagen and gelatin with little to no vitamins or minerals. In contrast, Gnarly Pet jerky delivers substantial protein content in every bite, supporting muscle maintenance and overall canine health.
