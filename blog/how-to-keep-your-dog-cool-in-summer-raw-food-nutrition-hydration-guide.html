Frequently Asked Questions

Can raw food help my dog stay cooler in summer?

Yes. Raw diets with high moisture content support better hydration and more efficient digestion, which generates less metabolic heat than processed kibble. This can help your dog maintain a more stable body temperature during warm weather.

How should I store Zeal Pet Food during summer?

Freeze-dried raw food should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Once prepared with water, consume within 24 hours and refrigerate any unused portions. Always keep raw ingredients properly stored to prevent spoilage in heat.

How much water should I add to freeze-dried meals?

Follow Zeal's packaging instructions, typically 1:1 or 1:2 ratio of food to water. Adjust based on your dog's preferences and summer hydration needs—extra water can increase the moisture content for added hydration benefits.

Is raw food safe for all dogs in summer?

Raw diets are generally safe for most healthy dogs. However, consult your veterinarian before switching diets, especially for puppies, senior dogs, or those with health conditions. Zeal Pet Food provides grain-free options suitable for various dietary needs.

What temperature should I serve raw food?

Many dogs prefer raw food served at room temperature or slightly cool. Avoid serving directly from the freezer in summer, as extreme cold contrasts can upset digestion. Let meals thaw naturally to a comfortable temperature first.
