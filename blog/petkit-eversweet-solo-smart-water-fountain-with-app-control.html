Frequently Asked Questions

Does the app work without WiFi, or do I need internet for basic fountain operation?

The fountain operates independently without WiFi—the app is purely optional. However, WiFi connectivity is required to access remote monitoring, water level alerts, and health insights. The fountain functions as a standard smart device when offline, though you lose the primary reason for the premium price. Most users keep WiFi enabled and experience reliable connectivity within 30 feet of the router.

How often do you actually need to replace the filters?

The activated carbon filter lasts 4-8 weeks depending on water hardness and pet count. I replaced mine every 6 weeks during testing. The pre-filter extends filter life significantly and should be rinsed weekly. Petkit sells replacement filters in packs of three for roughly $15-20. Skipping filter maintenance defeats the entire purpose of the smart system—water quality alerts become meaningless without fresh filters.

Will my cat actually use it, or will they ignore it like every other fountain?

That depends entirely on your cat's personality. The quiet operation helps—I observed that cats approached this fountain more frequently than louder alternatives, particularly anxious breeds. However, some cats simply prefer still water. The Eversweet Solo's appeal is observation through app data. You'll see exactly how often your cat drinks and can detect behavioral changes that signal health issues. This data matters more than guaranteed usage.

Is it worth upgrading from a basic fountain, or am I just paying for the app?

You're paying for quiet operation, better filtration, and health monitoring—the app is the enabler, not the entire value. If your current fountain is five years old or creates noticeable noise, the upgrade delivers genuine quality-of-life improvements. If you have a recent budget fountain that works fine, the price premium ($80-120 typical range) only justifies the switch if you want remote monitoring or have cats with hydration concerns your vet mentioned.
