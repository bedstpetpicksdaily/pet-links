Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Variable speed motor (6,000-8,000 RPM range) is genuinely useful. Lower speeds tackle thick, matted coats without stress; higher speeds handle fine detail work around the face and paws. Most budget clippers run one fixed speed, which means you're fighting the tool instead of working with it.
  • ✓ Cordless design with a 90-minute runtime covers a full medium-to-large dog grooming session without needing to hunt for an outlet or deal with cable management. The lithium-ion battery holds charge reasonably well over time—reviewers report consistent performance even after 18+ months of use.
  • ✓ Quiet operation compared to wired competitors. At around 65-70 decibels, anxious dogs are less likely to bail mid-grooming. This matters more than it sounds when you're trimming a Pomeranian's face or a nervous rescue dog.
  • ✓ Blade temperature stays manageable during extended use. The motor's efficiency means the blade rarely gets uncomfortably warm, reducing the risk of accidentally burning sensitive skin on ears or paw pads.
Cons
  • ✗ The charging dock design is awkward. It's proprietary and takes up more counter space than you'd expect. If you lose it, replacement charging solutions are limited—you can't just grab a USB-C cable and go.
  • ✗ Blade replacement costs add up quickly. Arf Pets blades run $15-25 per replacement, and if you're grooming weekly or dealing with heavily matted coats, you're looking at new blades every 6-8 weeks. This isn't mentioned upfront in the marketing materials.

Our Verdict

The Arf Pets Professional Cordless Dog Grooming Clipper Kit deserves its 4.3-star rating. It's a legitimate step up from the $40-60 budget clippers that frustrate most owners, but it's not a $300+ professional-grade tool. At the price point it typically sits (vary by vendor), it makes financial sense if you're grooming 2+ dogs regularly or if you've had a bad experience with cheaper alternatives. The variable speed motor alone justifies the premium over fixed-speed competitors. Where it stumbles—proprietary charging, ongoing blade costs—these are minor inconveniences, not dealbreakers. If you want to stop booking groomers every 6 weeks and actually maintain your dog's coat yourself, this kit gives you the capability to do it right. Just budget for blades and don't expect it to last forever without maintenance.

Frequently Asked Questions

How loud is this compared to professional grooming clippers?

The Arf Pets runs at approximately 65-70 decibels—quieter than most wired clippers in this price range. Professional salon clippers (the $400+ models) are typically 5-10 decibels quieter, but the difference is noticeable only to audiophiles. For home use, especially with noise-sensitive dogs, this is actually an advantage over wired alternatives.

Does the 90-minute battery life hold up long-term?

Yes, based on owner reports spanning 12+ months. Lithium-ion batteries do degrade gradually, so you'll likely see 80-85 minutes after a year of regular use instead of the full 90. If you're doing marathon grooming sessions, charge during breaks. Most single-dog grooming takes 45-60 minutes, so one charge covers it.

Can you use third-party blades, or are you locked into Arf Pets brand?

The clipper accepts standard A5-sized blades, which means you can technically use aftermarket options from other brands. However, Arf Pets blade compatibility isn't always guaranteed—many users report fitment issues with non-Arf blades. Stick with Arf Pets brand blades unless you're willing to experiment at the risk of damaging the clipper head. Generic blades might save $5 per blade, but it's not worth the hassle.
