What Are the Best Grain Free Dog Treats for Dogs?

Grain free dog treats are exactly what they sound like — snacks and training rewards made without common grains like wheat, corn, or soy. Instead, they use high-quality protein sources like real chicken, beef, salmon, or turkey, paired with wholesome ingredients like sweet potatoes, peas, or chickpeas. The idea is to keep things closer to what dogs would naturally eat, without the fillers that can sometimes cause digestive upset or allergic reactions in sensitive pups.

These treats come in all shapes and sizes — from soft, chewy bites perfect for training sessions to crunchy biscuits great for rewarding good behavior throughout the day. Most grain free options are also free from artificial preservatives, colors, and flavors, which is a huge win for pet owners who like to know exactly what they are feeding their four-legged family members. Frequently Asked Questions

Are grain free dog treats actually healthier?

Grain-free treats eliminate common allergens like wheat and corn, which benefit dogs with food sensitivities. However, healthy grains are fine for most dogs — grain-free is most important for dogs with diagnosed grain allergies.

What ingredients should I avoid in dog treats?

Avoid treats containing xylitol (toxic to dogs), artificial colors, BHA/BHT preservatives, propylene glycol, and unnamed 'meat by-products'. Always read the ingredient list.

How many treats can I give my dog per day?

Treats should make up no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. For most medium-sized dogs, that's 5-10 small treats per day depending on treat calorie content.

Are grain free treats good for training?

Yes. Small, soft grain-free treats work well for training because dogs find them highly rewarding and they're easy to consume quickly between commands.
