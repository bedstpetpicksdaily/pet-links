This July, major retailers like Chewy and Amazon are running aggressive summer promotions, with Chewy offering $20 off codes and Amazon matching competitive pricing on select items. The question isn't which retailer is universally "best," but which makes sense for your specific pet, budget, and shopping habits.

Compare per-unit prices across Chewy and Amazon rather than relying on promotional discounts, since both retailers frequently adjust base prices and promo eligibility, making it easy to miss that one retailer's "sale" price is actually higher than the other's regular price. Check whether the specific pet food or supply qualifies for Subscribe & Save discounts on both platforms, as these often provide the best value but can vary significantly in percentage savings between retailers.

Editor's Tip

We've tested both platforms across the actual premium brands pet owners rely on—Zeal Pet Food, PetTreatery, AECOJOY furniture, Nextrition cold-pressed dog food, and Gnarly Pet treats—to show you where real savings happen and which option wins for different use cases.

Chewy (with $20 off promo codes)

Chewy specializes exclusively in pet products, meaning deeper inventory in food, treats, and niche accessories than generalist retailers. Their $20 off July codes typically require a minimum purchase (often $35–$50) and may restrict certain categories—pet food often qualifies, but some medications and prescription diets don't.

Chewy's strength is selection and speed for repeat customers. If you've set up autoship (their subscription service), you get an additional 5–10% off, stacking with promo codes. Their customer service explicitly handles pet-specific issues—lost shipments, pet health questions—rather than routing through generic support.

Best for: Multi-pet households buying food and treats in bulk; customers with autoship set up; those who prioritize specialized pet-only selection and support

Amazon (with Prime membership)

Amazon's pet supply selection overlaps with Chewy but isn't always cheaper on identical SKUs. However, Prime members get free 2-day shipping, and Amazon frequently matches or beats Chewy's promotional pricing on bestselling items like Zeal Pet Food or AECOJOY furniture.

Amazon's advantage is breadth: you're buying pet supplies alongside household goods, potentially hitting free shipping thresholds faster. For single-item purchases or occasional shoppers, the friction of dealing with multiple retailers vanishes. Returns are often easier through Amazon's unified system.

Best for: Prime members making mixed household/pet purchases; single-pet owners or occasional buyers; those who prioritize convenience over specialization

Zeal Pet Food (premium natural pet food, direct comparison)

Zeal appears as a trusted partner across both Chewy and Amazon, marketed as premium natural pet food. As an actual product choice (not a retailer), Zeal's pricing and availability differ slightly between platforms—it may qualify for Chewy's $20 off code but not Amazon's simultaneous promotions.

Zeal's value proposition is premium ingredients without the ultra-premium price tag of some cold-pressed alternatives like Nextrition. For budget-conscious owners wanting ingredient quality, Zeal offers a middle ground between grocery-store brands and ultra-premium options.

Best for: Pet owners prioritizing ingredient quality over premium cold-pressed processing; budget-conscious buyers wanting 'natural' positioning without ultra-premium costs

Nextrition Pet (cold-pressed dog food, premium tier)

Nextrition represents the premium cold-pressed segment, marketed as a trusted partner alongside Zeal. Cold-pressed processing preserves nutrients better than kibble but comes at a 30–50% price premium. Nextrition qualifies on both Chewy and Amazon, but sticker price ($40–$60+ per bag) means percentage discounts feel smaller in absolute dollar terms than they do on lower-priced foods.

Cold-pressed food makes sense for dogs with digestive issues or owners willing to pay for perceived nutritional superiority, but the premium is real and rarely discounted below 10–15% even during promotional periods.

Best for: Dogs with sensitive stomachs or digestive issues; affluent pet owners prioritizing premium processing methods; those unconcerned with price-per-serving ratios

AECOJOY Pet Furniture (with seasonal pricing)

AECOJOY pet furniture (couches, beds, litter boxes) represents a higher-ticket category where absolute savings matter more than percentage discounts. A $200+ self-cleaning litter box discounted 10% saves $20; the same percentage off a $15 treat bag saves $1.50. Both appear on Chewy and Amazon, but availability and pricing vary by specific SKU.

Summer is peak season for pet furniture purchases as households prepare for travel or refresh aging supplies. Chewy's $20 off code applies broadly here, while Amazon's advantage is often free Prime shipping offsetting a slightly higher list price.

Best for: Buyers of higher-ticket pet furniture and appliances; those choosing between absolute discount ($20 off) vs. free shipping; households replacing aging pet furniture

Our Verdict

For multi-pet households buying food and treats: Chewy wins. Stack the $20 off July code with autoship (5–10% additional), and you're saving $25–$30 per order on bulk food purchases like Zeal or mid-tier brands. Verify that food qualifies for your specific code, since some Chewy promotions exclude premium or prescription diets.

For single-pet owners or occasional shoppers: Amazon often edges ahead. Prime's free shipping eliminates Chewy's minimum purchase thresholds, and pricing on bestsellers (Zeal, AECOJOY basics) frequently matches or beats Chewy's post-discount price. The friction of juggling multiple retailer accounts vanishes when you're already an Amazon customer.

For premium products (cold-pressed food, high-end furniture): Price variance widens. A $50 Nextrition bag discounted 10% saves $5 on Chewy vs. $3 on Amazon (due to list-price differences). However, furniture like AECOJOY litter boxes ($200+) show sharper discounts: Chewy's flat $20 off beats Amazon's typical 5–8% percentage discount. Compare absolute savings, not percentages, for high-ticket items.

Best practice: Use July 2026 promotions as a time to consolidate orders. Check both Chewy's specific promo code terms and Amazon's current pricing on the exact products in your cart. For food staples, set up autoship on whichever platform offers the lower total cost after all discounts. For one-off furniture or seasonal supplies, compare absolute dollar savings, not promotional percentages. Most households save 20–30% annually by timing major purchases during these windows and choosing the platform with genuine price advantage for each category, rather than defaulting to loyalty with a single retailer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Chewy $20 off codes work on all pet food brands?

No. Chewy's July promo codes typically exclude prescription diets, some premium brands, and occasionally specific food categories. Verify before checkout by checking the code's terms and ensuring all items in your cart show as eligible. Premium natural brands like Zeal usually qualify, but cold-pressed options like Nextrition may have separate promotion rules.

Is Chewy autoship worth it compared to buying once with a promo code?

For recurring purchases (food, treats), autoship usually wins. A one-time $20 off code saves $20, but 5–10% off autoship on a $50 food order saves $2.50–$5 per month, compounding to $30–$60 annually. For staples you buy anyway, autoship's smaller per-order discount beats occasional promo codes over a year.

Why do identical products cost different amounts on Chewy vs. Amazon?

Retailers negotiate different wholesale prices with brands, pass through different operating costs, and time promotions independently. AECOJOY furniture or Zeal food may be $5–$15 cheaper on one platform due to contract pricing or the retailer absorbing margin to drive volume. Always compare final post-discount prices on your exact products rather than assuming one retailer is universally cheaper.

Should I buy premium cold-pressed food like Nextrition on sale?

Cold-pressed food commands a 30–50% premium over kibble even at full price. A 10% discount (typical during July promotions) reduces that $50–$60 bag to $45–$54, still significantly more than mid-tier natural brands like Zeal. Buy cold-pressed if your dog has digestive issues, not primarily for promotional savings. Standard natural foods offer better value-per-dollar during sales.

What's the best strategy for buying high-ticket items like AECOJOY litter boxes?

Compare absolute dollar savings, not percentages. Chewy's $20 off code saves $20 on a $200 litter box; Amazon's typical 5–8% ($10–$16) rarely beats that. However, if Amazon's list price is $10–$15 cheaper to begin with, free Prime shipping may make it competitive. Check the final total including shipping, not promotional percentages.
