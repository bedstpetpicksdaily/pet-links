What Is the Best Brush for Golden Retriever?

The best brush for a Golden Retriever is one that tackles their thick, double-layer coat without causing discomfort or skin irritation. Golden Retrievers have a dense undercoat beneath a longer outer coat, which means shedding is constant — and without the right brush, mats and tangles can form quickly. The most highly recommended tools in 2026 include slicker brushes, undercoat rakes, and deshedding tools like the Furminator, each designed to reach deep into that undercoat and pull out loose fur before it ends up on your furniture.

What separates a great brush from a mediocre one comes down to pin design, handle ergonomics, and how gently it glides through dense fur. A good slicker brush with fine, angled pins can detangle the outer coat, while an undercoat rake or deshedding tool gets to the root of the shedding problem — literally. Many Golden Retriever owners find that using a combination of two brushes, one for daily maintenance and one for weekly deep grooming, keeps their dog's coat looking show-worthy year-round.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I brush my dog?

Short-haired dogs benefit from brushing 1-2 times per week. Long-haired and double-coated breeds should be brushed daily during shedding seasons and 3-4 times per week otherwise.

Do self-cleaning slicker brushes work?

Yes. The retractable bristle mechanism makes it easy to remove collected fur with a single button press — no more picking hair out of the bristles by hand.

Can I use a dog brush on cats?

Many slicker brushes are marketed for both dogs and cats. However, cats have more sensitive skin, so choose a brush with softer, more flexible bristles for feline use.

What is the best brush for a shedding dog?

Self-cleaning slicker brushes excel at removing loose fur from the topcoat. For undercoat shedding, combine with an undercoat rake or deshedding tool for best results.
