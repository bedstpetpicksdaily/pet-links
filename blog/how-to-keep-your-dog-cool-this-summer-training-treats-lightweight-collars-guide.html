Choose Natural, Protein-Packed Training Treats for Summer

When temperatures rise, the treats you choose for training matter significantly. Heavy, fat-laden treats can upset your dog's stomach and make them feel sluggish in the heat. Natural, high-protein training treats are ideal because they provide quick energy without weighing your dog down. Gnarly Pet offers protein-packed dog chews and jerky that are made from natural ingredients, free from artificial additives, and perfectly portioned for training sessions. These treats are lightweight and easy to digest, making them perfect for rewarding your dog during outdoor summer adventures without causing digestive distress.

Invest in Lightweight, Breathable Dog Collars

A heavy collar can trap heat around your dog's neck and cause discomfort during summer months. Lightweight collars made from breathable materials allow air circulation and reduce the risk of heat-related issues. Gnarly Pet's collection of dog collars and pet apparel is designed with summer comfort in mind, featuring materials that won't absorb excessive heat or moisture. Look for collars with reflective elements for visibility during early morning or evening walks when temperatures are cooler, and ensure the fit is snug enough to be secure but loose enough to allow two fingers to slip underneath.

Strategic Training During Cooler Hours

Summer training doesn't have to stop—you just need to be strategic about timing. Train your dog during early morning or late evening hours when temperatures are cooler and more manageable. Always bring fresh water, take frequent breaks, and use high-value rewards like Gnarly Pet's natural jerky treats to keep your dog motivated. These training treats are small and convenient, making them easy to carry on walks and perfect for positive reinforcement without overfeeding during hot weather.

Additional Summer Safety Tips

Beyond treats and collars, consider providing cooling options like dog-safe popsicles, access to shade, and maintaining a consistent hydration routine. Never leave your dog in a parked car, even with windows down, and watch for signs of heat exhaustion including excessive panting, drooling, or lethargy. When combined with proper nutrition from quality treats and comfortable, breathable gear from brands like Gnarly Pet, these practices create a comprehensive approach to summer dog care that keeps your pup safe and happy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are natural dog treats better for summer training?

Yes, natural dog treats like those from Gnarly Pet are easier to digest and don't contain fillers that can upset your dog's stomach in heat. They provide quick energy without heaviness, making them ideal for warm-weather training sessions.

What should I look for in a summer dog collar?

Look for lightweight materials, breathable fabrics, reflective elements for visibility, and proper fit that allows two fingers to slip underneath. Gnarly Pet offers collars designed specifically for comfort and safety during summer months.

How often should I give my dog training treats during summer?

Use treats sparingly during hot weather to avoid overfeeding. Keep training sessions short, focus on early morning or evening hours, and always have fresh water available. Protein-packed treats from Gnarly Pet are nutrient-dense, so smaller portions go further.

Can lightweight collars still be durable?

Absolutely. Quality lightweight collars from brands like Gnarly Pet are made from durable, breathable materials that withstand regular use while providing superior comfort in warm weather compared to heavy traditional collars.
