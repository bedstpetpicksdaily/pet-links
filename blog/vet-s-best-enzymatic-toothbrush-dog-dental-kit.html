Frequently Asked Questions

Is the enzymatic toothpaste safe if my dog swallows it?

Yes. Vet's Best formulated this paste specifically for dogs, meaning it's safe to ingest. The enzymes (glucose oxidase and lactoperoxidase) work on bacteria naturally present in the mouth, so swallowing small amounts during brushing won't cause harm. You're not applying fluoride-based human toothpaste that requires spitting.

How often should I brush my dog's teeth with this kit?

Ideally 5-7 times weekly for meaningful plaque reduction, though 3 times weekly shows measurable improvement within 4-6 weeks. Daily is optimal but unrealistic for most households. The enzymatic formula works gradually, so consistency matters more than frequency.

Which brush should I use for my dog's size?

Start with the finger brush for toy breeds under 20 pounds or puppies getting acclimated. Use the dual-headed brush for medium dogs (20-50 pounds) as your primary tool. The standard toothbrush handles large breeds better, though some dogs prefer the control of a finger brush regardless of size. Your dog's comfort matters most.

Will this eliminate bad dog breath?

Partially, if bad breath stems from plaque and bacteria. This kit addresses surface buildup effectively. However, persistent foul odor can signal deeper gum disease, oral tumors, or systemic health issues requiring veterinary diagnosis. Use this as a prevention tool, not a cure for established dental disease.

How does this compare to dental chews or water additives?

Direct brushing removes plaque mechanically plus enzymatically—far more effective than chews or additives alone. The combination approach (brushing + chews) works better than either method independently, but this kit handles the heavy lifting without relying on your dog to cooperate with chews.
