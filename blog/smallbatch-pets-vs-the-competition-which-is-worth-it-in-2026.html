Frequently Asked Questions

Is Smallbatch Pets raw food safe for dogs?

Yes, Smallbatch Pets' raw meals are made from USDA-approved proteins and formulated with food safety protocols. Raw feeding is safe when sourced from reputable manufacturers like Smallbatch Pets. Always handle frozen raw meals with proper storage and hygiene practices, just as you would human food.

How does Smallbatch Pets compare to The Farmer's Dog?

Both are premium brands, but they differ in approach. Smallbatch Pets offers raw, freeze-dried, and lightly cooked options, while The Farmer's Dog focuses exclusively on fresh, lightly cooked meals. Smallbatch provides more variety, while The Farmer's Dog offers convenience with direct delivery. The choice depends on your pet's needs and your feeding philosophy.

Can I transition my pet to Smallbatch Pets gradually?

Absolutely. We recommend a gradual transition over 7-10 days, mixing increasing amounts of Smallbatch Pets food with their current diet. This prevents digestive upset. Smallbatch Pets' website provides detailed transition guides for switching from kibble to raw, freeze-dried, or lightly cooked meals.

Is Smallbatch Pets appropriate for all dog breeds and ages?

Smallbatch Pets offers formulas designed for different life stages, though their primary focus is adult dogs. Senior dogs and puppies can eat Smallbatch products, but consult your veterinarian for puppies under one year to ensure appropriate calcium and phosphorus ratios for proper bone development.

How do I store Smallbatch Pets frozen meals?

Store frozen meals in your freezer at 0°F or below. Most frozen raw meals last 6-12 months when properly stored. Thaw meals in the refrigerator 24 hours before feeding. This requires dedicated freezer space, which is worth planning for before ordering.
