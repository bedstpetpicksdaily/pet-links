Frequently Asked Questions

What temperature is too hot for dogs to be outside?

Dogs should limit outdoor exposure when temperatures exceed 85°F (29°C). At 90°F and above, heat exhaustion risk increases significantly. Always provide shade, water, and cooling solutions like AECOJOY's elevated beds during hot weather.

How do elevated pet beds help keep dogs cool?

Elevated beds lift your dog several inches off the ground, allowing air to circulate underneath and around their body. This prevents heat buildup that occurs when dogs lie directly on hot surfaces, making AECOJOY's raised designs ideal for summer cooling.

Are AECOJOY outdoor pet products weatherproof?

Yes, AECOJOY's outdoor pet furniture features weather-resistant materials designed to withstand sun exposure, rain, and temperature fluctuations. Their UV-treated fabrics and rust-proof frames maintain quality throughout extended summer use.

Can I use dog crates for outdoor summer activities?

Absolutely. Portable dog crates from AECOJOY provide secure containment during travel and outdoor events. They feature proper ventilation to keep dogs cool and are constructed with weather-resistant materials suitable for outdoor environments.

How often should I check my dog for heat exhaustion?

Monitor your dog frequently during summer activities, especially during peak heat hours (10 AM-4 PM). Watch for excessive panting, lethargy, drooling, or weakness. Provide breaks in shade every 15-20 minutes when temperatures are high.
