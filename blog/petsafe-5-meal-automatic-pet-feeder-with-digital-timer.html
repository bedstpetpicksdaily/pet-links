Can it actually keep food fresh in July heat without refrigeration?

Not indefinitely. Dry kibble stays fresh longer than wet food (2-3 days unrefrigerated in 80-degree heat), but compartments aren't sealed or insulated. If you live somewhere hot and humid, compartments can develop mold after 48 hours. For July feeding in warm climates, limit compartment-filling to 3-4 days maximum, or store the feeder in a cool spot indoors and only use it for immediate meals.
