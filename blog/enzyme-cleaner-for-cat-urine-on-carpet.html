Frequently Asked Questions

Does enzyme cleaner actually remove cat urine smell from carpet?

Yes, enzyme cleaners are the most effective solution for eliminating cat urine odor because they contain biological enzymes that break down the uric acid crystals responsible for the smell, rather than just masking it. Unlike regular cleaners, enzymes actually digest the odor-causing molecules at a molecular level, so the smell doesn't return when the carpet gets humid or wet. For best results, allow the enzyme cleaner to soak into the carpet for at least 10-15 minutes before blotting dry.

How long do you leave enzyme cleaner on cat urine in carpet?

You should leave enzyme cleaner on the affected area for a minimum of 10-15 minutes, but for dried or set-in urine stains, leaving it for 30 minutes to an hour produces significantly better results. Some heavily soaked areas may require you to cover the treated spot with plastic wrap and let it sit overnight to give the enzymes enough time to fully break down the uric acid. Never use heat or a steam cleaner to speed up drying, as heat permanently sets urine odor into carpet fibers.

Can I use enzyme cleaner on old dried cat urine stains in carpet?

Yes, enzyme cleaners work on old, dried cat urine stains, but they require more time and sometimes multiple applications compared to fresh stains. You should re-wet the stained area with clean water first before applying the enzyme cleaner, as this helps reactivate the dried urine crystals and allows the enzymes to penetrate deeper into the carpet padding. For stains that have been set for weeks or months, you may need to repeat the treatment two or three times to fully eliminate the odor.
