Frequently Asked Questions

How does this compare to professional cordless clippers used in grooming salons?

Arf Pets occupies the serious-hobbyist tier, not the professional tier. Salon clippers (like Andis or Wahl's professional lines) cost $300-600 and include superior blade quality, faster motors (5,000+ RPM vs. Arf's 3,500-4,000 RPM typical range), and warranties that cover commercial use. Arf Pets is engineered for home use—you'll notice the difference on thick undercoats or severely matted fur, but for maintenance grooming, the gap is narrower than the price difference.

Will this work on thick-coated dogs like Golden Retrievers or Huskies?

Yes, but with caveats. The heavy-duty motor handles thickness better than budget models, and the multiple blade sizes give you options. However, the motor heat issue becomes real on double-coated breeds during long sessions. Plan for shorter grooming windows (20-30 minute stretches) with cooldown breaks. Many owners with thick-coated dogs pair this with a corded clipper for heavy-duty stripping and use the cordless for finishing work—that's the honest sweet spot for this kit's capabilities.

Is the included charging dock reliable, or should I replace it?

The dock works fine for basic charging, but it's not magnetic or particularly premium feeling. Reviewers report occasional connection issues after 12+ months of use, though this isn't widespread. For $15-25, you could upgrade to an aftermarket dock with better contacts if you want insurance, but most owners don't need to. Just make sure you're charging in a dry environment—moisture in the contacts is the real culprit when charging problems emerge.
