Frequently Asked Questions

Are premium pet treats suitable for dogs or cats with food allergies?

Yes, but only if the ingredient list is examined carefully. Premium brands formulate without common fillers and artificial additives, which can help. However, you must check whether the specific proteins or ingredients match your pet's known allergies or sensitivities. A premium brand doesn't automatically guarantee compatibility with food-sensitive pets.

How should I compare ingredient quality between premium brands?

Check ingredient list order on the package: meat or meat meals appearing in the first few ingredients indicate higher protein content. Avoid brands relying heavily on grains or unnamed fillers early in the list. Cross-reference multiple brands at the same price point to identify which offers superior sourcing without premium markup inflation.

Does premium pricing guarantee better quality or sourcing?

No. Premium pricing doesn't always correlate with higher quality or better sourcing. Compare the actual ingredient lists, verify AAFCO certification or third-party testing labels, and calculate price-per-ounce to ensure you're not overpaying for smaller portions simply marketed as premium.

Can I use premium treats like PetTreatery during pet training?

Yes, premium treats work well as training rewards. However, adjust the treat size and overall daily portion to avoid exceeding your pet's caloric needs. Premium treats may be smaller or richer than mass-market alternatives, so measure carefully.

Where can I buy PetTreatery versus established competitors?

PetTreatery availability is primarily online, while established premium competitors may have wider local pet store presence. If immediate availability and in-store selection matter, traditional premium brands may offer more retail convenience. Online sourcing requires planning ahead for delivery times.
