Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the battery actually last on a full charge?

Real-world runtime sits around 45-50 minutes on a single charge for continuous grooming. This varies based on blade density—working through matted fur drains the battery faster than light maintenance. I'd recommend charging after each pet if you're grooming multiple dogs in succession.

Are the 5 guard combs really enough for different coat types?

Yes, they cover the essentials. The range runs from shorter guards for cleanup and sanitary trims to longer options for fluffier finishes. However, if you're working with unusual coat types (like doodles with thick undercoats), you might want supplementary specialty blades beyond what's included.

Does the clipper get hot enough to burn my dog's skin?

Not burn-level hot, but definitely warm enough to notice. After 25-30 minutes of continuous use, the blade becomes too warm for comfort. I'd pause for 5 minutes every half-hour during grooming sessions. This is normal for cordless models but worth knowing if you're used to corded clippers.

Is this better than cheaper cordless clippers under $50?

Marginally, yes. Budget cordless options sacrifice battery reliability and blade quality. The Arf Pets model holds a charge more consistently and delivers steadier blade speed. You're paying for reliability—whether that justifies the price depends on how often you groom.

Will my noise-sensitive dog tolerate this?

Probably not without acclimation work. This clipper runs at roughly 75-80 dB, which registers as loud to anxious pets. If your dog already struggles with vacuum cleaners or hair dryers, invest time in desensitization before expecting a calm grooming experience.
