What Makes Cold-Pressed Dog Food Superior?

Cold-pressing is a manufacturing process that gently prepares raw ingredients at low temperatures, preserving enzymes, vitamins, and nutrients that traditional high-heat kibble processing destroys. This method results in food that's closer to a dog's ancestral diet, promoting better digestion, shinier coats, and improved energy levels. During summer months, when dogs are more active outdoors, the enhanced bioavailability of cold-pressed nutrition becomes even more valuable.

Nextrition Pet specializes in this premium approach, using whole-food ingredients like human-grade proteins, vegetables, and fruits. Frequently Asked Questions

Is cold-pressed dog food better than traditional kibble?

Cold-pressed dog food preserves more nutrients, enzymes, and vitamins through gentle processing, making it more digestible and bioavailable than heat-processed kibble. Most dogs show improved coat quality, energy levels, and digestion within 2-4 weeks of switching.

How does Nextrition Pet compare to raw or freeze-dried brands?

Nextrition Pet's cold-pressed format offers similar nutritional benefits to raw food but with better shelf stability, easier handling, and lower cost. Unlike freeze-dried options, cold-pressed food requires no rehydration and maintains gut-health benefits through natural probiotics.

Is cold-pressed dog food safe for dogs with sensitive stomachs?

Yes. Cold-pressed dog food is often recommended for sensitive stomachs because the low-heat process preserves digestive enzymes and beneficial bacteria. Nextrition Pet's gut-healthy formulations with added probiotics make them an excellent choice for dogs with digestive issues.

Can I mix cold-pressed food with other dog foods?

Yes, you can gradually transition your dog to cold-pressed food or mix it with their current diet. Nextrition Pet recommends a 7-10 day transition period to avoid digestive upset, mixing increasing amounts of cold-pressed food with their regular diet.
