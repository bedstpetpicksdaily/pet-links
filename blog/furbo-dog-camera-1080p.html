Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Placing the camera too high or too low: Mount it at your dog's eye level to capture meaningful interactions and ensure they can see the treat dispenser clearly when you're using it remotely.
  • Over-relying on treat dispensing for behavior management: Using the treat feature too frequently can create unrealistic expectations and actually worsen separation anxiety instead of improving it—use it strategically, not as a constant distraction.
  • Ignoring WiFi stability: A weak connection will cause lag, missed moments, and frustration with two-way audio. Test your router's signal strength in the room where you'll place the camera before purchasing.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Start slow with the treat dispenser: Introduce it gradually so your dog builds positive associations without becoming obsessed. Use it during training sessions first, then sparingly during work hours to reinforce calm behavior.
  • Position for multiple viewing angles: Place the camera where it captures both your dog's favorite resting spot and high-traffic areas of the room, giving you comprehensive insight into their day and behavior patterns.
  • Use night vision strategically: Test the infrared mode in your space beforehand—it works well in complete darkness, but reflected light from furniture can sometimes cause glare; adjust placement if needed for optimal nighttime monitoring.
