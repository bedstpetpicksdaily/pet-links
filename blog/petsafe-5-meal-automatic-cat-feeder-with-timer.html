Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work with wet food or only dry kibble?

Dry kibble only. The motor mechanism relies on gravity and disk rotation, so wet food will gunk up the works within hours. Some owners have tried freezing wet food, but that defeats the purpose of automatic feeding. Stick to quality dry food, or use this for dry portions and hand-feed wet meals separately.

How loud is the motor when it rotates to the next meal?

Quieter than most people expect—think soft mechanical click rather than loud beep. It won't wake you from a deep sleep, but light sleepers might notice it. The rotation takes about 2-3 seconds total. This is actually one area where it outperforms more expensive electronic feeders with noisy servo motors.

Can multiple cats use this feeder, or will they fight over portions?

Technically yes, but practically? Only if they're already trained to eat together without conflict. The compartments portion out exactly enough for the scheduled time, so if one cat eats two meals' worth before the next rotation, the other cat gets nothing. Better approach: one feeder per cat, or use this for one cat and hand-feed the other. July is actually a good time to set up separate feeding stations before the stress of back-to-school chaos hits households.
