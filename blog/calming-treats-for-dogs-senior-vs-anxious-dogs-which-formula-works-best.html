Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between L-theanine, chamomile, and valerian root for dog anxiety?

L-theanine promotes relaxation without sedation by increasing calming neurotransmitters. Chamomile is gentler and better for mild anxiety or sensitive systems. Valerian root is stronger but can cause drowsiness—better for nighttime or severe anxiety. Brands combining multiple ingredients (synergistic effects) work better than single-ingredient treats.

Why do senior dogs need different calming treats than anxious younger dogs?

Senior dogs experience multiple simultaneous issues: anxiety plus joint stiffness, digestive sensitivity, and slower metabolism. A formula addressing only anxiety ignores these compounding problems. Senior-specific treats add glucosamine, chondroitin, and probiotics while keeping calories lower to prevent weight gain that worsens joint problems.

Does texture actually matter, or is it just marketing?

Texture is functional, not marketing. Anxious dogs under stress physically struggle to chew hard treats—soft formats ensure they ingest the calming ingredients during peak anxiety. Senior dogs with dental sensitivity or missing teeth can't process crunchy treats. Soft textures aren't luxuries; they're delivery mechanisms.

Is third-party testing worth paying extra for?

Yes. Third-party testing certifies that ingredient potency matches label claims and detects contaminants. Brands without it may have inconsistent or false potency levels, meaning you're paying for relief that doesn't arrive. It's insurance against wasted money.

Can I use the same calming treat for both my anxious young dog and my senior dog?

Not optimally. Your anxious younger dog won't benefit from glucosamine they don't need (wasted cost). Your senior dog will lack joint and digestive support with an anxiety-only formula. Single-problem formulas cost less and work better than trying one product to solve two different situations.
