Choosing the Right Summer Dog Collar for Heat & Comfort

A quality dog collar is more than just an ID holder—it's a key piece of summer gear that impacts your dog's comfort and safety. During hot months, traditional heavy collars can trap heat and restrict airflow around your dog's neck. Look for collars made from breathable, lightweight materials that won't absorb excess heat or moisture. Mesh collars, nylon weaves, and reflective designs are ideal choices that allow air circulation while providing visibility during evening walks.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes summer collars different from regular dog collars?

Summer collars are designed with breathable materials that prevent heat buildup and allow airflow around your dog's neck. They're typically lighter, often made from mesh or nylon, and may include reflective elements for visibility during early morning or evening walks.

Are high-protein treats safe to give my dog during summer?

Yes, high-protein treats are excellent for active dogs, especially during summer. They support muscle recovery and provide sustained energy. However, ensure treats don't replace regular meals and always provide fresh water, as protein metabolism requires adequate hydration.

How often should I use training treats like Gnarly Pet's products?

Training treats should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. Gnarly Pet's natural, protein-packed treats are perfect for daily training and rewards without overfeeding. Adjust your dog's regular meal portions if adding treats to their diet.

Do all dogs need summer apparel?

Not all dogs need apparel, but light-colored or short-haired breeds, senior dogs, and those with sensitive skin benefit greatly from UV protection. Lightweight, breathable apparel also helps prevent overheating during extended outdoor activities.

What are the best natural dog treats for active dogs?

The best natural dog treats are protein-rich, free from artificial additives, and easy to digest. Gnarly Pet's jerky, chews, and protein-packed treats meet these criteria, making them ideal for active dogs that need muscle support and sustained energy.
