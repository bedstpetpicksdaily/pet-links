Frequently Asked Questions

What size dog crate do I need for a large breed?

For large breeds, you'll want a crate that's at least 42-48 inches long, 28-30 inches wide, and 30-32 inches tall, allowing your dog to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably. A good rule of thumb is to measure your dog's length from nose to tail and add 4 inches, then choose a crate that accommodates that measurement. Oversized crates (48-54 inches) are ideal for giant breeds like Great Danes or Mastiffs.

Are plastic or metal dog crates better for large dogs?

Metal crates are generally better for large breeds because they're more durable, easier to clean, and provide better ventilation and visibility. Plastic crates can work but may not withstand the weight and strength of large dogs that test their boundaries, potentially becoming damaged or unsafe. Metal crates also tend to last longer and are easier to transport or store when not in use.

How much weight can a large dog crate hold?

Heavy-duty large dog crates typically support dogs weighing 70-150+ pounds, depending on the quality and construction of the crate. Always check the manufacturer's weight rating before purchasing, as standard crates may collapse or become unsafe with extra-large or XL breeds. Look for crates with reinforced frames and heavy-gauge metal if you have a dog over 100 pounds.

Should I get a crate with a divider for my large breed puppy?

Yes, a divider is highly recommended for large breed puppies since they grow quickly and you'll want to adjust the crate space as they develop. A divider prevents puppies from using one section as a bathroom and another for sleeping, which helps with housebreaking. You can purchase crates with removable dividers or buy them separately for most standard large crates.
