Why Raw Dog Food is Ideal for Summer

Raw dog food offers several advantages during warmer months when dogs are more active. Unlike heavily processed kibble, raw diets are packed with bioavailable nutrients that dogs digest more efficiently, meaning less energy is wasted on digestion and more is available for outdoor activities. Raw food also tends to have higher moisture content, which helps keep dogs hydrated—crucial during summer when heat stress is a concern. Additionally, many raw food formulas are grain-free, reducing inflammation and supporting optimal digestive health, which translates to better performance and stamina.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw dog food safe for summer feeding?

Yes, raw dog food is perfectly safe for summer when handled properly. Ensure it's stored in the freezer, thaw only what you'll use immediately, and maintain proper food hygiene practices. Zeal's freeze-dried raw format actually simplifies storage and reduces handling concerns while preserving nutritional integrity.

Can raw dog food help with summer heat stress?

Raw dog food's higher moisture content and easier digestibility can help support hydration and reduce digestive strain during hot months. Additionally, the anti-inflammatory benefits of grain-free raw diets may help some dogs handle summer heat more comfortably.

How much raw food should I feed my active dog in summer?

Active dogs may require more calories during summer months. Most vets recommend 2-3% of body weight daily for adult dogs, but highly active pets may need 3-4%. Consult your veterinarian about adjusting portions based on your dog's specific activity level and metabolism.
