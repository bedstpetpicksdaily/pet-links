Frequently Asked Questions

How does the laser feature work, and is it safe?

The PetDual projects a laser pattern across the floor and walls during play sessions. Most cats love it, and the laser is eye-safe at normal distances. However, some cats obsess over lasers and can develop anxiety if they can never 'catch' the light. If your cat already fixates on laser pointers, this feature might frustrate rather than entertain. You can typically disable the laser and use just the ball launcher if needed.

Can the ball launcher be used without the automatic timer?

Yes. The device includes manual and automatic modes. Manual mode lets you trigger the launcher whenever you want, which is helpful if your cat loses interest in automatic play or if you want more control over playtime. Many owners use manual mode in the evening when they're home and automatic mode during work hours.

Is the PetDual worth the price compared to cheaper alternatives?

Only if you value the dual features and automation. A basic $15–20 ball launcher works just fine for most cats and lasts years. The PetDual's extra cost ($40–60+ depending on sales) buys you the laser feature, better build quality, and smart timer controls. For budget-conscious shoppers: if your cat is content with simple toys, the cheaper option wins. If your cat needs more stimulation or you want hands-free play while you work, the extra investment pays off. Check reviews for your specific price at purchase—deals shift weekly on Amazon.

How many balls come with it, and are replacements expensive?

Most PetDual packages include 2–3 balls. Replacement balls are affordable (usually $5–8 for a pack) and widely available, so you won't be locked into overpriced proprietary accessories. The balls are standard plastic or rubber, so they hold up well with regular use.

Will this work for multiple cats, or just one?

It works best with one cat at a time. If you have multiple cats, they'll compete for the ball, and the smaller cat often gets squeezed out. However, running it multiple times daily (morning and evening sessions) helps both cats get play time without conflict. Some owners rotate which cat uses it, while others run it when their cats are in separate rooms.
