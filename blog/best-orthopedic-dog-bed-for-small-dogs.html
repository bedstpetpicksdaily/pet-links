What to Look For

  • Memory foam thickness (at least 3-4 inches): Small dogs need adequate cushioning to relieve pressure on their joints, but the bed shouldn't be so thick that it's difficult for arthritic dogs to climb in and out. Look for high-density memory foam that contours to your dog's body without being overly soft or squishy.
  • Washable, removable covers: Small dogs spend significant time on their beds, and accidents happen. A cover you can remove and machine wash keeps the bed fresh and hygienic, while protecting the foam core underneath from damage and extending the bed's lifespan.
  • Size proportionate to your dog (not oversized): A bed that's too large doesn't provide the security and support small dogs crave. Your dog should be able to stretch out fully but also curl up with the sides providing gentle containment—typically 24-30 inches long for most small breeds.

What to Avoid

  • Overly large "one-size-fits-all" orthopedic beds: A 40-inch bed might seem like good value, but it overwhelms small dogs, provides no sense of security, and wastes money on excess material. Small dogs specifically benefit from appropriately-sized beds that let them feel contained and protected while still offering enough room to stretch.
  • Cheap memory foam that collapses within months: Budget orthopedic beds sometimes use low-density foam that flattens in weeks, completely defeating the purpose. This is false economy—you'll end up replacing the bed sooner and your dog won't get the support they need in the meantime.
  • Non-removable covers: While slightly cheaper upfront, beds with glued-on covers become impossible to clean properly and will deteriorate quickly with a small dog's daily use. The cost savings disappear when you have to replace the entire bed after a year.

    • Frequently Asked Questions

    Are orthopedic dog beds worth it?

    Yes, especially for senior dogs, large breeds, and dogs with joint issues. Memory foam distributes weight evenly and relieves pressure on hips and elbows — vets widely recommend them for dogs over 7 years old.

    How thick should an orthopedic dog bed be?

    At least 3-4 inches of memory foam is recommended for effective joint support. Thinner foam compresses too easily and loses its orthopedic benefit.

    Are orthopedic dog beds machine washable?

    Most quality orthopedic dog beds have removable, machine-washable covers. The foam insert itself should not go in the washing machine.

    What size orthopedic dog bed does my dog need?

    Your dog should be able to stretch out fully without hanging off the edge. Measure your dog from nose to tail and add 6-12 inches for the ideal bed size.
