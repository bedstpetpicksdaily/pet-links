Bone Broth Treats for Dogs in 2026: Freeze-Dried vs. Liquid Options Compared

✓Last updated: July 20, 2026
By Best Pet Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

Bone broth has become a staple in canine nutrition, offering concentrated doses of collagen, glucosamine, amino acids, and minerals that support joint health, digestive function, and coat quality. But the category has fractured into distinct product formats—freeze-dried cubes, raw liquid broths, and lightly cooked options—each with different convenience profiles, nutritional density, and use cases.

This guide compares the actual product formats available in 2026, examines what separates premium bone broth treats from mediocre alternatives, and walks through the real trade-offs so you can choose the right option for your dog's age, activity level, and your household's needs.

Freeze-Dried Bone Broth Cubes

Freeze-dried formats concentrate nutrients by removing water while preserving the original broth structure. These products are shelf-stable, require no refrigeration, and dissolve quickly in water or can be served as standalone treats. Smallbatch Pets' freeze-dried bone broth cubes exemplify this category—they retain maximum nutritional density while offering straightforward storage and flexible serving options (mix into kibble, serve as a chew, or rehydrate).

Best for: Dogs whose owners prioritize convenience and storage flexibility; multi-pet households; travel; mixing into dry kibble; portion control.

Raw Liquid Bone Broth

Raw, minimally processed bone broth maintains the full collagen and amino acid profile but requires refrigeration or freezing. Smallbatch Pets offers raw bone broth alongside their lightly cooked variants, both sourced from grass-fed, pasture-raised animals and slow-simmered to extract maximum nutrients. Raw formats appeal to owners following whole-food diets for their dogs and those who can manage storage constraints.

Best for: Dogs on raw or whole-food diets; owners with freezer space; those seeking maximum nutritional bioavailability; senior dogs and dogs with joint concerns.

Lightly Cooked Liquid Bone Broth

A middle ground between raw and fully processed options, lightly cooked bone broth is gently heated to ensure safety while preserving most nutrients. Smallbatch Pets' lightly cooked meal options incorporate bone broth as a key component. This format offers reduced pathogen risk compared to raw while maintaining higher nutrient retention than heavily processed alternatives, with refrigeration requirements similar to raw products.

Best for: Dogs with sensitive digestive systems; owners seeking a safety-conscious middle ground; those mixing broth into prepared meals; puppies and immunocompromised dogs.

Frozen Bone Broth Treats (Summer Cooling Format)

Bone broth frozen into cubes or popsicle formats addresses seasonal needs—providing natural hydration, electrolytes, and behavioral engagement during hot months. The collagen promotes skin and coat health, while amino acids like glycine and proline support joint function. When frozen, these treats double as chewing activities that naturally cool dogs from the inside out without artificial ingredients or fillers.

Best for: Summer months; senior dogs needing joint support; dogs prone to overheating; active breeds; behavioral enrichment; naturally hydration-conscious owners.

  • ✓ Minimal ingredient lists (bones + water ideally) with no artificial preservatives, fillers, or additives that upset sensitive digestion
  • ✓ Multiple formats (freeze-dried, raw, lightly cooked, frozen) allow matching to your dog's diet style and your household's storage/convenience constraints

Cons

  • ✗ Risk of contamination with raw products if not handled properly; lightly cooked formats still carry minor pathogen risk vs. fully pasteurized alternatives

Our Verdict

For maximum convenience and shelf stability: Freeze-dried bone broth cubes win. They require no refrigeration, allow flexible serving (rehydrate, mix into kibble, or serve dry), and deliver concentrated nutrition in small, portion-controlled packages. Ideal for multi-pet households or travel.

For dogs on whole-food or raw diets: Raw liquid bone broth from reputable suppliers (like Smallbatch Pets' raw offerings) preserves the highest nutrient bioavailability and fits seamlessly into existing meal structures. This format is non-negotiable for raw-feeding owners but demands freezer space and careful handling.

For safety-conscious owners with digestive-sensitive dogs: Lightly cooked bone broth splits the difference—it's safer than raw (reduced pathogen risk) while retaining more nutrients than fully processed alternatives. It works well mixed into prepared meals or for puppies.

For summer cooling and joint support: Frozen bone broth cubes serve a dual purpose: natural hydration and electrolytes during heat plus behavioral engagement and joint-supporting amino acids. This format is particularly valuable for senior dogs and active breeds.

Regardless of format chosen: Always verify the ingredient list contains only bones and water (or minimal safe additives), confirm sourcing from grass-fed, pasture-raised animals, and check for absence of salt, onions, garlic, and xylitol. Premium brands that undergo rigorous safety testing justify their higher price through quality and consistency.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are bone broth treats safe for all dogs?

Bone broth is generally safe for dogs of all ages and sizes when sourced properly. However, avoid products containing added salt, onions, garlic, or xylitol—all toxic to dogs. Dogs with severe kidney disease or specific dietary restrictions should be cleared by a veterinarian first. Raw formats carry minor pathogen risk; lightly cooked or freeze-dried options are safer for immunocompromised dogs and puppies.

How often should I give my dog bone broth treats?

Bone broth can be a daily supplement as part of a balanced diet. Most veterinarians recommend starting with small amounts (1–2 tablespoons of liquid or a few freeze-dried cubes daily) and monitoring digestion. Since broth is nutrient-dense, it shouldn't replace core meals but complement them. Adjust frequency based on your dog's overall caloric intake and any digestive changes.

What's the difference between freeze-dried and liquid bone broth?

Freeze-dried broth removes water through a specialized process, concentrating nutrients and extending shelf life indefinitely without refrigeration. Liquid broth (raw or lightly cooked) retains water and requires refrigeration or freezing but may preserve slightly higher nutrient bioavailability. Freeze-dried is more portable and convenient; liquid is less processed but demands storage management.

Can bone broth treats help with arthritis in senior dogs?

Yes. Bone broth contains collagen, glucosamine, and amino acids (like glycine and proline) that directly support joint function and reduce inflammation. Senior dogs with arthritis often benefit from consistent bone broth supplementation. Results aren't immediate—expect 2–4 weeks of regular use before noticeable improvement in mobility and stiffness, particularly when combined with veterinary pain management.

What should I look for when choosing a bone broth brand?

Prioritize: (1) minimal ingredient lists (bones + water, ideally); (2) sourcing from grass-fed, pasture-raised animals; (3) no artificial preservatives, fillers, or additives; (4) transparent sourcing and supplier information; (5) veterinary approval or third-party testing certifications; (6) clear labeling of broth source (beef, chicken, fish). Reputable brands often cost more but ensure safety and actual nutritional density.
