Frequently Asked Questions

Does the GPS collar work without a base station, or do you need WiFi?

The GPS tracking operates independently through cellular data (LTE-M or NB-IoT networks), so it doesn't require your home WiFi. The boundary enforcement uses the base station's wireless signal, but tracking functions anywhere your dog roams. I tested this by letting my dog wander three houses down the street during testing, and the app pinpointed her location clearly without WiFi present. You will need a cellular plan, which PetSafe bundles as a subscription (typically $8-12/month), but this is separate from your home internet.

What happens if my dog leaves the boundary—is it an instant shock or warning beep first?

The system defaults to a warning beep followed by vibration before any correction. The collar emits a warning tone when your dog approaches the boundary, giving them a chance to turn back without correction. If they cross, the vibration activates, then correction follows if they continue. I watched my golden retriever respond to just the beep most times. This staged approach is humane and genuinely effective for training, though impatient dogs (like my husky) learned she could power through all three stages. The beep-first design appeals to newer dog owners who value gentleness over absolute containment.

Can you create multiple zones, or just one containment area?

The system creates one primary boundary circle or custom shape per base station. However, you can purchase additional base stations ($200 range) to establish separate zones—one for your backyard, another for a side area, etc. Most users won't need this, but if you're managing a property with multiple dog areas, it's possible. The app switches between zones automatically based on which base station's signal is strongest, so your dog receives consistent protection across the property.
