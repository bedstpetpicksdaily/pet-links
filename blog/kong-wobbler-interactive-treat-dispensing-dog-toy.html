Frequently Asked Questions

What size dogs is the Kong Wobbler suitable for?

The Kong Wobbler comes in different sizes to accommodate various dog breeds. The standard size works well for medium to large dogs, while smaller versions are available for toy and small breed dogs. Always check the size recommendations before purchasing.

How do I fill the Kong Wobbler?

Simply fill the interior chamber with your dog's regular kibble, treats, or a combination of both. You can adjust the difficulty by controlling how many treats you add—fewer treats make it harder, while more treats make it easier.

Is the Kong Wobbler dishwasher safe?

Most Kong Wobblers are top-rack dishwasher safe, but it's best to check the specific product packaging. Hand washing is also an effective option to extend the toy's lifespan.

Can the Kong Wobbler help with separation anxiety?

Yes, the Kong Wobbler can be a helpful tool for managing separation anxiety by keeping your dog mentally engaged and occupied while you're away. However, it should be used as part of a comprehensive anxiety management strategy.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overfilling the toy: Packing too many treats inside makes the toy harder to use and frustrating rather than rewarding, causing some dogs to give up quickly.
  • Using it as a meal replacement without supervision: While great for interactive feeding, the Wobbler shouldn't replace all structured meals—monitor portion sizes to avoid overfeeding.
  • Leaving it out unsupervised with aggressive chewers: Some dogs may try to destroy the toy itself rather than work for treats, so always supervise and store it safely when not in use.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Start with larger treats: Begin with bigger, easier-to-dispense treats so your dog experiences quick wins and stays motivated to keep playing with the toy.
  • Use it during peak energy times: Deploy the Wobbler when your dog is most playful and energetic—typically morning or late afternoon—for maximum engagement and satisfaction.
  • Rotate treats and kibble: Mix different treat sizes, kibble varieties, and even freeze wet food inside to keep the novelty fresh and prevent your dog from losing interest.
