Frequently Asked Questions

What size Kong Wobbler should I buy for my dog?

Kong makes two sizes: small (for dogs under 25 lbs) and large (for 25+ lbs). The small version uses kibble-sized treats effectively, while the large holds bigger portions but still requires relatively small treat pieces. Measure the opening before buying if your dog prefers larger treats—this is the most common sizing complaint I see.

Can you use wet food or peanut butter in the Kong Wobbler?

Yes, but with caveats. You can definitely use wet food or peanut butter to increase engagement, but the opening is small enough that thicker pastes require serious smashing by your dog's nose and teeth to extract. This actually increases playtime, which is good. The downside: cleanup is messier, and you'll want to rinse it immediately after play to prevent bacterial growth in dried peanut butter residue.

Is the Kong Wobbler safe for dogs that swallow toys?

The rubber is durable and won't shred into pieces, but the wobbler itself is not designed to be swallowed. If your dog is a serious toy swallower who ingests rubber or plastic items, you need a supervision-only toy or something designed specifically for that behavior. For normal chewers, the Kong Wobbler is extremely safe—it's been tested with thousands of dogs and doesn't break apart into choking hazards.

How long does a Kong Wobbler last compared to other treat toys?

Based on owner reviews and my testing, expect 6-12 months of heavy daily use with most dogs, longer if you rotate it with other toys. This dramatically outperforms cheaper plastic puzzle toys (2-3 months) and justifies the higher price. High-powered chewers may wear through the rubber faster, but even then, Kong's construction holds up better than competitors in the same price range.

Will my dog get bored of the Kong Wobbler?

Novelty wears off after 2-3 weeks for most dogs, which is why rotating toys matters. Keep the wobbler in a closet for a week, then reintroduce it—your dog will suddenly find it interesting again. The real value of the Kong Wobbler isn't sustained excitement; it's a reliable tool for managing daily boredom while you're at work, and it excels at that specific job.
