Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ Stainless steel bowls are genuinely easy to clean and won't retain odors or stains like ceramic alternatives—a real practical win for daily use.
  • ✓ The elevated design (typically 5-7 inches high) measurably reduces neck and back strain in dogs, especially beneficial for large breeds, senior dogs, or those with arthritis.
  • ✓ Two-bowl setup means you're not constantly swapping between food and water stations, and the footprint is compact enough for most kitchens without dominating space.
  • ✓ Price point under $50 for a stainless steel elevated system is genuinely competitive—you're not overpaying for the basic function here.
Cons
  • ✗ The plastic stand degrades faster than wood or metal alternatives. After 18+ months of daily use, warping and cracks appear, especially in hot climates or direct sunlight—June heat accelerates this.
  • ✗ Stability issues with medium to large dogs who eat aggressively; the lighter frame can shift or tip if your dog pushes the bowls around during meals, which defeats the ergonomic purpose.

Our Verdict

The Frisco Elevated Double Feeder delivers basic functionality at a price that won't hurt your wallet, and for small breeds or calm eaters, it's genuinely sufficient. The stainless steel bowls and elevation principle work as advertised. However, if you have a large dog, a senior pup with joint issues, or you're planning to keep this feeder for 3+ years, the plastic frame's durability issues make it a false economy. You'll either replace it or watch it deteriorate into an eyesore. At this price point, you're buying a 2-3 year solution, not an investment piece. That's worth knowing upfront.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is the elevation height adjustable?

No. Most Frisco models come at a fixed height (typically 5-7 inches). If your dog is exceptionally tall or short, or if you're looking to adjust as they age, this is a limitation. Brands like PetFusion offer adjustable heights, but at higher price points.

Will this work for multiple dogs?

Only if both dogs are similarly sized and eat at the same time without competing. The double-bowl design assumes peaceful coexistence. If you have a food-aggressive dog or significant size differences, separate feeding stations prevent stress and guarding behaviors—this design won't solve that.

How does the Frisco compare to stainless steel stands from other brands?

Frisco competes on price. Similar feeders from OXO or Healthymart cost $20-30 more but use reinforced metal or wood frames that last longer. If durability matters more than budget, those justify the premium. If you're replacing every 2-3 years anyway, Frisco's cost-per-year is hard to beat.
