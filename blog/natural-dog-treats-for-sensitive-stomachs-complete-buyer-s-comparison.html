Understanding Sensitive Stomach Issues in Dogs

A dog's sensitive stomach can manifest through vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, or general digestive discomfort. Common culprits include artificial preservatives, artificial colors, corn, wheat, soy, and low-quality proteins. When selecting treats, look for limited ingredient formulas with natural, whole-food components that are easier to digest. Quality matters significantly—premium brands invest in ingredient sourcing and testing, ensuring each treat supports digestive wellness rather than triggering adverse reactions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What ingredients should I avoid in dog treats for sensitive stomachs?

Avoid artificial preservatives, artificial colors, corn, wheat, soy, by-products, and low-quality proteins. These ingredients commonly trigger digestive issues. Look for single-source proteins and natural whole-food components instead.

How do I know if my dog has a sensitive stomach?

Signs include frequent vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, excessive gas, or skin irritation. Consult your veterinarian to rule out underlying health conditions and get recommendations for dietary adjustments, including treat selection.

Are PetTreatery treats suitable for all dog breeds?

Yes, PetTreatery offers premium natural pet treats suitable for dogs of all sizes and breeds. However, always check specific product formulations and consult your vet if your dog has known allergies or medical conditions.

Can natural treats help improve my dog's digestion?

High-quality natural treats with digestive-supporting ingredients like pumpkin and probiotics can support digestive health. When you eliminate artificial additives and fillers, many dogs experience improved digestion and reduced stomach upset.

How should I transition my dog to new treats?

Introduce new treats gradually over 7-10 days, mixing small amounts with their regular treats. This allows their digestive system to adjust and helps you identify any potential sensitivities before fully switching.
