Cold-Pressed Dog Food vs. Traditional Kibble: What's the Difference?

Traditional kibble relies on high-temperature extrusion (temperatures exceeding 300°F) to create shelf-stable pieces. While convenient and affordable, this process destroys heat-sensitive vitamins, enzymes, and beneficial nutrients. Cold-pressing, by contrast, applies gentle pressure at low temperatures (under 90°F), preserving the nutritional integrity of whole-food ingredients like meat, vegetables, and probiotics. This means cold-pressed formulas deliver significantly higher nutrient bioavailability—your dog's body can actually use what they're eating.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does cold-pressed dog food stay fresh?

Cold-pressed dog food typically lasts 6-12 months frozen and 2-4 weeks refrigerated. Always check Nextrition Pet's packaging for specific shelf-life guidance and store in airtight containers to maintain freshness and nutrient quality.

Can I mix cold-pressed food with traditional kibble?

Yes, you can gradually transition by mixing cold-pressed food with kibble over 7-10 days. This helps your dog's digestive system adjust to the superior nutrient density. Nextrition Pet recommends following their transition guide for optimal results.

Is cold-pressed dog food suitable for all breeds and ages?

Nextrition Pet offers cold-pressed formulas for different life stages and dietary needs. However, consult your veterinarian before switching, especially if your dog has specific health conditions or dietary restrictions.

Why is cold-pressed dog food more expensive?

Premium ingredients, gentle cold-pressing technology, live probiotics, refrigeration requirements, and superior nutrient retention justify the higher cost. You feed less volume while delivering more nutrition, often providing better long-term value.

How quickly will I see improvements in my dog's health?

Most dogs show visible improvements in coat quality, energy, and digestion within 2-4 weeks. Gut health optimization and immune system strengthening may take 6-8 weeks, depending on your individual dog's baseline health.
