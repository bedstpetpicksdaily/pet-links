What We Looked For in the Best Cat Scratchers

Our evaluation process went beyond marketing claims. We assessed each scratcher based on these critical factors:

Frequently Asked Questions

What type of cat scratcher do cats prefer most?

Most cats strongly prefer sisal rope or sisal fabric scratchers because the texture closely mimics tree bark and satisfies their natural scratching instincts. Corrugated cardboard scratchers are a close second and are especially popular with cats who like to scratch horizontally. The key is offering both orientations — vertical and horizontal — to see which style your individual cat gravitates toward.

Why does my cat ignore the scratcher I bought?

Cats often ignore scratchers that are too wobbly, too short, or placed in the wrong location — they need a scratcher that stays stable and is tall enough for a full body stretch, typically at least 28 inches for vertical posts. Placement matters hugely, so try putting the scratcher right next to the furniture they already scratch. You can also rub catnip on it or dangle a toy nearby to build a positive association.

How do I get my cat to use a scratching post instead of my furniture?

Place the scratching post directly in front of the furniture your cat targets, since cats scratch in areas they already frequent. Use positive reinforcement by rewarding your cat with treats every time they use the post, and temporarily cover the furniture with double-sided tape or a plastic protector to make it less appealing. Gradually move the post a few inches each week toward your preferred location once the habit is established.

How often should you replace a cat scratcher?

Most cat scratchers should be replaced every 6 to 12 months depending on how heavily your cat uses it, since worn-down surfaces become less satisfying to scratch and can fray into ingestible pieces. Corrugated cardboard scratchers typically need replacing more frequently, around every 2 to 3 months, because cats chew through them faster. A good rule of thumb is to replace it when the surface looks completely shredded or your cat starts losing interest in it.
