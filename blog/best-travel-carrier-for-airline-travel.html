What to Look For

  • Airline Compliance: Your carrier must meet IATA (International Air Transport Association) standards and fit within your airline's specific dimensions. Most carriers for in-cabin travel need to measure around 17 x 8 x 8 inches or smaller—always check your airline's requirements before purchasing. Hard-sided or soft-sided carriers that zip completely closed are typically required.
  • Ventilation and Visibility: Your pet needs adequate airflow and should be able to see out during the flight. Look for carriers with mesh panels on multiple sides and adequate ventilation holes that don't compromise structural integrity. A pet who can see their surroundings tends to be calmer during turbulence.
  • Comfortable Interior Space: Your pet should be able to stand, turn around, and lie down in a natural position. A carrier that's too cramped creates anxiety, while one with a comfortable padded bottom and enough headroom reduces stress significantly. For longer flights, this becomes even more important.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What size travel carrier is allowed on airlines?

    Most airlines allow pet carriers measuring 17-19 inches long, 8-10 inches wide, and 7-8 inches tall in the cabin, though dimensions vary by carrier. For checked baggage, larger carriers up to 27x21x13 inches are typically permitted. Always verify your specific airline's pet carrier requirements before purchasing, as some carriers like Southwest and Delta have different dimension limits.

    What makes the best airline approved pet carrier?

    The best airline pet carriers are hard-sided, well-ventilated, have secure latches, and meet IATA (International Air Transport Association) standards. Look for carriers with absorbent flooring, comfortable cushioning, and enough space for your pet to stand and turn around. Soft-sided carriers are lighter and more flexible, making them ideal if you want to fit the carrier under airplane seats.

    Can you bring a soft-sided carrier on a plane?

    Yes, soft-sided carriers are approved on most airlines and often preferred because they're lighter and can squeeze under seats more easily than hard carriers. They must still meet dimension requirements (typically 17x8x7 inches for cabin travel) and have proper ventilation holes. Soft carriers work best for smaller pets and shorter flights where they'll have adequate space.

    How do I know if my carrier fits under the airplane seat?

    Standard airline underseat dimensions are approximately 17 inches long, 7-8 inches tall, and 8-9 inches wide, though these vary by aircraft. Measure your carrier beforehand and compare it to your airline's specific specifications on their website—most provide diagrams showing approved dimensions. When in doubt, contact your airline directly or choose a carrier from their approved list to avoid issues at check-in.
