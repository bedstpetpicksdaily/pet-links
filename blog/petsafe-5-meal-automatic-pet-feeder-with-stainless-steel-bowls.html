Frequently Asked Questions

Will my dog or cat break into unreleased meals?

Depends on your pet's determination level. Small to medium dogs and most cats won't access sealed compartments. Large, food-obsessed dogs or clever cats that figure out mechanical systems might. This feeder assumes your pet respects the device. It's not a secure container—it's a timed dispenser. If your pet has a history of counter surfing or destroying puzzle feeders, expect trouble.

How often do you actually need to clean the stainless steel bowls?

At minimum, 2-3 times per week depending on your pet's eating habits and your climate. Stainless steel doesn't trap bacteria like plastic, but dried food and saliva still accumulate. The real convenience is that these go straight into the dishwasher—no hand-scrubbing. For busy people, that's the actual time-saver, not avoiding cleaning entirely.

Can you set different meal sizes for each of the 5 compartments?

Yes, you can customize portion sizes for each meal—important if your routine changes throughout the day. Morning meal might be larger, afternoon smaller, evening normal. This flexibility is genuinely useful for weight management or pets with variable appetites. However, adjusting requires going through the programming menu each time, so don't expect to change portions on the fly.

Does it work for both cats and dogs?

Works fine for both, though the bowl size matters. The stainless steel bowls are standard pet-feeder dimensions—deep enough for dry kibble, suitable for most wet food portions. Cats generally use it without issue. Very large dogs might find portions limiting if you need to feed 5+ cups daily; this isn't designed for giant breed dogs eating massive quantities.

What happens if the power goes out?

The feeder stops dispensing until power returns. There's no backup battery, and the meals won't trigger manually. If you lose power frequently or live somewhere with unreliable electrical service, this isn't reliable backup feeding. You'll need a manual feeding plan for outages, which defeats part of the automation purpose.
