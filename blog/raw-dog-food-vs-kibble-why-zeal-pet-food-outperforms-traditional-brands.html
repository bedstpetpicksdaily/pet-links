Raw Dog Food vs. Kibble: Understanding the Core Differences

Traditional kibble is processed at extremely high temperatures, a method designed for shelf stability and convenience rather than nutritional optimization. This manufacturing process destroys many heat-sensitive nutrients, enzymes, and probiotics that support canine health. Additionally, kibble requires binders and fillers to maintain its shape, often including grains and by-products that dogs struggle to digest efficiently.

Raw dog food, by contrast, preserves the nutritional integrity of whole ingredients. When you choose raw pet food like Zeal's offerings, you're providing your dog with nutrient-dense meals that closely resemble their ancestral diet. The proteins remain bioavailable, the natural enzymes stay intact, and the grain-free pet nutrition approach eliminates digestive irritants that plague many kibble-fed dogs.

Why Freeze-Dried Pet Food Bridges Raw and Convenience

For pet owners concerned about food safety or storage challenges, Zeal Pet Food's freeze-dried pet food option offers an excellent middle ground. Freeze-drying preserves raw nutrition while eliminating bacterial concerns and simplifying storage. This process removes moisture without applying heat, maintaining enzyme activity and nutrient density far superior to traditional kibble. Zeal's freeze-dried formulations deliver the health benefits of raw feeding with the convenience modern pet parents appreciate.

Can I mix Zeal's freeze-dried food with kibble?

Why is grain-free pet nutrition better?
