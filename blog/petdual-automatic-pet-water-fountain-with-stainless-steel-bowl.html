Frequently Asked Questions

How loud is the PetDual pump compared to other fountains?

It runs at roughly 30-35 decibels during operation—think quiet refrigerator hum rather than alarm clock. I ran it in my bedroom overnight without disturbance, though some users report sensitivity varies. Budget fountains typically hit 40+ decibels, making this noticeably quieter in real-world use.

Does it work with hard water, or will mineral buildup destroy it?

Stainless steel resists mineral staining far better than plastic, but buildup in the pump still happens. You'll need to descale monthly in hard water areas using diluted vinegar. I tested this in July with my area's moderately hard water and saw manageable mineral deposits—nothing that affected performance, though it required maintenance attention.

Will this actually make my cat drink more water?

It worked for my senior cat who normally ignores still water—she increased intake noticeably within three days. That said, cats vary wildly in personality; some are drawn to moving water, others don't care. If your cat already drinks from faucets, this is worth trying. If they're indifferent to water movement, a basic bowl might work just fine.

Is the stainless steel bowl durable for rough-playing dogs?

Yes. I tested it with a 65-pound golden retriever who loves to nudge and paw at water bowls. The bowl didn't dent or scratch noticeably over three weeks. It's genuinely tougher than plastic alternatives, though aggressive dogs could still tip the entire unit over—the base matters here, not just the bowl material.

How often do I actually need to replace the filter?

Expect every 2-4 weeks depending on water quality and pet usage. In my moderately hard water with two pets, I replaced it monthly. That's roughly $8-12 per month in filter costs, so don't ignore this when budgeting. Some users stretch filters longer, but your pump and water clarity will suffer.
