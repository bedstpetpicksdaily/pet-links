Frequently Asked Questions

How does the pet hair detection actually work, and does it make a real difference?

The Deebot X2 Omni uses AI to recognize pet hair patterns on floors and automatically boosts suction by 20-30% when detected. Pet owners report noticeably cleaner results compared to non-AI models, especially on tile. However, this feature doesn't replace regular brushing—it enhances pickup, not replaces it. Expect to clean the brush every 2-3 weeks instead of weekly with budget models.

Is the self-cleaning station worth the extra cost over a regular self-emptying model?

Regular self-emptying models empty into a bin you swap out monthly. The X2 Omni's station auto-cleans the mop pads and empties the dustbin, extending bin capacity to 2+ weeks and mop lifespan by 30-40%. For pet owners managing hair buildup, this cuts maintenance burden significantly. If you vacuum weekly anyway, a $500 cheaper model with basic self-emptying might suffice.

Will this work on carpet, or is it better suited for hard floors?

The Deebot X2 Omni works on both, but pet hair detection performs best on hard floors where strands are more visible to sensors. On thick carpet, it relies on standard suction rather than AI detection. If your home is 80%+ carpet, the pet hair AI advantage shrinks, making a $400-500 alternative more sensible. Mixed flooring (carpet + tile) is where this model shines most.

How long does the battery last, and does it need frequent charging?

The X2 Omni runs 240+ minutes on a full charge—enough to cover 3,000+ square feet in one cycle. Most single-level pet homes finish in 60-90 minutes, so recharging happens every 1-2 days. Pet owners report no issues with runtime unless they have multi-level homes and treat each floor separately, which defeats the point of buying a robot.

How does this compare to other pet-specific robot vacuums like the Roborock S7 MaxV?

Both have pet hair detection and stellar reviews. The Roborock S7 MaxV costs $300-400 less but lacks the auto-mopping station and self-cleaning feature. For cat owners or those with light shedding, the Roborock is better value. Dog owners with long-haired breeds (Labs, Golden Retrievers, German Shepherds) and tile/laminate floors see more ROI from the Deebot's mopping + auto-cleaning combo.
