Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should my dog drink in summer?

General guidelines suggest 0.5 to 1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily, but summer increases this requirement significantly. Active dogs may need double this amount. Fresh water should always be available. If your dog eats moisture-rich food like Zeal's raw formulations, their total fluid intake from food and water combined will support proper hydration.

Is grain-free dog food better for summer?

Grain-free formulations often digest more efficiently and are less inflammatory than grain-based kibble, which can be beneficial during heat stress. However, 'grain-free' isn't automatically superior—quality matters most. Zeal Pet Food's grain-free options combine this benefit with premium natural ingredients and optimal nutrient density, making them excellent summer choices.

Can I feed raw dog food in hot weather?

Yes, absolutely. In fact, raw dog food can be ideal for summer because it's nutrient-dense, moisture-rich, and often more palatable to dogs with reduced appetites. Zeal's raw formulations are specifically designed for optimal nutrition and food safety. Just follow storage guidelines and practice proper food handling hygiene year-round.

Should I reduce my dog's portions in summer?

Many dogs naturally eat less in summer due to heat stress. Rather than forcing large meals, consider smaller portions more frequently. Since Zeal Pet Food is nutrient-concentrated, your dog receives complete nutrition from smaller serving sizes, making this approach practical and effective.

What signs indicate my dog is overheating?

Watch for excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, vomiting, or collapse. Proper nutrition and hydration with premium food like Zeal Pet Food supports your dog's natural cooling mechanisms. However, heat stroke is serious—contact your vet immediately if you notice severe symptoms.
