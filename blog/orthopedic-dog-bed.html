Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between an orthopedic dog bed and a regular dog bed?

An orthopedic dog bed uses high-density memory foam or supportive foam layers designed to contour to your dog's body and relieve joint pressure. Regular dog beds typically use basic polyester fill or low-quality foam that compresses quickly and offers little to no therapeutic support.

Are orthopedic dog beds worth it for younger dogs?

Absolutely! While orthopedic beds are especially beneficial for senior dogs or those with joint conditions, younger dogs also benefit from the added support — particularly large and giant breeds that are genetically predisposed to hip and elbow issues. Starting early can even help prevent long-term joint problems.

How do I clean an orthopedic dog bed?

Most orthopedic dog beds come with removable covers that are machine washable. Simply unzip the cover, toss it in the washing machine on a gentle cycle, and air dry or tumble dry on low. The foam core itself should be spot-cleaned with mild soap and water and allowed to air dry completely before reassembling.

What size orthopedic dog bed should I buy for a large dog?

For large breeds (over 50 lbs), look for beds labeled 'Large' or 'Extra-Large,' typically measuring at least 40x30 inches. A good rule of thumb is to measure your dog from nose to tail while they are stretched out, then add 6 to 12 inches to ensure they have plenty of room to sprawl comfortably.
