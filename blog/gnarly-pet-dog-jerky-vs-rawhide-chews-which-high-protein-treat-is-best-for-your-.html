Understanding Rawhide Chews: The Traditional Choice

Rawhide chews have long been a go-to option for dog owners seeking affordable, long-lasting chews. These treats are made from the inner layer of cow or horse hides, which are cleaned, dried, and often shaped into various forms. While rawhide does provide some protein and can help with dental health through mechanical chewing, there are significant concerns that have caused veterinarians and pet nutritionists to question their safety.

The primary issue with rawhide is digestibility. When dogs chew rawhide, pieces can break off and be swallowed, potentially causing intestinal blockages or choking hazards. Additionally, rawhide manufacturing processes sometimes involve chemical treatments and bleaching agents that raise health concerns. For dogs with sensitive digestion or those prone to swallowing large chunks, rawhide presents genuine risks.
