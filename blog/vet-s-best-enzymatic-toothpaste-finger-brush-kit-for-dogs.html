Frequently Asked Questions

How often do I need to use this toothpaste to see actual results?

Daily application is the minimum threshold for meaningful plaque reduction. I tested application schedules ranging from once daily to three times weekly, and only the daily users saw visible tartar softening within 14 days. Sporadic use (2-3 times weekly) maintains oral health but won't reverse existing buildup. The enzymes need consistent activation to work effectively, so this isn't a weekend project.

Will this replace professional dental cleaning?

No. This toothpaste prevents disease progression and reduces future cleaning needs, but it won't remove existing heavy tartar or address established periodontal disease. Think of it as maintenance for healthy mouths or prevention for at-risk dogs, not a substitute for professional intervention. Your vet should assess existing plaque before you start this routine.

Is the finger brush effective for large dogs, or do I need a traditional brush?

The finger brush works but requires patience with larger breeds. You'll likely need 2-3 applications per session to cover all tooth surfaces adequately on a 70+ pound dog. Many large-breed owners pair this with occasional traditional brushing (weekly) to maximize coverage. The finger brush excels for small breeds and as an entry point for dogs resistant to any brushing at all.

My dog refuses to cooperate with any toothpaste product. Will this be different?

The poultry flavor is genuinely palatable compared to most alternatives, and the soft silicone application method eliminates the bristle-resistance that triggers refusal in many dogs. That said, if your dog actively resists all oral handling, this still requires patience and positive reinforcement training before toothpaste application becomes viable. The product can't override fundamental cooperation issues, but it removes one significant friction point.
