Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Furbo Dog Camera compatible with all dog sizes?

Yes, the Furbo works with dogs of all sizes. However, the treat dispenser is designed for small to medium-sized treats, so very large dogs may need appropriately-sized treats for optimal dispensing.

Do I need a subscription to use the Furbo Dog Camera?

Basic features like live video and treat dispensing are free. Optional Furbo Premium subscriptions unlock cloud recording and extended video history, but they're not required for core functionality.

Can the Furbo Dog Camera work without WiFi?

No, the Furbo requires a stable 2.4GHz WiFi connection for live streaming, notifications, and remote features. It cannot function on cellular data alone.

What app do I need to use the Furbo?

The Furbo app is available for both iOS and Android devices. You can download it from the Apple App Store or Google Play Store to control your device remotely.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Relying solely on the camera for training. While Furbo helps you monitor behavior, it shouldn't replace consistent in-person training or professional help for serious behavioral issues. Use it as a tool to observe patterns, not as a substitute for proper dog training.
  • Overusing the treat dispenser. It's tempting to toss treats constantly, but this can lead to weight gain and makes your dog dependent on the camera for entertainment. Reserve treats for occasional reinforcement, not continuous distraction.
  • Placing the camera in poor lighting or blocked angles. Position Furbo at eye level in a well-lit area with a clear view of your dog's main space. A camera aimed at a wall or placed in dim corners defeats the purpose of having visual monitoring.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Introduce the camera gradually during your presence. Let your dog get comfortable with the device before you leave. Toss treats through it while you're home so your pup associates it with positive experiences, not just your absence.
  • Use two-way audio strategically, not constantly. Hearing your voice can comfort an anxious dog, but talking to them too often can reinforce the anxiety. Save audio interactions for moments when your dog seems distressed or to redirect unwanted behavior.
  • Review daily footage patterns to understand your dog's habits. Most dogs settle into predictable routines once you leave. Watch a few hours of video to understand when your dog naps, eats, or gets restless—this insight helps you time walks, adjust feeding schedules, and identify real problems versus normal boredom.
