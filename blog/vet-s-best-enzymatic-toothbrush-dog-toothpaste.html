How often should I brush my dog's teeth with Vet's Best Enzymatic Toothpaste?

For optimal results, brush your dog's teeth daily or at least 3-4 times per week. Daily brushing provides the best plaque prevention and maintains fresher breath.

Is Vet's Best Enzymatic Toothpaste safe if my dog swallows it?

Yes, this toothpaste is designed to be safe for dogs and is formulated without fluoride, making it safe if small amounts are ingested during brushing.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using toothpaste as a substitute for brushing: Enzymatic formulas work best when combined with mechanical brushing; simply letting your dog swallow the paste won't deliver optimal results.
  • Brushing inconsistently: Most dental benefits come from regular use (ideally daily or at least 4-5 times weekly), so sporadic brushing limits the enzymatic toothpaste's effectiveness.
  • Ignoring professional cleanings: Over-the-counter toothpaste cannot replace professional veterinary cleanings, which are essential for addressing existing tartar and checking for underlying dental disease.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Establish a consistent routine: Brush your dog's teeth at the same time each day—morning or night—so it becomes a natural habit for both you and your pet, leading to better compliance and results.
  • Use a soft-bristled brush or finger brush: Pair this toothpaste with the right tools; a gentle approach helps your dog stay comfortable and allows the enzymes to work along the gum line where plaque accumulates.
  • Give it time to work: Enzymatic formulas require consistent application over several weeks to show visible improvements in tartar reduction; don't expect overnight results, but trust the process with regular use.
