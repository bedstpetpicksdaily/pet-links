Frequently Asked Questions

Is the Oster A5 suitable for thick or double-coated breeds like Golden Retrievers?

Yes, but with caveats. The A5 can handle double coats when the coat is properly brushed and matting-free beforehand. However, it's not a power machine—if you're trying to cut through severely matted hair, this clipper will work harder and slower than professional equipment. For maintenance grooming on healthy coats, it's reliable. For rescue-situation matting removal, you might need something more powerful.

How often do you need to replace blades on the A5?

With proper maintenance (cleaning after each use, occasional sharpening), a single blade lasts 6-12 months for typical home grooming. At $15-25 per replacement blade, your annual blade cost is roughly $25-50. This is substantially cheaper than proprietary blade systems where replacements cost $60+. Professional groomers using the A5 daily will need blade maintenance every 3-4 months.

Does the Oster A5 get hot after extended use?

The motor itself stays cool—that's the magnetic motor's main advantage. However, the blade can get warm to the touch after 30+ minutes of continuous cutting. Most home groomers work in 15-20 minute intervals anyway, so this isn't a practical issue. If you notice excessive heat after 10 minutes, your blade tension is likely too tight or the blade needs cleaning. The motor won't overheat like budget cordless clippers often do.
