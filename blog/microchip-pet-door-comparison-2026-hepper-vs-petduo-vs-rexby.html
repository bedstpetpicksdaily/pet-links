Frequently Asked Questions

Will a microchip pet door work with my pet's existing microchip, or do I need a special one?

Your pet's existing microchip will work as long as the door's reader supports your chip's frequency standard. Most pets have either 125 kHz or 134.2 kHz chips. Before purchasing, verify compatibility with your veterinarian or the microchip manufacturer—some older chips or international standards may not match the door's reader. Rexby offers broader universal compatibility across standards, while Hepper and PetDuo require checking specific frequencies.

What's the actual total cost when you include installation?

Device prices for smart doors range from approximately $100–$250+ depending on brand. However, the Hepper model specifically may require professional installation due to frame spacing requirements, adding $100–$300+ to the total. PetDuo and Rexby are designed for DIY installation, keeping total costs closer to the device price alone. Always budget for potential frame modifications or professional help before purchasing.

How does a $250 Rexby door compare to a $100 budget smart door?

Budget smart doors often lack the biometric reliability and weather-sealing of premium models, leading to false entries, missed scans, or drafts in real-world use. The $250+ mid-market and premium options (Rexby, Hepper, PetDuo) have proven 4.3-star ratings across 500+ reviews, indicating consistent reliability. The price difference reflects weather resistance, dual-door designs, app integration (Rexby), and installation ease—features that reduce frustration and replacement costs over time.

Will these doors keep raccoons and other wildlife out?

Yes, microchip-activated doors are specifically designed to prevent wildlife entry by requiring a registered pet's microchip to trigger opening. Since raccoons and opossums don't carry microchips, the door remains locked against them. However, installation gaps or frame misalignment (a common problem) can defeat this purpose, so careful measurement and proper installation are critical.

What happens if batteries die or the app loses connection?

Smart doors typically have battery backup systems, but exact behavior depends on the model. Most revert to a locked state when power is lost, preventing unwanted entry but also potentially trapping pets outside. Rexby's app connectivity can fail if your router drops or service interrupts, though the microchip reader usually functions independently. Always check the specific model's backup power specifications before purchasing, especially if you have outdoor-only pets.

Do I need this if I only have one pet?

A microchip pet door is genuinely useful for single-pet households if stray animals, neighborhood pets, or wildlife regularly access your home. The $250+ price is harder to justify for convenience alone, but becomes reasonable if you're dealing with actual intrusions. For single pets, lower-cost options may suffice; multi-pet households and those with persistent wildlife problems see clearer ROI.
