Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between raw dog food and freeze-dried pet food?

Raw dog food contains fresh, uncooked ingredients that preserve maximum nutrients and natural enzymes. Freeze-dried pet food undergoes a preservation process that removes moisture while maintaining nutritional integrity, offering a shelf-stable alternative with similar benefits. Both options provide superior nutrition compared to traditional kibble.

Is grain-free pet nutrition better for all pets?

Grain-free formulations work exceptionally well for pets with grain sensitivities, allergies, or digestive issues. However, the best diet depends on individual pet needs. Zeal Pet Food offers both grain-free and grain-inclusive options, allowing you to choose based on your pet's specific health requirements and preferences.

How should I store Zeal Pet Food products?

Raw dog food should be stored in a freezer at 0°F or below. Freeze-dried meals can be stored in a cool, dry place or refrigerated for extended shelf life. Always follow Zeal Pet Food's specific storage guidelines to maintain product freshness and nutritional quality.

Can I transition my pet to Zeal Pet Food immediately?

For best results, introduce any new pet food gradually over 7-10 days, mixing increasing amounts of the new food with their current diet. This allows your pet's digestive system to adjust. Consult with your veterinarian if your pet has specific health conditions or sensitivities.

Is Zeal Pet Food suitable for pets with allergies?

Zeal Pet Food's grain-free, natural formulations are designed for pets with common allergies. Their raw dog food and freeze-dried options contain limited, high-quality ingredients that reduce allergen exposure. However, individual pets have unique needs—consult your vet to identify specific allergies before selecting a formula.
