Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the Boltz actually keep a cat entertained?

Most owners report 15-20 minutes per active play session, and cats will return to it throughout the day if you rotate other toys. Don't expect hour-long entertainment from a single device. The LED attraction brings them back more consistently than non-lit rolling balls, but novelty does fade within 2-3 weeks if it's the only interactive toy available.

Will it survive an aggressive chewer?

The reinforced shell handles normal pouncing and batting without issue, which is why the durability reviews are solid. However, if you have a cat that genuinely destroys toys by chewing through plastic, this isn't indestructible—treat it as a supervised play toy rather than something to leave unsupervised 24/7.

Is this better than a laser pointer or feather wand?

Different tools for different situations. The Boltz excels at solo entertainment when you're not home or busy. Laser pointers and wands are faster for active play sessions and better for cats that want direct interaction with you. Best strategy: use the Boltz as one tool among several, rotating every few days to maintain novelty and engagement.
