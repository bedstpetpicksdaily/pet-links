The Importance of Proper Hydration During Summer

Water is your dog's best friend during summer months. Dogs don't sweat like humans do; they cool themselves primarily through panting, which actually requires adequate hydration to be effective. Ensure your dog has access to fresh, cool water at all times—indoors and outdoors. During walks and outdoor activities, bring a portable water bowl or bottle designed for dogs. Many pet owners underestimate how quickly their dogs can become dehydrated, especially during exercise or training sessions in the heat. Dehydration can lead to serious health complications including heat stroke, so make hydration your top priority.

When training your dog in summer, consider using hydrating training treats rather than dry kibble. Gnarly Pet's natural dog treats and protein-packed jerky are excellent options because they provide nutritional value and energy without being overly dehydrating. These high-quality treats can be part of your training routine while supporting your dog's overall wellness during hot weather.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much water should my dog drink during summer?

Dogs typically need one ounce of water per pound of body weight daily, but this increases during summer and activity. Provide constant access to fresh water and encourage drinking throughout the day. If your dog isn't drinking enough, consult your veterinarian.

What are the signs of heat stroke in dogs?

Signs include excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, vomiting, diarrhea, and collapse. If you notice these symptoms, move your dog to a cool area, provide water, and seek immediate veterinary care. Prevention through proper hydration and cooling gear is always better than treatment.

Are natural dog treats like Gnarly Pet better for summer training?

Yes, natural treats with high protein content and minimal fillers are better for summer training because they provide sustained energy without excessive sodium or artificial ingredients that could contribute to dehydration or digestive issues.

How often should I groom my dog during summer?

Brush your dog regularly to remove excess coat and improve air circulation to the skin. Depending on breed, professional grooming every 4-8 weeks can help with heat management. However, avoid shaving double-coated breeds as their coat provides insulation against heat.

Can I use cooling gear while training my dog?

Yes, cooling gear can be worn during training, especially during warmer parts of the day. Choose lightweight options that allow freedom of movement and don't restrict airflow. Always monitor your dog for signs of overheating even with cooling gear in place.
