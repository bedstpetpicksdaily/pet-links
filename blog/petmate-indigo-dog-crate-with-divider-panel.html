Pros & Cons

Pros
  • ✓ The divider panel is genuinely useful and actually stays in place. Unlike cheap plastic dividers that shift during normal use, this one locks firmly, making it viable for puppies that need graduated space as they grow—or for managing pack dynamics without buying separate crates.
  • ✓ Double-door design saves real sanity. Having access from both the front and side means you can position it in corners, against walls, or in tight spaces without sacrificing functionality. That flexibility matters in real homes where space is premium real estate.
  • ✓ Ventilation design prevents the 'hot box' problem. The perforated metal construction on three sides actually circulates air instead of turning the interior into a sauna during summer heat—a real consideration in July when your dog is already overheated.
  • ✓ Robust handle construction and secure latches. The top handle is reinforced metal, not cheaply riveted plastic, and the latches engage with an audible click. Dogs can't accidentally pop these open mid-transport, which matters if you ever move your crate or take your dog anywhere by car.
Cons
  • ✗ The plastic base pan is thinner than you'd expect at this price point. Some owners report it flexing slightly during cleaning or when larger dogs shift their weight. It's not a dealbreaker, but a heavier-gauge pan would justify the cost better and last through multiple dogs.
  • ✗ Assembly takes longer than advertised. The instructions say 15-20 minutes, but if you're not mechanically inclined, budget 45 minutes minimum. The bolt-and-wing-nut system works fine but isn't the most intuitive for first-time crate buyers.

Our Verdict

The Petmate Indigo with divider is a solid workhorse crate that justifies its cost for anyone needing practical multi-dog management or a puppy-to-adult solution that doesn't require buying twice. The 4.3-star rating from 500+ real owners means it performs consistently, and the engineering details—double doors, actual ventilation, metal handles, secure latches—separate it from the disposable crates you'll replace in two years. If budget is your only constraint, it's worth the investment. If you're comparing it to premium brands like Midwest or Impact, you're trading some steel thickness and aesthetics for a crate that does the job without theatrical marketing. For July adoptions or summer crating situations where heat and ventilation matter, this is a legitimate choice.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size dog does the Petmate Indigo actually fit?

The 'large' model works for dogs 70-90 lbs comfortably, though some larger breeds find it snug. Measure your dog from nose to rear and compare to the interior dimensions before buying—this crate rewards honest sizing. Too much space defeats house-training; too little creates stress. The divider panel helps here by letting you right-size a growing puppy without resizing later.

Does the divider panel hold up over time?

Yes, but only if used as intended. The panel isn't a toy or climbing structure. Owners report the locking mechanism stays firm through years of repositioning, and the plastic doesn't crack under normal adjustment. Don't expect it to withstand a 90-lb dog repeatedly slamming against it, though—it's built for containment, not combat.

How does this compare to wire crates and fabric crates?

Wire crates are more durable long-term but harder to transport and take up visual space in your home. Fabric crates are portable but collapse if a dog panics or has accidents. The Petmate Indigo (plastic/metal hybrid) sits in the middle: lighter than wire, more structured than fabric, and genuinely portable. If you're moving the crate frequently or need it to look less 'kennel-like' in a small space, this design wins. If you need industrial durability for a rescue operation, wire is still king.
