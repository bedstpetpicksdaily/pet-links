Understanding Your Dog's Summer Nutritional Needs

During summer, dogs have different metabolic demands than in cooler seasons. Heat increases their water loss through panting and perspiration, while their energy expenditure may change depending on activity levels. A raw food diet addresses these needs naturally by containing higher moisture content compared to dry kibble. Zeal Pet Food's grain-free raw formulas are specifically designed to provide optimal nutrition without unnecessary fillers that can be harder to digest and generate excess body heat during digestion.

Raw dog food also reduces the thermic effect of digestion—the energy required to break down food. Since processed kibble requires more digestive effort, it generates more internal heat, which is counterproductive during summer months. Freeze-dried and fresh raw options from Zeal Pet Food offer the perfect balance of nutrition and digestibility, ensuring your dog absorbs maximum nutrients while minimizing digestive strain.

Hydration: The Foundation of Keeping Dogs Cool

Proper hydration is non-negotiable during summer. Beyond just drinking water, your dog's diet plays a crucial role in overall hydration. Raw food diets naturally contain higher water content than conventional dry kibble, with fresh raw meals containing approximately 70% moisture. This means that feeding your dog Zeal Pet Food's raw options contributes significantly to their daily fluid intake.

Ensure your dog has constant access to fresh, cool water throughout the day. Consider adding ice cubes to their water bowl or offering frozen water dishes. Additionally, raw food portions can be slightly moistened with water or broth to enhance hydration further. Some pet owners even freeze raw food portions into ice treats—a practice that combines nutrition with cooling benefits. Monitor your dog's water intake and urine color; pale yellow indicates proper hydration, while dark urine suggests your dog needs more fluids.

Best Practices for Summer Raw Feeding

Feeding raw food in summer requires slight adjustments to your routine. Serve meals in smaller portions more frequently rather than one large meal, as this reduces digestive heat generation. Consider offering meals during cooler parts of the day—early morning or evening—to avoid feeding during peak heat hours.

Zeal Pet Food's freeze-dried raw options are ideal for summer because they're lightweight, require no refrigeration during outings, and rehydrate easily with cool water. This makes them perfect for travel, camping, or day trips. Keep raw food properly stored in the freezer until 30 minutes before feeding to maintain freshness and provide a naturally cool meal that helps regulate your dog's body temperature.

Combining Raw Food with Summer Activity

Active dogs during summer need enhanced nutrition to support their lifestyle. Raw diets provide bioavailable protein and essential fatty acids that support muscle recovery and coat health. Zeal Pet Food's premium formulations ensure your active dog gets sustained energy without the crashes associated with high-carb processed foods. The grain-free approach also prevents inflammatory responses that could be exacerbated by summer heat and increased activity.

Avoid heavy feeding sessions immediately before or after intense exercise. Instead, space meals and activities appropriately—feed at least 2-3 hours before vigorous play to prevent digestive issues. Post-exercise, allow your dog to cool down and rehydrate before offering food.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is raw food safe to feed during hot summer months?

Yes, raw food is safe year-round when stored properly. Keep it frozen until 30 minutes before feeding, and don't leave raw meals out for more than 15-20 minutes. The higher moisture content actually benefits dogs in summer heat.

How much water should my dog drink in summer?

Generally, dogs should drink 0.5-1 ounce of water per pound of body weight daily. In summer, this may increase by 25-50% depending on activity level and heat exposure. Raw food diets contribute to this intake through their natural moisture content.

Can I freeze Zeal Pet Food raw meals as summer treats?

Absolutely! Freezing raw food portions creates nutritious cooling treats. You can also mix raw food with water or broth and freeze for a hydrating ice treat that combines nutrition with temperature regulation.

Should I change my dog's diet when transitioning to raw food?

Yes, transition gradually over 7-10 days by mixing increasing amounts of raw food with their current diet. This allows their digestive system to adjust. Start with 25% raw food and increase by 25% every 2-3 days.
