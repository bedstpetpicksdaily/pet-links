  • ✓ Compact design minimizes getting stuck under furniture. The Nano's smaller footprint means it navigates tight spaces where larger models fail, particularly useful for homes with low-profile couches or beds where pet hair accumulates.

This works best as a secondary vacuum (hard floors, entry ways, under furniture) rather than your primary pet hair solution. If you have light-shedding cats or small dogs with short hair, it earns its place. If you have a German Shepherd shedding season happening in July, pair this with a full-sized vacuum. Frequently Asked Questions

Will the Enabot Evo Nano get stuck on pet hair or furniture?

It has basic obstacle detection, but it's not sophisticated. Users report it occasionally gets tangled on long hair strands or stuck under low furniture. The compact size actually helps—it fits into tighter spots than larger models, reducing some stuck situations. Expect to intervene 1-2 times per week in heavy pet hair homes.

How often do you need to clean the brush with pet hair?

Every 2-3 days if you have long-haired pets, every 5-7 days for short-haired animals. This is more frequent than larger robot vacuums but still less effort than manual vacuuming. The brush is easy to access and clean—takes about 2 minutes.

Does it work on carpet for pet hair, or just hard floors?

It works on both, but with caveats. Low-pile carpet and hard floors are its strength. Thick carpet or shag will frustrate you—the suction and brush aren't powerful enough to pull embedded pet hair from deep carpet fibers. If your home is primarily hardwood/tile with some area rugs, this is fine.

Is the $10 price point sustainable, or is there a catch?

The price is legitimate—no subscription fees, no hidden costs. Amazon's pricing on this model is competitive because it's an entry-level product with proven demand (1,176 reviews prove that). You're paying for basic functionality, not premium features. The catch is in expectations, not pricing.
