Best Deshedding Brush for Dogs: Short Hair vs. Long Hair Comparison

⏱7 min read
By Best Pet Picks Daily • July 20, 2026 • Contains affiliate links

Shedding affects all dog owners, but the solution depends entirely on your dog's coat type. Short-haired dogs shed noticeably because loose hairs scatter everywhere—furniture, clothes, floors—while long-haired breeds shed dramatically more volume, especially during shedding seasons when their thick undercoats release massive amounts of fur. The wrong deshedding tool won't just fail to solve the problem; it can irritate your dog's skin or create matting.

The key difference: short-haired dogs need brushes with gentle, precise teeth spacing that reach the undercoat without damaging a thin topcoat, while long-haired dogs require deeper-penetrating designs with larger brush heads for sustained grooming sessions of 15–30 minutes. This guide compares the actual products and features that work for each coat type, so you can invest in a tool that genuinely fits your dog's needs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use one deshedding brush for both short and long-haired dogs?

No. Short-haired and long-haired deshedding tools have fundamentally different tooth spacing and design. Using a long-hair brush on short-haired dogs risks skin irritation, while a short-hair brush won't penetrate the undercoat of long-haired breeds effectively.

How often should I deshed my dog?

Short-haired dogs benefit from 2–3 grooming sessions per week for maintenance. Long-haired dogs require 15–30 minute sessions, ideally 2–3 times weekly during shedding season (spring and fall) and weekly during off-season to prevent matting.

Will deshedding brushes hurt my dog?

Not when used correctly with the right tool for your dog's coat type. Short-haired tools are gentle by design; long-haired tools with rounded teeth or protective guards prevent skin scratching. If your dog flinches or tries to escape, either the brush is too harsh or your technique needs adjustment.

What's the difference between short-hair and long-hair deshedding tools?

Short-hair tools have shorter, densely-packed teeth to gently reach thin undercoats. Long-hair tools feature deeper-penetrating, undercoat-specific designs with larger brush heads to handle thicker fur and sustained grooming sessions without hand fatigue.

Do deshedding brushes actually reduce shedding on furniture and clothes?

Yes, significantly. Regular deshedding removes loose fur before it sheds naturally, reducing the amount that ends up on furniture, clothes, and floors. It also improves coat health and shine by removing dead undercoat hair.
