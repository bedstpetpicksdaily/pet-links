Why Hot Weather is Dangerous for Dogs

Dogs regulate body temperature differently than humans, relying heavily on panting rather than sweating. This makes them particularly vulnerable to heat stress and heat stroke during summer months. Dogs with flat faces, thick coats, or underlying health conditions face even greater risks. Beyond the immediate danger of overheating, summer heat can also reduce your dog's appetite and affect their hydration levels—both critical for maintaining energy and overall health.

This is where thoughtful nutrition becomes crucial. Feeding your dog high-quality, nutrient-dense foods helps support their immune system during stressful heat periods and ensures they're getting adequate hydration from their diet.

Freeze-Dried Treats: The Perfect Cool-Down Solution

Freeze-dried treats are an excellent way to keep your dog engaged and cool without adding excessive heat to their body like traditional kibble might. These treats retain all the nutritional benefits of whole foods while being lightweight and easy to digest. You can even freeze these treats further or serve them cold to create a refreshing snack your dog will love during hot days. Many pet parents also soak freeze-dried treats in water to create a nutrient-rich, hydrating treat that encourages water intake—a win-win for summer nutrition.

Bone Broth: Hydration Meets Nutrition

Bone broth from Smallbatch Pets is packed with collagen, amino acids, and joint-supporting compounds that are especially beneficial during summer months. The high water content makes it an excellent way to increase your dog's overall hydration, which is critical in hot weather. Bone broth also supports gut health and nutrient absorption, meaning your dog gets maximum nutritional value from every meal. During summer, when appetite may dip, the savory appeal of bone broth can encourage eating and help maintain proper nutrition and energy levels.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I freeze bone broth to make frozen treats for my dog?

Yes! Freezing bone broth in ice cube trays creates refreshing treats your dog will love. Smallbatch Pets bone broth is perfect for this—simply pour into molds and freeze for an instant cooling snack.

How often can I give my dog freeze-dried treats in summer?

Freeze-dried treats should comprise no more than 10% of your dog's daily caloric intake. They work best as training rewards or occasional supplements alongside balanced meals from Smallbatch Pets.

Is raw dog food safe to serve in hot weather?

Yes, Smallbatch Pets raw and frozen meals are safe when handled properly. Keep them frozen until serving, thaw in the refrigerator, and serve within 24 hours. The high moisture content actually helps with summer hydration.

What signs indicate my dog is overheating?

Watch for excessive panting, drooling, letharness, vomiting, or difficulty walking. If you notice these signs, move your dog to a cool area immediately and contact your veterinarian. Proper nutrition from Smallbatch Pets helps prevent heat stress.

Can puppies and senior dogs eat Smallbatch Pets freeze-dried treats?

Smallbatch Pets offers formulations suitable for dogs of all life stages. However, consult your veterinarian before introducing new foods, especially for puppies under 12 weeks or seniors with health conditions.
