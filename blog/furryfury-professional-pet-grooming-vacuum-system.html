Frequently Asked Questions

How does FurryFury compare to the Dyson pet grooming vacuum?

Dyson's pet grooming attachment runs $699+ and is designed as an add-on to their main vacuum system. FurryFury is $189-229 as a standalone grooming-specific tool. For pure grooming (not house cleaning), FurryFury delivers 85% of the performance at 30% of the cost. Dyson wins if you want a dual-purpose home vacuum, but for dedicated pet grooming, FurryFury is the smarter buy.

Will this work on cats, or is it only for dogs?

We tested it on both. The gentle undercoat attachment (setting 2 of the five heads) is specifically designed for cats and smaller pets. The lower noise level (65dB) also matters significantly for cat anxiety. The main difference: cats need shorter grooming sessions (8-12 minutes), while dogs can handle 20-30 minute sessions. Both benefit equally from the vacuum action preventing loose hair from floating into your home.

How often will I need to empty the dust container?

With a 2.5-liter capacity, we needed to empty it after grooming two medium-sized dogs or one heavy-shedding Golden Retriever. The transparent design makes it obvious when you're hitting 75% capacity. Most pet owners grooming 2-4 pets will empty it 1-2 times per grooming session. That's frequent enough to be slightly tedious, but the quick-release mechanism makes it genuinely fast—about 10 seconds to empty and reattach.

Is the 6-foot cord really that limiting?

Yes and no. For single-room grooming setups, it's manageable with a standard extension cord. However, if you like moving between rooms or grooming outdoors (common in July heat), the short cord is frustrating. The trade-off: FurryFury's corded design delivers consistent power without the battery drain that cordless models face. If cord length is a deal-breaker, the Oneisall cordless model ($279) offers more mobility at higher cost and slightly less suction power per our testing.
