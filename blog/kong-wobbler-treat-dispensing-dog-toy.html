Frequently Asked Questions

What size dogs is the KONG Wobbler suitable for?

The standard KONG Wobbler works best for small to medium-sized dogs (up to 35 lbs). KONG also offers larger versions for bigger breeds, so check the product specifications for your dog's weight.

How do you adjust the treat difficulty on the KONG Wobbler?

The Wobbler has an adjustable opening on top that controls how easily treats fall out. You can make it easier or harder based on your dog's skill level and engagement needs.

Is the KONG Wobbler safe for aggressive chewers?

Yes, KONG uses durable, non-toxic rubber designed to withstand heavy chewing. However, always supervise playtime and replace the toy if pieces become loose or damaged.

Can you use regular kibble in the KONG Wobbler?

Absolutely! The Wobbler works with both kibble and treats. Using kibble is a great way to turn mealtimes into interactive play sessions while managing calorie intake.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overfilling with treats: Many owners stuff the Wobbler completely full, making it too difficult for dogs to dispense treats and causing frustration instead of engagement. Fill it only three-quarters full for optimal challenge level.
  • Using it as a meal replacement without supervision: While the Wobbler works great for portion control, leaving it unattended for extended periods can lead to dogs getting bored or frustrated, potentially damaging the toy.
  • Choosing treats that are too large or too small: Treats that don't fit the dispensing holes properly either won't come out or will tumble out too easily, defeating the purpose. Measure your treats against the toy's openings before filling.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Introduce it gradually: Start with treats that dispense easily so your dog experiences quick wins and builds confidence. Once your dog understands the mechanism, slowly transition to trickier treat sizes and shapes.
  • Use it strategically during high-energy times: Deploy the Wobbler during peak restlessness—mid-afternoon slumps, before you leave for work, or when guests arrive. This channels natural energy into productive play rather than destructive behavior.
  • Rotate it with other toys: Keep the Wobbler fresh by putting it away for a few days, then bringing it back. This prevents habituation and maintains your dog's excitement and engagement over time.
