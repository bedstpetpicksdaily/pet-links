Does the PetDuo work with all microchip types?

The system is compatible with most major microchip frequencies (125kHz, 128kHz, and 134.2kHz), but not every chip in existence. Before purchasing, contact your veterinarian to confirm your pet's microchip frequency. This is critical—ordering and discovering incompatibility is frustrating and wastes money. Some older microchips or less common brands may not work, which would be a dealbreaker.

How does this compare to cheaper smart pet door alternatives?

Budget alternatives exist around $150-250, but they typically use RFID collar tags instead of microchip recognition. This matters because tags can be lost, stolen, or malfunction, whereas microchips are permanent. If your pet is an escape artist or outdoor adventurer, the tag approach creates risk. The PetDuo's microchip-only approach is more secure but costs more upfront. For pets who stay relatively close to home, cheaper RFID systems are genuinely acceptable.

Will this keep raccoons and other wildlife out?

Yes, but with important context. The door only opens when it recognizes your pet's microchip, so raccoons and opossums cannot trigger it. However, if your regular pet enters and leaves frequently, determined wildlife might sneak through behind them. This isn't a failure of the system—no smart door prevents this entirely. The PetDuo reduces wildlife intrusion significantly compared to standard doors, but it's not a bulletproof barrier if you live in a high-traffic wildlife area.
