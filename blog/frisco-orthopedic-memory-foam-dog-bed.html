Cons

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this actually memory foam or is it just regular foam labeled 'memory'?

Real question. Frisco uses actual memory foam, not the cheap polyurethane garbage that heats up immediately. You'll feel the 2-3 second sink when you press it—that's legitimate memory foam behavior. Budget beds use foam that springs back instantly. The difference matters for arthritic dogs because true memory foam reduces pressure points. That said, this isn't Tempurpedic-grade density; it's entry-level memory foam. For the price, it's genuine.

How long does it actually last before flattening?

Based on the review patterns: 8-12 months for dogs under 70 pounds who rotate sleep spots. 5-8 months for dogs over 80 pounds or dogs with a favorite corner they sleep in constantly. Premium beds ($60+) hold shape for 18-24 months. This bed doesn't compete on longevity—it competes on replacement cost. You can buy 2-3 of these before spending what one fancy bed costs. For senior dogs with limited remaining time, this math makes sense.

Does the cover really stay on, or does it slip around?

The elastic corners are tight enough to stay put through normal use. Washing shrinks it slightly, which actually helps grip. The one caveat: if you have a large dog who drags the bed around by the edges daily, expect some shifting. Most owners don't experience this. The cover is removable and machine-washable at 30°C (cold water), and it doesn't hold odors after washing, which separates this from budget alternatives that retain dog smell permanently.
