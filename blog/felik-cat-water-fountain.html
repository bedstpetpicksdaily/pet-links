Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Neglecting regular cleaning: Even with a filter, these fountains need weekly rinses and monthly deep cleans to prevent bacteria buildup and keep your cat interested in drinking.
  • Ignoring water level: Letting the reservoir run too low forces the pump to work harder and can cause it to run dry, damaging the motor—check it daily, especially in warm months.
  • Using tap water exclusively: Hard water minerals accumulate quickly in ceramic fountains; use filtered or distilled water when possible to extend the fountain's lifespan and reduce maintenance.

Expert Tips for Best Results

  • Position strategically: Place the fountain away from your cat's litter box and food bowls—cats instinctively avoid water sources near their bathroom areas, so placement directly impacts usage.
  • Replace filters on schedule: Don't wait until water looks cloudy; swap carbon filters every 2-4 weeks depending on your water quality to maintain optimal filtration and taste appeal.
  • Keep it running 24/7: Cats are attracted to moving water, so leave the fountain on continuously; the small energy cost is offset by potential health benefits and your cat's increased hydration.
