Frequently Asked Questions

Will this work on my long-haired dog?

Maybe partially. Long-haired breeds like Goldens and Aussies have denser undercoats that require more aggressive deshedding. The Arf Pets tool works, but reviewers with long-haired dogs often mention needing multiple passes and sometimes combining it with a dematting comb. Short to medium-haired dogs get noticeably faster results.

How often do I need to replace this tool?

Budget-conscious owners report 3-6 months of 1-2 weekly grooming sessions before teeth start bending noticeably. Heavy use (large shedding breeds, multiple dogs, or professional grooming) can shorten that to 8-12 weeks. At $10 per replacement, factor annual tool cost into your budget if you groom frequently.

Is this significantly cheaper than premium alternatives?

Yes, legitimately. Premium deshedding tools from brands like FURminator or Chris Christensen run $25-45 and have reinforced metal teeth lasting 2-3 years. The Arf Pets tool costs one-third as much but requires replacement sooner. For casual groomers, the Arf Pets saves money. For weekly intensive grooming, a premium tool's durability might justify the higher upfront cost over 2-3 years.

Does it actually reduce shedding in my home?

Yes, but realistic expectations matter. You're removing loose undercoat before it naturally sheds everywhere, but you're not stopping shedding entirely. Owners report noticeably less fur on couches and clothes after regular sessions. Results are most visible for short-coated breeds; long-haired dogs still shed considerably.

What's the difference between the two sides?

The finer-toothed side grabs loose undercoat effectively and works best on clean, mat-free sections. The coarser side handles mild mats, larger loose hairs, and topcoat. Most users default to the fine side for 80% of the work and switch to the coarse side for problem areas or matted spots.
