Frequently Asked Questions

How long will my dog actually stay occupied with this toy?

Real-world timing: 20-35 minutes on first introduction, dropping to 10-15 minutes after your dog understands the puzzle mechanics (usually by day 5-7). The engagement window is genuine but not infinite. Smart dogs solve puzzles faster, so if you have a border collie or poodle, plan for shorter sessions than owners of more laid-back breeds.

Is this durable enough for heavy chewers?

It depends on your definition of 'heavy chewer.' For dogs who chew aggressively but aren't destructive (most dogs), yes—this toy handles sustained paw contact and moderate tooth pressure. However, if you have a dog that shreds rubber toys or a pit bull-type breed with a serious bite force, this probably isn't heavy-duty enough. It's designed for intelligent engagement, not extreme chew resistance.

Can I put wet food or just kibble in here?

Kibble and hard training treats work best. Wet food creates a cleaning nightmare, and the compartments will trap moisture and smell after a few hours—especially problematic in July heat. Some owners use freeze-dried meat or soft kibble, which works okay, but standard kibble is genuinely the path of least resistance for daily use.

Is it actually better than cheaper puzzle toys?

Yes, but with caveats. Budget puzzle toys ($10-15) often crack within weeks or bore dogs instantly because the puzzle is too easy. Cheerble sits in the mid-tier price range and justifies it through durability and moderate difficulty balance. You're not buying a luxury product, but you're buying something that won't end up in the trash or donation bin by September.
