How does the Arf Pets rake compare to the FURminator or Underworx deshedding tools?

Will this actually reduce the amount my dog sheds?

Yes, but context matters. A deshedding rake removes loose undercoat that's already shedding—it doesn't stop shedding itself. Most owners report removing enough loose hair in one session to noticeably reduce household shedding for 1-2 weeks after grooming. The Arf Pets does this job competently based on its 4.3-star feedback. You'll see visible results in your first use, but you'll need to repeat grooming every 2-4 weeks to maintain those results. It's not a one-time fix.

Is the double-sided design actually useful or just marketing?

It's genuinely useful. The fine-tooth side handles undercoat removal, while the coarser side works on matted sections and top coat. Instead of buying two separate tools, you get both functions here. This is a legitimately smart design choice at this price point and explains why many reviewers specifically praise it. For dogs with mixed coat density, this matters.

What coat types does this work best on?

Reviews suggest best results on medium to thick double coats: Labs, Golden Retrievers, German Shepherds, and Australian Shepherds. Single-coated dogs (Poodles, Boxers) produce less loose undercoat, so the tool's benefit is limited. Short-coated dogs shed less visibly, but you'll still remove loose hair. Very thick, matted coats (unmaintained long coats) may require a more aggressive tool. Check your dog's coat type before buying.
