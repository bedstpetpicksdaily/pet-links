Frequently Asked Questions

How long does the cooling gel actually stay cool?

The gel stays cool for 2-4 hours after your dog first lies down, then gradually returns to room temperature. It's not air conditioning—it's more like a naturally cooler sleeping surface that doesn't absorb and retain heat like standard foam. On humid July nights, that difference keeps dogs from panting through their sleep.

Will this help my dog's arthritis or is that marketing?

Memory foam that molds to pressure points genuinely reduces strain on arthritic joints. You'll likely notice your dog getting up easier in the morning and moving with less stiffness. That said, it's supportive care, not a cure. If your vet hasn't diagnosed arthritis yet, don't assume this bed will fix limping—that needs professional diagnosis first.

Is the removable cover worth the hassle, or should I just buy a cheaper bed and replace it?

Washing the cover costs you 15 minutes and a load of laundry. Replacing a $160 bed costs you $160 and two days waiting for shipping. Over 5 years, washable covers save you hundreds of dollars, especially if you have a dog prone to accidents or live with kids and mud.

How does PetDuo compare to Barkbox or Casper dog beds?

PetDuo prioritizes cooling gel and orthopedic support at a mid-range price. Casper costs more ($300+) but offers premium materials and longer warranties. Barkbox positions itself as budget-friendly but skimps on memory foam thickness. Pick PetDuo if you want solid orthopedic support without premium pricing; pick Casper only if budget is irrelevant.

Will a queen size actually fit in my bedroom, or is it massive?

Queen dog beds run roughly 54 inches long by 36 inches wide—similar to a human twin bed footprint. Measure your floor space first. It's bigger than most 'large' beds but not so enormous that it dominates a typical bedroom. Most owners fit it along a wall or in a corner without reshuffling furniture.
