Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the breed identification really?

Wisdom Panel tests 1,800+ genetic markers across their database of 350+ dog breeds and breed mixes. Real-world accuracy sits above 99% for primary breeds. In our testing with Bailey, the results matched her visual appearance and vet's assessment perfectly. The granular breakdown (showing percentages like 42% golden retriever vs. just 'retriever mix') gives you actionable information for diet, exercise, and health planning.

Will this test work for my purebred dog?

Yes, absolutely. Even if you have papers, the health screening component provides immense value—it identifies genetic predispositions your dog may carry that papers won't show. We recommend it especially for breeders trying to make informed breeding decisions and owners of breeds prone to specific conditions (like hip dysplasia in German Shepherds or heart issues in Cavaliers).

What happens if the results show something scary like a genetic disease?

The report separates results into risk levels: 'At Risk' means your dog likely inherited two copies of a disease gene and will probably develop the condition; 'Carrier' means they have one copy and won't get sick but could pass it on; 'Clear' means no genetic predisposition. Wisdom Panel includes educational resources explaining what each result means practically. We'd strongly recommend reviewing the health report with your vet—they can discuss monitoring protocols or preventative strategies specific to your dog's age and breed.

How does Wisdom Panel compare to Embark?

Both are solid tests with 99%+ accuracy. Embark costs $30-50 less and includes geographic origin data for breeds. Wisdom Panel has a larger health database (200+ conditions vs. Embark's health focus) and slightly faster turnaround (2-3 weeks vs. 3-4 weeks). For pure budget-conscious buyers, Embark wins. For comprehensive health screening, Wisdom Panel edges ahead. Neither is a bad choice—it depends on whether health predisposition data or ancestry geography matters more to you.
