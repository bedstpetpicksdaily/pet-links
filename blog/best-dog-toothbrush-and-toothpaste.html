Best Dog Toothbrush and Toothpaste: Complete Buyer's Guide

Dental disease affects up to 80% of dogs over the age of three, making oral hygiene one of the most overlooked aspects of pet care. Investing in the right dog toothbrush and toothpaste combination can prevent costly dental procedures, eliminate bad breath, and add years to your dog's life.

What We Looked For

After testing dozens of products and consulting with veterinary dentists, we evaluated dog dental care products based on four critical criteria:

  • Bristle Design and Material: We prioritized soft, angled bristles that reach the gum line without causing irritation. Nylon bristles should be rounded at the tips, and we looked for two-tiered designs that clean both tooth surfaces and subgingival areas where plaque accumulates.
  • Toothpaste Formulation: Enzymatic toothpastes with glucose oxidase and lactoperoxidase break down plaque biofilm without requiring rinsing. We avoided fluoride and xylitol (toxic to dogs) and prioritized products with veterinary approval from organizations like the Veterinary Oral Health Council (VOHC).
  • Palatability and Safety: Since dogs won't cooperate with brushing if they hate the taste, we tested flavors that dogs actually enjoy. All products had to be safe if swallowed and free from harmful additives.
  • Ease of Use: We evaluated handle ergonomics, bristle durability after 2+ weeks of daily use, and whether the toothpaste dispenser was intuitive. Products that frustrated owners wouldn't get used consistently.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I brush my dog?

Short-haired dogs benefit from brushing 1-2 times per week. Long-haired and double-coated breeds should be brushed daily during shedding seasons and 3-4 times per week otherwise.

Do self-cleaning slicker brushes work?

Yes. The retractable bristle mechanism makes it easy to remove collected fur with a single button press — no more picking hair out of the bristles by hand.

Can I use a dog brush on cats?

Many slicker brushes are marketed for both dogs and cats. However, cats have more sensitive skin, so choose a brush with softer, more flexible bristles for feline use.

What is the best brush for a shedding dog?

Self-cleaning slicker brushes excel at removing loose fur from the topcoat. For undercoat shedding, combine with an undercoat rake or deshedding tool for best results.
