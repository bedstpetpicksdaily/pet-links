Frequently Asked Questions

Does the voice recording feature actually work, or is it just marketing fluff?

It's functional but limited. The feeder records up to 10 seconds of your voice, and it plays during feeding time. Does your dog care? That depends on your dog's attachment to your voice specifically. Owners with separation anxiety cases report modest improvements in eating behavior—dogs that refused to eat from automatic feeders sometimes engage better hearing a familiar voice. It's not a cure-all, but it's not useless either. The real value is psychological for the owner, knowing they've added that extra touch.

How often do you actually need to refill this, and does the 5-meal capacity justify the price?

At maximum capacity (4 cups per meal), you're storing 20 cups total—roughly 5 days of food for a 50-pound dog eating standard portions. Most owners refill weekly. The 5-meal carousel is genuinely better than 2-3 meal models for people with unpredictable schedules or multi-day work trips. Is it worth paying extra? Yes, if you have a regular dog and irregular hours. If you're home most days, a 3-meal feeder costs $20-30 less and does the job fine.

Will this work with wet food, raw, or prescription kibble?

No to wet food—the mechanism can't process moisture. Raw food won't work either due to spoilage risk and texture inconsistency. Prescription kibble *sometimes* works if the pieces are uniform size, but reports are mixed. Larger breed prescription foods (designed with bigger kibble) occasionally jam the carousel. If your vet prescribed specific food, test a small batch first before committing. Standard dry kibble is what this feeder is built for; anything else is you working against the design.
