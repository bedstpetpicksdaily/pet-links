Part 1

What This Business Actually Is

At its core this is a content affiliate business — a network of seven niche websites that publish product review articles, earn commissions when readers buy products through affiliate links, and display advertising to every visitor regardless of whether they buy anything.

The key innovation is that almost none of it requires manual work. A custom-built automation system runs continuously and handles content creation, publishing, SEO optimization, social distribution, and site maintenance on a loop. No articles are written manually. No websites are updated by hand. No social posts are scheduled by a human. The machine does it.

This is not a side hustle. Built to scale, this is the infrastructure of a seven-figure media company operating at a fraction of the cost of a traditional agency.

Part 3

How Revenue Is Generated

Three revenue streams are active simultaneously. They are independent of each other — if one underperforms, the others carry the business.

Stream 1: Amazon Associates

Every product review contains an Amazon affiliate link. When a visitor clicks and buys anything on Amazon within 24 hours, a commission is earned. Commission rates vary by category — typically 3–8%.

Each site has its own Amazon Associates tracking tag so revenue is measured per site. The content engine is deliberately engineered toward high-ticket products priced $100–$300+. A 5% commission on a $1,300 electric tricycle earns $65. A 5% commission on a $20 dog toy earns $1. The math is obvious.

Product ideas are weighted: 40% at $30–100, 40% at $100–300, 20% at $300+. Higher-priced products earn dramatically more per click.

Stream 2: Awin Affiliate Network

Beyond Amazon, the business is approved on Awin — a professional affiliate network connecting publishers with premium brands. These pay higher commissions than Amazon and include dedicated account managers and exclusive deals.

Stream 3: Google AdSense → Mediavine

Every page on every site has Google AdSense units. AdSense pays for every view and click — even when no purchase is made. This is pure passive income from traffic alone.

The long-term plan is to replace AdSense with Mediavine once traffic hits 50,000 sessions/month. Mediavine pays 3–5x more per visitor than AdSense by serving premium programmatic ads. This single upgrade multiplies advertising revenue several times over with zero additional work.

Part 4

The Content Engine

Content is what drives traffic, and traffic is what makes money. The content engine runs automatically and produces publish-ready articles without human input.

How a Post Is Created

  1. Product idea generation — The AI generates a list of specific, real Amazon products in the niche, weighted toward higher price points, specific brand names, and buyer-intent keywords.
  2. Deduplication — The system checks a list of already-published products and skips anything already covered.
  3. AI writing — Claude AI writes a full SEO-optimized review: title, meta description, intro, pros/cons, verdict, and FAQ entries.
  4. Image sourcing — The Pexels API is queried for a relevant lifestyle photo (free, no storage cost).
  5. HTML assembly — Everything is assembled into a complete, styled HTML file with all SEO markup baked in.
  6. Publication — The file is written and pushed to GitHub. Live within minutes.
  7. Indexing — New URLs are submitted to Bing via IndexNow for immediate indexing.

Part 5

The SEO Architecture

Every post is built for Google from the ground up. SEO is baked into every article at creation and layered in afterward by automated injector scripts.

What Every Post Has

Meta Tags

  • Unique <title> tag — keyword-optimized, under 60 characters
  • Unique meta description — 150–160 characters, written by AI specifically per post
  • Canonical URL tag — prevents duplicate content penalties

Schema Markup (JSON-LD)

  • FAQPage schema — FAQ entries display as expanded results in Google
  • BreadcrumbList schema — Shows path in search results: Home → All Reviews → Post Title
  • Product + AggregateRating schema — Star ratings (⭐4.1–4.9) appear directly in Google search results, dramatically increasing click-through rates

Social Sharing

  • Open Graph + Twitter Card meta tags — every post renders with a proper image preview when shared on Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn
  • Pinterest, Twitter/X, Reddit, Facebook share buttons on every post

E-E-A-T Signals

Google rewards Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust. Built-in signals include:

  • Author bio box — Every post identifies the reviewer by niche expertise
  • About page — Each site explains who runs it and how products are selected
  • Privacy Policy page — Required for AdSense and affiliate compliance
  • Affiliate disclosure — FTC-required disclosure on every post

Internal Linking

  • "You Might Also Like" section on every post — keeps visitors browsing, passes link equity between posts
  • Sticky nav bar on every post — visitors can explore the full site from any article
  • Category index pages at /blog/[category]/ — rank for broader keyword clusters

Technical SEO

  • Descriptive image alt text on every image — Google image search signal
  • Auto-generated sitemap.xml submitted to Google Search Console
  • RSS feeds for all 7 sites — submitted to Google News and Flipboard
  • IndexNow protocol — new URLs submitted to Bing/Yahoo instantly
  • Blog freshener — 3 posts per site refreshed monthly so Google sees an active site

The Injector System

Rather than rebuilding every post from scratch when something changes, injectors are small scripts that scan all posts for a unique HTML marker, skip posts that already have it, inject the new code into posts that don't, and track progress in a JSON log.

This means adding a new feature — like star ratings or affiliate click tracking — deploys across 1,200+ posts over the next several cycles automatically. No manual work.

Part 8

Analytics and Tracking

Complete visibility into how the business is performing — per site, per post, per click.

Google Analytics 4 (Per Site)

Each of the 7 sites has its own GA4 property so data never mixes. What's visible in real time:

  • Daily, weekly, and monthly visitors per site
  • Which posts get the most traffic
  • Where visitors come from — Google, Bing, social, direct
  • Time on page and bounce rate
  • Affiliate clicks — Every click on an affiliate link fires a custom affiliate_click GA4 event with the exact URL, link text, and page it was clicked from

The affiliate click tracking is the most powerful feature. Even before commission reports arrive, GA4 shows exactly which posts are sending people to buy. High click-rate posts are the ones to build more content around.

Google Search Console

All 7 sites are verified. Real-time data from Google itself:

  • Which search queries are bringing visitors
  • Which posts rank for which keywords
  • Average position in search results
  • Click-through rates from search
  • Crawl errors and indexing issues

Microsoft Clarity

Free heatmap tool showing exactly where people click, scroll, and rage-click. Better than GA4 for understanding why people leave a page. Paired with GA4 for complete behavioral data.
